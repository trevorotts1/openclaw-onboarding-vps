# Interdepartmental Communication Guidelines
**Version:** 1.0 | March 16, 2026

## The Model: Direct Communication + Master Orchestrator Awareness

Departments communicate directly with each other. There is no approval bottleneck. When Video needs Audio, Video goes directly to Audio. When Sales needs Graphics, Sales goes directly to Graphics.

However, every cross-department request is CC'd to the master orchestrator so it maintains full awareness of all work in motion across the company.

**This is the CC model, not the approval model.**
- Approval model: every request must go through the orchestrator first (slow, bottleneck)
- CC model: depts talk directly, orchestrator gets copied automatically (fast, aware)

---

## When to Use Direct Communication

Use direct dept-to-dept communication when:
- You need an asset, output, or deliverable from another department
- You are handing off a finished deliverable to another department
- You need a quick clarification from another department
- You are coordinating a multi-department project

---

## When to Route Through Master Orchestrator

Route through the master orchestrator when:
- You do not know which department should handle a task
- A task spans more than 3 departments simultaneously
- There is a conflict between departments about who owns a task
- A department is unresponsive and you need the orchestrator to intervene

---

## Response Time Expectations

| Priority | Response Time |
|----------|--------------|
| ASAP / Urgent | Within 2 hours |
| Standard | Within 24 hours |
| Low priority | Within 48 hours |

If a deadline cannot be met, notify the requesting department immediately. Do not go silent.

| Requesting | Receiving | Common Need |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|
| Sales | Audio | Voice scripts, AI phone agent scripts |
| Sales | Graphics | Sales deck visuals, proposal graphics |
| Sales | Creative | Email copy, follow-up sequences |
| Marketing | Graphics | Ad images, social media visuals |
| Marketing | Video | Video ads, social content |
| Marketing | Audio | Podcast content, audio ads |
| Video | Graphics | Thumbnails, title cards, lower thirds |
| Video | Audio | Voiceovers, background music |

## Activity Logging

Every cross-department request and delivery is logged in the master orchestrator's daily company log automatically. The log entry format is:

```
HH:MM — [FromDept]→[ToDept]: [action] [JobID] ✅/🔄
```

Example:
```
09:14 — video-dept→audio-dept: Voiceover requested [VIDEO-20260316-001] 🔄
11:32 — audio-dept→video-dept: Voiceover delivered [VIDEO-20260316-001] ✅
```

---

## Rules

1. Always use the Cross-Department Request Template (see universal-sops/cross-dept-request-template.md)
2. Always assign a Job ID
3. Always CC the master orchestrator
4. Never go silent on a pending request — communicate delays proactively
5. Never re-route a request without telling the original requesting department
6. The receiving department owns the delivery — not the requesting department
