## Overview

The Goal Setter department owns five Standard Operating Procedures — one for every layer of the goal system. They fire at different cadences (on-demand, weekly, quarterly, annual) but share a common foundation: the DMAIC framework (Define, Measure, Analyze, Improve, Control). Every SOP lives in Section 3 — Analyze — because at its core, goal-setting is diagnostic work: understanding the gap between where {{TOKEN}} is and where {{TOKEN}} wants to be.

Each SOP feeds the next: the Goal Definition Sprint produces goals the Milestone Map breaks down, the Weekly Pulse tracks against those milestones, the Quarterly Reset evaluates the quarter's pulse data, and the Annual Ceremony synthesizes four resets into a year's wisdom.

## DMAIC Alignment

All five SOPs are DMAIC Section 3 — **Analyze.** This is intentional. The Goal Setter's primary value is diagnostic: understanding where {{TOKEN}} is now, where she wants to be, what's in the way, and what the evidence says about the best path forward.

| DMAIC Phase | Goal Setter Application |
|---|---|
| **Define** | What does success look like? (embedded in PA-23-01 Step 3) |
| **Measure** | What's the baseline? What metric moves? (PA-23-01 Step 3, PA-23-03 Step 1) |
| **Analyze** | What's the gap? What's causing it? (ALL FIVE SOPs — this is the core layer) |
| **Improve** | What actions close the gap? (PA-23-03 Step 4 re-focusing, PA-23-04 Step 3 portfolio decisions) |
| **Control** | How do we sustain? How do we detect drift? (PA-23-03 yellow flags, PA-23-02 risk buffers) |
