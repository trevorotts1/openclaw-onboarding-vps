# PA-23-05: Annual Goal Ceremony

**SOP ID:** PA-23-05
**Phase:** DMAIC Section 3 — Analyze

**Token:** {{TOKEN}}

## Purpose

To conduct a reflective, evidence-based annual review that closes the current goal cycle with integrity and opens the next year with clarity. The Annual Goal Ceremony is the Goal Setter department's capstone — bridging retrospective wisdom into forward architecture.

## Prerequisites

- Four completed Quarterly Goal Resets (PA-23-04) from the past year
- Full year of Weekly Goal Pulse entries (PA-23-03)
- Annual business metrics (revenue, profit, customers, team, brand)
- 2-3 hours of protected, unhurried time — this is a ceremony, not a checkbox

## Procedure

### Step 1: Year-in-Review Compilation (45 min)

Go through every quarterly reset and extract standout moments — wins that surprised {{TOKEN}}, failures that taught the hardest lessons, goals retired and why. Organize into three categories:

- **Celebrations:** Goals achieved, breakthroughs, moments {{TOKEN}} exceeded expectations. Be specific: "Closed the Peterson deal ($47K)" hits differently than "grew revenue."
- **Lessons:** What {{TOKEN}} learned that changes how business works going forward. "Referral partners outperform paid ads 3:1" carries real operational weight.
- **Releases:** Goals, beliefs, or patterns {{TOKEN}} is deliberately leaving behind. "We no longer believe we need to be on every platform." "Releasing the 24-hour response time goal in favor of thoughtful replies within one business day."

### Step 2: Theme Extraction (30 min)

Look across all four quarterly resets and the Celebrations/Lessons/Releases. What patterns repeat? Identify 2-3 themes that define {{TOKEN}}'s year. A theme is not a goal — it's the through-line connecting goals. Examples: "The year {{TOKEN}} learned to delegate" or "The year {{TOKEN}} chose depth over breadth."

Document each theme with specific evidence from the quarterly resets. Themes without evidence are just stories; themes with evidence are strategy.

### Step 3: Goal Architecture Blueprint (60 min)

With the year's themes anchored, sketch the architecture for the year ahead. This is NOT goal-setting — that's PA-23-01's job. This step defines the structure goals will live inside:

- **Vision Statement:** One paragraph describing {{TOKEN}}'s desired state 12 months from now. Not metrics — feeling. "{{TOKEN}} wakes up excited because the business runs without constant intervention, revenue has crossed target, and the team genuinely owns their outcomes."
- **Pillars:** 3 strategic pillars any goal next year must align with. Examples: "Operational Independence," "Revenue Durability," "Brand Authority."
- **Non-Negotiables:** 2-3 boundaries protecting {{TOKEN}}'s wellbeing. "No launch months in July or December." "No new initiatives without sunsetting an existing one."
- **Success Definition:** How will {{TOKEN}} know the year was a win, independent of any specific goal? Write it as if reading at next year's ceremony.

### Step 4: Ceremony Close (15 min)

Acknowledge the year fully. Read the Celebrations aloud. Thank the Lessons. Release the Releases with intention. Hand the Goal Architecture Blueprint to PA-23-01 for execution — one cycle ends, the next begins.

## Output

- Year-in-Review document (Celebrations, Lessons, Releases)
- 2-3 Annual Themes with evidence backing
- Goal Architecture Blueprint (Vision, Pillars, Non-Negotiables, Success Definition)
- Archived quarterly reset package for the completed year

## Quality Gate

- [ ] Celebrations are specific, named achievements — not generic categories
- [ ] Every theme is supported by evidence from at least two quarterly resets
- [ ] The Vision Statement passes the "read it aloud" test
- [ ] Non-Negotiables are real boundaries, not aspirations disguised as rules

## Escalation

| Signal | Route To |
|---|---|
| Themes cannot be supported by evidence from quarterly resets | Return to data — themes without evidence are just stories |
| Vision Statement lands flat when read aloud | Revise; Specialist 17 (The Challenger) for honest feedback |
| Non-Negotiables are aspirations disguised as rules | Tighten; each must be a binary boundary |
| {{TOKEN}} cannot name a single celebration | Specialist 28 (Celebration Agent) — pattern of overlooking wins |

## Definition of Done

{{TOKEN}} has a complete Year-in-Review (Celebrations/Lessons/Releases), 2–3 evidence-backed themes, and a Goal Architecture Blueprint handed off to PA-23-01 for execution.

## Tone & Persona Note

Ceremonial steward. This is a rite of passage, not a meeting. Read the Celebrations aloud with weight. Thank the Lessons. Release the Releases with intention. {{TOKEN}} should feel the year — not just review it. Faith-respectful; culturally fluent for Black women professionals.
