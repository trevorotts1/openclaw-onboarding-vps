# PA-23-04: Quarterly Goal Reset

**Department:** Goal Setter (Specialist 23)
**SOP ID:** PA-23-04
**Phase:** DMAIC Section 3 — Analyze
**Version:** 1.0
**Token:** {{TOKEN}}

---

## Purpose

To perform a structured quarterly analysis of goal performance — what worked, what didn't, and what must change for the quarter ahead. This is a performance review of the goal SYSTEM, not of {{TOKEN}}. The Quarterly Goal Reset ensures goals evolve as the business evolves, rather than becoming stale artifacts nobody believes in.

## Prerequisites

- 12+ weeks of Weekly Goal Pulse entries from PA-23-03
- Current milestone map from PA-23-02
- 90 minutes of focused time
- Access to business metrics for the full quarter

## Procedure

### Step 1: Data Compilation (20 min)

Pull every Weekly Pulse entry from the past quarter. Extract the confidence scores and plot them on a trend line — one per goal. Look for: consistent trends (up or down), sudden drops, and goals that hovered at confidence 3 for weeks without improving.

Next, compile the hard numbers. For each goal's primary metric, chart the actual trajectory against the planned trajectory from the milestone map. The gap between the lines IS the story.

### Step 2: Root Cause Analysis (30 min)

For every goal that missed its target by more than 15%, conduct a structured root cause inquiry using the Five Whys method: start with "Goal X missed by Y%," then ask "why?" five times, documenting each answer until you reach an actionable systemic cause. Any chain ending at "we were too busy" or "it just didn't happen" needs a second pass — those are symptoms, not causes.

### Step 3: Goal Portfolio Decision (25 min)

For each active goal, make one of four decisions:
- **Continue:** On track. No changes.
- **Adjust:** Keep the goal, change the timeline, metric, or approach.
- **Pause:** Still important, but other priorities take precedence this quarter.
- **Retire:** No longer relevant to {{TOKEN}}'s priorities. Retiring a goal is wisdom, not failure.

Goals don't earn permanence. Every quarter, every goal must re-justify its existence against {{TOKEN}}'s current reality.

### Step 4: Reset Documentation (15 min)

Write a one-page Quarterly Reset Summary containing: goal portfolio decisions with rationale, top three root cause findings, and one systemic improvement to the goal process itself (e.g., "Yellow flags: reduce average escalation time from 4 days to 24 hours").

## Output

- Quarterly Reset Summary (one page)
- Updated milestone maps for all "Continue" and "Adjust" goals
- Documentation archive of retired goals for future reference

## Quality Gate

- [ ] Every goal has a documented Continue/Adjust/Pause/Retire decision with rationale
- [ ] Every missed target has a completed Five Whys chain reaching actionable root cause
- [ ] The Reset Summary includes at least one systemic process improvement

## Escalation

| Signal | Route To |
|---|---|
| Five Whys chain ends at "we were too busy" or "it just didn't happen" | Second pass required — those are symptoms, not causes |
| Same goal missed by >15% for two consecutive quarters | Specialist 17 (The Challenger) — systemic pattern |
| Goal portfolio shows all goals paused or retired — none continue | Specialist 08 (My Coach) — strategic alignment check |
| Root cause analysis reveals resource gap beyond coaching scope | Operator — structural constraint |

## Definition of Done

{{TOKEN}} has a documented Continue/Adjust/Pause/Retire decision for every goal with rationale, completed Five Whys for every missed target, and at least one systemic process improvement identified.

## Tone & Persona Note

Honest auditor, not prosecutor. The quarterly reset reviews the SYSTEM, not {{TOKEN}}. Retiring a goal is wisdom, not failure — name it as such. "What did the goal system teach us this quarter?" is the framing, not "why didn't you hit this?" Faith-respectful; culturally fluent for Black women professionals.
