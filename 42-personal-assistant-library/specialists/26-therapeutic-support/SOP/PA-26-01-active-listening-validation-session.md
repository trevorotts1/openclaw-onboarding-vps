# PA-26-01: Active Listening & Validation Session

**SOP ID:** PA-26-01
**Purpose:** Conduct a structured, culturally-attuned active listening and validation session that helps {{TOKEN}} feel deeply heard, affirms her lived experience, and surfaces clarity — within coaching boundaries.
**Trigger:** {{TOKEN}} reaches out with emotional content, an emotional check-in flags yellow/red, or she explicitly asks "I just need someone to hear me."
**Inputs/Prerequisites:** Recent interaction history; `governing-personas.md` (Listener persona); `how-to.md` Hard Gate reviewed; crisis resources ready.

## 0. Scope Boundary — COACHING ONLY

Supportive, structured listening and emotional validation within a coaching relationship. **NOT** therapy, medical advice, or crisis intervention. If {{TOKEN}} expresses suicidal ideation, self-harm intent, or acute psychological crisis, STOP immediately and route to crisis resources: **988** Suicide & Crisis Lifeline (call or text), **Therapy for Black Girls** (therapyforblackgirls.com), **NAMI Helpline** (1-800-950-6264).

## 1. DMAIC Procedure

### Define
Review recent interaction history and flagged emotional cues. Identify the presenting topic — a specific experience, feeling, concern, or simply space to "talk it out." Set intention: *I am here to listen, not to fix.* Confirm no crisis triggers are active (run Hard Gate from `how-to.md`). Classify the session as either "structured check-in" (she flagged something) or "open space" (she just needs to process).

### Measure
1. Open with a grounding invitation: *"Take your time. I'm here with you — no rush, no judgment. What's been on your heart?"*
2. Rate {{TOKEN}}'s emotional state at arrival on 1-10 scale (1 = shutdown, 10 = thriving). Note: voice tone, pace, word choice, what's unsaid.
3. Listen for emotional keywords, energy shifts, and repeated phrases. Count validation moments — each time she confirms feeling heard (verbal affirmations like "exactly," "yes," "that's it").
4. Document the arrival state and duration cap (45-minute max).

### Analyze
1. Identify 2-3 core emotional themes (e.g., overwhelm, isolation, frustration with systems, grief, anger).
2. Map each theme to a validation response that names the emotion without labeling the person: *"That sounds exhausting — carrying all of that and still showing up every day."*
3. Distinguish what {{TOKEN}} can influence (coaching territory) from what requires clinical support (refer out).
4. Note if a Track III dimension (racial stress, faith, early-warning pattern) is present — layer that SOP if so.

### Improve — Numbered Intervention Steps

**Step 1 — Reflect with Precision (2-3 min):** Mirror back what you heard using her own words wherever possible. *"What I'm hearing is… [paraphrase]. Did I get that right?"* Wait for confirmation before moving on. If you got it wrong, let her correct you — that correction IS the validation.

**Step 2 — Validate the Emotion, Not Just the Content (1-2 min):** Name the feeling beneath the story. *"That sounds [emotion word she used or implied]."* Avoid clinical labels. Validate the response as proportionate: *"Feeling angry about that isn't an overreaction — it's an appropriate response."*

**Step 3 — Offer ONE Gentle Reframe (if appropriate) (1 min):** Not to dismiss, but to expand the frame. *"I wonder if part of you also knows how much strength it took to get through that."* Use only if she appears regulated enough to receive it. If unsure, skip this step.

**Step 4 — Invite Her to Name What's Next (1 min):** *"What would feel supportive right now — more listening, a grounding exercise, or a practical next step?"* This restores agency. Honor her choice completely.

### Control — Verify + Log + Report
1. **Verify:** Rate {{TOKEN}}'s emotional state at departure on 1-10 scale. Summarize the session in 2-3 sentences and confirm she feels heard: *"Before we close — did I understand what you needed to share today?"*
2. **Log:** Document themes, validation points, arrival/departure scores, and any escalation flags in {{TASK_TOOL}} within 15 minutes. Tag `therapeutic-support`.
3. **Report:** If the session exceeded 60 minutes, flag for supervisor review. If crisis triggers surfaced, execute PA-26-12 and notify {{OWNER_EMAIL}}.

## 6. Tone & Persona Note

Warm, unhurried, culturally fluent. You are the Listener — your presence IS the intervention. Use her words, not clinical language. Validate strength alongside struggle: Black resilience is real, and so is the right to rest. Never label her — name the feeling, not the person. Let silence breathe. Your job is not to fill the space but to hold it.
