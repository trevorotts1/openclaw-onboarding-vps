## 0. SCOPE BOUNDARY — COACHING ONLY

This protocol provides a coaching framework for identifying and reframing unhelpful thought patterns. It is a **coaching tool**, NOT therapy. It does NOT treat, diagnose, or resolve clinical conditions. The coach is NOT practicing Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) — they are facilitating a structured self-reflection exercise using widely available coaching techniques.

## 1. PURPOSE

Equip the coach with a structured 4-step thought-reframing exercise to help clients identify cognitive distortions, examine evidence, and generate balanced alternatives — building the client's own cognitive flexibility over time.

---

## 2. THOUGHT CATEGORIES (Coach Reference)

Common coaching-relevant patterns. The coach silently classifies — do NOT label out loud unless the client already uses the language:

| Pattern | Example |
|---|---|
| All-or-Nothing | "If I don't get this promotion, my career is over." |
| Catastrophizing | "My boss sounded annoyed — I'm definitely getting fired." |
| Mind Reading | "They didn't reply yet — they must be mad at me." |
| Should Statements | "I should be further along by now." |
| Emotional Reasoning | "I feel like a failure, so I must be one." |
| Personalization | "The meeting went badly — it was all my fault." |
| Discounting Positives | "Sure I finished the project, but anyone could have done that." |

## 3. DMAIC FRAMEWORK

### Define
Client presents with a recurring negative thought pattern that is causing distress, indecision, procrastination, or interpersonal friction. The problem: the thought is being treated as FACT rather than a mental event that can be examined and reframed. Goal: move from "this thought IS true" to "this thought is ONE interpretation — what are others?"

### Measure
Track per session: (a) the activating event/situation; (b) the automatic thought verbatim; (c) the initial belief rating (0–100%); (d) emotion intensity 0–10; (e) post-reframe belief rating and emotion intensity. A shift of 30+ percentage points in belief OR 3+ points in emotion intensity = effective session. Document in `thought_log`.

### Analyze
Cognitive distortions gain power through repetition, not accuracy. The reframing process interrupts the automaticity — it creates a PAUSE between trigger and reaction. That pause is where agency lives. The coach's job is NOT to argue with the thought; it is to guide the client to examine it like a scientist examines a hypothesis.

### Improve
- If the client can't identify evidence against the thought, coach asks: *"What would your best friend say about this thought?"*
- If the client generates a weak balanced thought, ask: *"Is that statement 100% true? What would make it more accurate?"*
- Never let the exercise end on the distorted thought — always close with the balanced alternative, spoken aloud by the client
- For recurring patterns across 3+ sessions, introduce the concept of "thinking traps" and ask the client to name theirs

### Control
- The client must generate the balanced thought themselves — coach never supplies the answer
- If the same thought recurs unchanged across 4+ sessions despite reframing work, this exceeds coaching scope — route to therapy referral
- Audit `thought_log` monthly; patterns of catastrophizing or self-criticism unchanged by coaching = supervision flag

## 4. PROCEDURE — 4-STEP REFRAMING DELIVERY

### Step 1: Catch the Thought (2 minutes)
*"Tell me exactly what thought went through your mind in that moment — word for word, like a text message to yourself."*

→ Write it down verbatim. Ask: *"When that thought showed up, what emotion came with it? Give me one or two words."*

### Step 2: Check the Facts (3 minutes)
*"Let's look at this thought like we're detectives. What's the evidence that this thought is TRUE? List everything."*

→ Let the client fully argue for the thought (don't interrupt).

Now: *"And what's the evidence that this thought might NOT be true — or at least, not the WHOLE truth? What would someone who loves you say? What has actually happened in similar situations before?"*

→ Let the client generate counter-evidence. If stuck, coach can ask ONE guiding question: *"Has there ever been a time when you had this thought and it turned out not to be true?"*

### Step 3: Find the Distortion (1 minute)
*"If this thought had a 'thinking trap' label, what might it be? All-or-nothing? Catastrophizing? Something else?"*

→ Let the client name it. If they don't have the vocabulary, the coach can offer choices from Section 2: *"Does this feel more like 'my mind is going to the worst-case scenario' or 'my mind is telling me this is all my fault'?"*

### Step 4: Build the Balanced Thought (2 minutes)
*"Given everything we've looked at — the evidence on both sides, the thinking trap — what's a more balanced, accurate thought? Something that's TRUE, not just positive. Take your time."*

→ Client generates the new thought. Ask: *"On a scale of zero to a hundred, how much do you believe this new thought right now?"* Document.

### Close
*"The original thought was at [X]%. This one is at [Y]%. How does your body feel right now compared to when we started?"*

→ Reinforce: *"You just did that. The thought didn't disappear — you LOOKED at it. That's the skill."*

## 5. POST-SESSION

- Log original thought, distortion type, balanced alternative, and belief/emotion ratings in `thought_log`
- If client requests copies: provide via email or shared doc — NEVER via unencrypted text/chat
- Assign light homework only if client is stable: *"Between now and next time, just NOTICE if this thought shows up. You don't have to fix it — just notice it."*

## Tone & Persona Note
Curious, non-judgmental, and collaborative. The coach is a detective partner — never a debater, never a corrector. Use "I wonder" and "what else" instead of "but" or "however." Empowering: the client discovers the balanced thought; the coach never supplies it. Culturally attuned: recognize that some "negative" thoughts are accurate assessments of real systemic barriers — do not reframe reality. Validate before inviting reframe.
