**SOP ID:** PA-26-03

## 0. Scope Boundary — COACHING ONLY

This inventory identifies and organizes life stressors within a coaching framework. It is **NOT** therapy, **NOT** medical advice, and **NOT** crisis intervention. If {{TOKEN}} expresses suicidal ideation, self-harm intent, or acute psychological crisis, STOP immediately and route to: **988** (call or text), **Therapy for Black Girls** (therapyforblackgirls.com), **NAMI Helpline** (1-800-950-6264).

## 1. DMAIC Procedure

### Define
Open with grounding: *"Let's lay everything out — not to solve it all, just to see it clearly. No wrong answers."* {{TOKEN}} controls what she shares; skip anything she's not ready to name. Set the expectation: this is mapping, not solving. At most 8 stressors will be inventoried; anything beyond that means we pause and continue another day.

### Measure
1. Guide the **4-Bucket Inventory** conversationally:
   - **Body:** *"Sleep, energy, aches, appetite — how's your body feeling?"*
   - **Mind:** *"What's looping in your thoughts? Worries you can't shake?"*
   - **Relationships:** *"How are things with the people closest to you?"*
   - **Environment:** *"Work, home, finances — what's adding pressure?"*
2. For each stressor surfaced, document the specific item and bucket.
3. Reflect back for accuracy: *"So I'm hearing: not sleeping well, worried about your mom, behind at work. That's a lot. Did I miss anything?"*

### Analyze
1. For each stressor, ask two sorting questions:
   - **Sphere of Influence:** *"Do you have some control over this, or is it mostly outside your hands?"*
   - **Urgency:** *"Does this need attention today, this week, or is it background weight?"*
2. Plot into the **Triage Matrix**:

| | Within Influence | Outside Influence |
|---|---|---|
| **Urgent** | Lane 1: Coaching Action | Lane 2: Coping Support |
| **Background** | Lane 3: Scheduled Follow-Up | Lane 4: Acknowledge & Release |

3. Count stressors per lane. If 3+ in Lane 1 or Lane 2, flag for accelerated follow-up.

### Improve — Numbered Intervention Steps

**Step 1 — Lane 1 (Coaching Action, within 48 hours):** Co-create one small, achievable next step for each urgent/influence stressor. Must be specific, time-bound, and within her control. *"What's the smallest possible step you could take on this in the next two days?"* Document the action step with a deadline.

**Step 2 — Lane 2 (Coping Support, same session):** Offer grounding (PA-26-04), breathwork (PA-26-07), or a listening session (PA-26-01). Acknowledge the weight without false reassurance: *"That's a real situation, and right now you can't control it. What would help you get through today?"*

**Step 3 — Lane 3 (Scheduled Follow-Up, within 1 week):** Book a dedicated follow-up session. It's tracked, not forgotten. *"Let's put a pin in this and come back to it next [day]. It's on my radar."* Log the scheduled date.

**Step 4 — Lane 4 (Acknowledge & Release, immediate):** Name it, honor it, let it rest: *"That's real and heavy — and right now, it's not yours to carry alone. Let's set it down for today and revisit if it shifts."* Confirm she feels released, not dismissed. Revisit at next session to ensure no escalation.

**Step 5 — Crisis Check (every inventory):** Before closing, confirm no crisis indicators surfaced during the inventory. If any appeared → exit inventory and execute PA-26-12 immediately.

### Control — Verify + Log + Report
1. **Verify:** Summarize total stressors, lane distribution, and the ONE committed action step. Confirm {{TOKEN}} agrees with the triage: *"Does this feel right? Is there anything I miscategorized?"*
2. **Log:** Document Triage Matrix snapshot (stressors per lane), action step with deadline, and any escalation flags in {{TASK_TOOL}} within 15 minutes. Tag `therapeutic-support`.
3. **Report:** Follow up within one week if 3+ stressors in Lanes 1 or 2. Feed triage results into PA-26-11 trend data.

## 3. Metrics

1. **Action Step Completion:** % of Lane 1 action steps completed by {{TOKEN}} within the committed deadline (target: ≥70%)
2. **Stress Load Reduction:** Change in total active stressor count from inventory to follow-up (target: decrease of 1+ within 2 weeks)
3. **Follow-Up Timeliness:** % of Lane 3 scheduled sessions completed on time (target: ≥90%)

## 5. Definition of Done

The stress inventory is done when all four buckets have been explored, every stressor is classified in the Triage Matrix, at least one Lane 1 action step is committed to with a deadline, and the full matrix is logged in {{TASK_TOOL}}.

---

## 6. Tone & Persona Note

Body-first language honors that stress is felt somatically before it's named. "Outside my influence" stressors may include systemic realities — validate without minimizing: *"That's not a 'you' problem — that's a real weight the world puts on you."* Never rush. Sitting with one bucket the whole session is processing, not resistance. Close with affirmation: *"You did the brave thing today — you looked at it. I've got you."*
