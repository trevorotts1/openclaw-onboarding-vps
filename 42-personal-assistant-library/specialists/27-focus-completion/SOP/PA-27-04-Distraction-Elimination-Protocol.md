## Objective

Systematically eliminate digital, environmental, and internal distraction sources identified in PA-27-03 so focus blocks are genuinely protected and switch cost approaches zero.

## Why This Exists

Rubinstein, Meyer, and Evans (2001) proved even brief interruptions destroy cognitive momentum. A 30-second email check costs 30 seconds plus 15–25 minutes of deep-focus recovery. Distraction elimination is environment design, not willpower -- which is finite and depletes. A notification-free environment costs willpower once during setup and pays out focus every minute thereafter.

## DMAIC

### Define

A distraction is any stimulus that pulls attention away from the ONE task during a protected focus block. Distractions fall into three categories: Self-Interrupt (the operator initiates the break -- boredom, avoidance, curiosity), External-Interrupt (someone or something triggers it -- notification, person, alert), and Environmental (physical space breaks focus -- noise, discomfort, hunger). This protocol's scope covers all three categories through a layered countermeasure installation across digital, physical, and internal discipline domains. The protocol is triggered by a completed PA-27-03 Multitasking Audit that has identified specific triggers and categorized them by type.

### Measure

From the PA-27-03 audit data, capture: total switch events recorded, breakdown by category (Self-Interrupt count, External-Interrupt count, Environmental count), and the top 3 specific triggers within each category. The interruption frequency baseline is the raw switch count from the most recent audit day -- this is the number to reduce. A secondary baseline is the subjectively reported "how often did you feel pulled off task" score (1–10), captured at the end of each focus block in PA-27-01. Together, the objective switch count and the subjective distraction score provide a complete picture of interruption severity before countermeasures are applied.

### Analyze

Map each top-3 trigger to its root cause. "Email checking" maps to no scheduled email time -- the brain reflex-checks because it does not know when inbox access will happen next. "Slack pings" maps to notifications left on -- the environment, not the person, created the interruption. "Hunger mid-block" maps to no pre-block prep per PA-27-01. Triggers then route to categories: notification-based (Slack, email alerts, phone pings) → External-Interrupt; workspace conditions (noise, temperature, hunger, open-plan layout) → Environmental; social (coworker drop-ins, family interruptions, "quick questions") → External-Interrupt; internal (curiosity browsing, perfectionism loops, task-avoidance switching) → Self-Interrupt. The single largest category is the primary countermeasure target.

### Improve

Countermeasures are installed immediately -- do not phase, do not delay. One countermeasure per trigger category, selected from the Countermeasure Matrix below based on the root cause mapping from the Analyze phase. Installation is binary: the countermeasure is either active or it is not. A countermeasure you can override in 2 seconds by habit is not a countermeasure; add friction where needed (app blocker, phone lockbox, accountability partner check-in).

To select and apply countermeasures: start with the single largest switch category from the PA-27-03 audit. If Self-Interrupt dominates, install the Internal Discipline tier first (urge surfing, scratch pad, post-block release). If External-Interrupt dominates, harden the Digital Fortress tier (notifications OFF, email closed, status set). If Environmental leads, redesign the Physical Fortress tier before attempting behavioral changes -- environment always beats willpower. Install all countermeasures for the top category in one session, then lock them in for a minimum of 5 business days before evaluating effectiveness.

### Control

Success threshold: ≥50% reduction in self-reported interruptions from baseline after 2 weeks of full countermeasure installation, verified by a follow-up PA-27-03 audit. Re-audit via PA-27-03 within 5 business days of countermeasure installation, then weekly until switch count stabilizes below the target. If switch count drops below 30% reduction after 2 weeks, the countermeasures were insufficient -- either not fully installed, actively circumvented, or the wrong countermeasure was selected for the trigger. Escalate to Specialist 24 (Superwoman Syndrome) if the inability to unplug reflects a belief that availability equals worth. Escalate to Specialist 09 (Emotional Support & Wellbeing) if Environmental triggers dominate despite full physical fortress, suggesting the workspace itself is a chronic stressor. Notify Master Orchestrator if repeated countermeasure failure persists across 4+ weeks.

## Procedure

### Tier 1  --  Digital Fortress (10 minutes, permanent)
1. Notifications OFF -- not "do not disturb," OFF. Phone: every app off except Favorites calls. Desktop: no banners, badges, or sounds. Slack/Teams: disable all notifications, set status to "Focus Block -- back at [time]." Email: close the application entirely.
2. Browser: one tab, one window during blocks. Tab limiter installed.
3. Phone leaves the room physically. Not face-down. In another room.

### Tier 2  --  Physical Fortress (5 minutes per block)
1. Clear desk of everything not required for the ONE task.
2. Headphones with white/brown noise or instrumental-only music. Lyrics trigger language centers.
3. Door closed. Open-plan: visible "Focus Block" sign.
4. Water and snacks pre-positioned within arm's reach.

### Tier 3  --  Internal Discipline (ongoing)
1. **Urge surfing:** Distraction impulses peak and fade within 90 seconds. Note the urge, do not act on it.
2. **Scratch pad:** When a "don't forget to..." thought arises, write it on paper and return immediately to the task.
3. **Post-block release:** First 5 minutes after every block = designated checking time for all messages.

## Edge Cases

(a) **Environmental trigger is a chronic workspace issue (shared space, employer policy, open-plan office).** The protocol cannot remove the environment, so focus on personal countermeasures: noise-canceling headphones with white/brown noise, a visible "Focus Block -- Please Do Not Disturb" sign, time-shifting deep work to quieter hours (early morning or late afternoon when the space is less active), and communicating focus-block boundaries to colleagues in advance. If the employer policy actively prevents focus (e.g., required notification responsiveness), escalate to Specialist 24 (Superwoman Syndrome) for boundary-setting strategy and to Specialist 09 (Emotional Support & Wellbeing) if the workspace is a chronic stressor.

(b) **External-intrusion is from a person whose role requires availability (on-call, caregiving, direct reports).** The protocol accommodates necessary interrupts while minimizing unnecessary ones. Pre-negotiate: "I am in a focus block until [time]. If it's urgent, call me -- that is the only channel I will check. If it can wait, I will respond at [end time]." For caregiving, schedule blocks during known quiet windows and accept that some blocks will be interrupted -- the protocol is a tool, not a punishment. For direct reports, batch 1:1 time into designated windows so the team knows exactly when availability resumes.

(c) **Self-interrupt is masking a deeper avoidance pattern.** Loop in PA-27-03 Phase 3 (Root Cause) to distinguish distraction from avoidance. Distraction = the task is important but the environment is fighting you. Avoidance = the task triggers anxiety, perfectionism, or uncertainty, and the Self-Interrupt is an escape hatch. If Self-Interrupt dominates at >70% of all switches and environmental/external countermeasures are fully installed, the pattern is likely avoidance. Escalate to Specialist 09 (Emotional Support & Wellbeing) for emotional-blocker assessment if the pattern persists. Continue applying the scratch pad and urge-surfing countermeasures in the meantime -- they buy time for the underlying issue to be addressed.

## Countermeasure Matrix

| Trigger | Category | Countermeasure |
|---|---|---|
| Email reflex | Self-Interrupt | Close email. 3 scheduled check windows/day. |
| Slack/chat pings | External-Interrupt | Notifications off. Focus status set. |
| Phone scrolling | Self-Interrupt | Phone in another room. |
| Coworker drop-ins | External-Interrupt | Closed door + visible focus sign. |
| Mid-block hunger/thirst | Environmental | Pre-position supplies per PA-27-01. |
| "Just quickly" curiosity | Self-Interrupt | Scratch pad + urge surfing. |
| Noise / open office | Environmental | Noise-canceling headphones + white noise. |

## Success Metrics
- Switch count reduced ≥50% from baseline within 4 weeks
- Clean focus block completion rate (PA-27-01) ≥80%
- Subjective distraction score ≥8/10 per block

## Definition of Done
Distraction Elimination Protocol is complete when all Tier 1–3 countermeasures are installed and active, and a follow-up audit via PA-27-03 confirms switch count trending downward.
