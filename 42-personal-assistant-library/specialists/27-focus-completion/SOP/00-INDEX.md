## Research Foundation

All five SOPs are anchored in Rubinstein, J. S., Meyer, D. E., & Evans, J. E. (2001). Executive control of cognitive processes in task switching. *Journal of Experimental Psychology: Human Perception and Performance*, 27(4), 763-797. The central finding  --  that task-switching incurs measurable cognitive "switch costs" of 15-25 minutes per interruption  --  is the empirical spine of this entire department. Additional citations: Zeigarnik (1927) on open loops, Baumeister & Tierney (2011) on willpower as a finite resource.
