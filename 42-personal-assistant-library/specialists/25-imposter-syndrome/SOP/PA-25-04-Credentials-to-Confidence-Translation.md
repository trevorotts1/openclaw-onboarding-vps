## 1. Purpose
Bridge the gap between objective credentials (degrees, certifications, years of experience, awards, title, revenue generated) and subjective confidence — translating "I have it on paper" into "I feel it in my bones."

## 2. Scope Boundary
⚠️ **COACHING ONLY — NOT THERAPY.** This SOP helps clients connect their documented achievements to internal self-worth in professional contexts. It does NOT treat imposter syndrome rooted in childhood trauma, identity-based systemic exclusion requiring clinical processing, or conditions where the client's self-concept is so fractured they cannot acknowledge ANY positive attributes about themselves. If the client consistently rejects all evidence regardless of quality ("Yes, but..."), cannot name a single strength or achievement, or expresses that they "don't deserve" their credentials, warm-refer per PA-25-06. Coaching-grade resistance is "I acknowledge it but don't feel it." Clinical-grade resistance is "It doesn't count / I tricked them / I don't deserve to be here." The latter needs a therapist.

## 3. Procedure (DMAIC)

### Define
- Inventory the client's formal credentials: degrees, certifications, licenses, awards, promotions, publications, board seats, revenue milestones, team sizes managed, years in field.
- For each credential, ask: "When someone introduces you with this credential, what happens in your body?"
- Identify the "disconnect point": which credentials feel hollow or unearned?

### Measure
- Rate each credential on two scales (1–10): Objective Weight (how significant is this credential in the industry?) and Internal Weight (how much do YOU feel it?).
- Calculate the "Translation Gap" = Objective Weight minus Internal Weight for each credential. Sort by largest gap.
- The largest-gap credential is Session 1 priority.

### Analyze
- For the priority credential, trace the story the client tells themselves about it: "I only got this because..." or "Anyone could have..."
- Separate FACT from NARRATIVE: what part of their story is objectively true, and what part is interpretation?
- Identify the specific moment or person that planted the minimizing narrative (e.g., a former boss who dismissed a degree, a colleague who said "that certification is easy").
- Compare the client's self-assessment to an objective industry standard: in their field, what percentage of professionals actually hold this credential?

### Improve
- Build a "Credential Biography" for each high-gap credential: a 150-word narrative written in third person, describing how the credential was earned — the preparation, sacrifice, competition, and achievement. Have the client read it aloud as if it's someone they admire.
- Create a "Credential Visualization": the client physically holds the certificate, diploma, or award while describing the moment they earned it, noting sensory details (where they were, who told them, how they felt before the minimizing story took over).
- Install "Credential Anchoring": a physical gesture (e.g., touching a ring, a lapel pin, a bracelet) paired with a credential statement. Practice until the gesture automatically triggers the statement.
- Link each credential to at least one specific outcome: "Because I have this MBA, I made THIS decision that resulted in THIS revenue growth."

### Control
- Monthly credential review: add new credentials as earned; re-rate Translation Gaps.
- Install "Credential Pre-Flight": before any professional event where imposter feelings might surface, the client reviews their top 3 Credential Biographies.
- Gaps that remain >5 after 6 weeks of protocol → reassess for deeper work or clinical referral.

## 4. Deliverables
- Full Credential Inventory with Objective/Internal Weight ratings and Translation Gaps.
- Credential Biography for top 3 highest-gap credentials.
- Credential Anchoring gesture + statement pair (practiced and embodied).
