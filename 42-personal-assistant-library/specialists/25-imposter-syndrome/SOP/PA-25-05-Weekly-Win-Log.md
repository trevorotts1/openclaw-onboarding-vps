## 1. Purpose
Establish a sustainable weekly practice of capturing, documenting, and internalizing professional wins — small, medium, and large — to build an automatic counter-narrative to the brain's natural negativity bias that fuels imposter syndrome.

## 2. Scope Boundary
⚠️ **COACHING ONLY — NOT THERAPY.** This SOP builds a structured positive-reinforcement habit to counter imposter tendencies. It does NOT treat anhedonia (inability to feel pleasure), clinical depression where the client cannot experience positive emotion from ANY achievement, or conditions where the client is so dissociated they cannot recall their week. Warning signs: the client cannot identify a single positive event across an entire week despite objectively verifiable wins; the client reports feeling "nothing" when describing accomplishments; or the client's inability to register positives is accompanied by sleep/appetite disturbance lasting >2 weeks. These signal possible depression requiring warm-referral per PA-25-06. Coaching works when the client CAN feel wins but FORGETS them. Therapy is needed when the client CANNOT feel wins.

## 3. Procedure (DMAIC)

### Define
- Establish the win log format: digital (shared doc, Notion, or app) or physical (dedicated journal).
- Define "win" in 4 categories: (1) Tiny Wins — a good meeting, a compliment received, a task completed ahead of schedule. (2) Skill Wins — learning something new, improving a process, teaching someone else. (3) Impact Wins — measurable outcomes: revenue influenced, problems solved, people helped. (4) Courage Wins — doing something despite imposter feelings: speaking up, applying for something, saying no.
- Set the weekly cadence: same day and time each week (e.g., Friday at 4 PM). Schedule a recurring 15-minute block.

### Measure
- Week 1 baseline: have the client retroactively list wins from the past week. Count them. Most clients with imposter syndrome will produce 0–3 items. The reality is typically 10–20.
- Track win count per category weekly.
- Track the client's "win difficulty" — how long does it take them to recall the first win? Target: under 30 seconds by week 4.

### Analyze
- Review the weekly log with the client. Look for patterns: which category is hardest to populate? (Most clients struggle hardest with Impact Wins — they discount their own contribution.)
- Identify "win deletion": which objectively positive events did the client experience but NOT log? Why was it dismissed? ("That's just my job," "Anyone could have done that.")
- For each deleted win, apply the "Friend Test": "If your closest colleague accomplished this exact thing, would you tell them it doesn't count?"
- Map each logged win to the competence it demonstrates — not just what happened, but what it PROVES about the client.

### Improve
- Install "Win Capture Triggers": (1) End of each workday — 60-second voice memo listing wins. (2) When receiving a compliment — immediately note it verbatim in a designated place. (3) When the imposter voice says "that was nothing" — that is the EXACT signal to log it.
- Teach the "3-Dimensional Win": for each win, answer: (1) What did I do? (2) What skill did I use? (3) What does this prove about me?
- Build a "Win Review Ritual": at the weekly session, the client reads their top 3 wins aloud with sustained eye contact, no minimizing language, no "just" or "only."
- Transfer verified wins to PA-25-01 Evidence Ledger under the appropriate category.

### Control
- Track weekly win count — target: 10+ wins per week consistently by week 6.
- Monitor win quality: are clients logging progressively more Impact Wins and Courage Wins (not just Tiny Wins)?
- Quarterly "Win Portfolio" review: the client presents their top 10 wins from the quarter as if presenting to a board.
- Recurrence of "0-win weeks" after week 4 → reassess for clinical referral.
