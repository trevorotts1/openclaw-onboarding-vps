## 1. Purpose
Prepare the client to enter high-stakes environments — board meetings, conference panels, investor pitches, media appearances — with grounded presence and authority, neutralizing imposter-driven performance anxiety before the room is entered.

## 2. Scope Boundary
⚠️ **COACHING ONLY — NOT THERAPY.** This SOP addresses situational performance anxiety and self-presentation coaching in professional contexts. It does NOT treat social anxiety disorder, panic disorder, agoraphobia, or any clinical condition. Warning signs requiring immediate warm-referral per PA-25-06: the client experiences panic attacks (racing heart, shortness of breath, feeling of dying) when entering rooms; the client avoids professional situations entirely and it is impairing their career; the client dissociates or "blacks out" during presentations; or physical symptoms (trembling, sweating, nausea) are so severe they prevent the client from speaking. Situational nerves that resolve with preparation and practice are COACHING territory. Disabling physiological responses are THERAPY territory.

## 3. Procedure (DMAIC)

### Define
- Identify the specific upcoming high-stakes event: date, audience, format, duration, the client's role.
- Define the desired outcome: "After this event, I want to feel _____ and the audience should perceive me as _____."
- Name the imposter fear: "What's the worst thing that could happen, and what would that mean about me?"

### Measure
- Pre-event confidence baseline (1–10) for 3 dimensions: Content Mastery, Delivery Poise, Authority Perception.
- List known preparation gaps: what does the client currently NOT feel ready for?
- Quantify prep time available vs. prep time needed.

### Analyze
- Separate content gaps from confidence gaps: does the client lack knowledge, or lack belief in their knowledge?
- Identify the "comparison spiral" trigger: who in the room triggers imposter feelings and why?
- Review the client's actual credentials and preparation against the mental standard they've set (which is usually unrealistically high).
- Map the client's unique perspective advantage: what does THIS client know that NO ONE else in the room knows?

### Improve
- Build a "Room Ownership Kit": (1) Opening Statement — the first 60 seconds scripted and rehearsed. (2) 3 Anchor Facts — irrefutable credentials the client can mentally anchor to if imposter feelings spike. (3) Bridge Phrases — 5 transition phrases for when the client needs a moment ("That's a great question — let me frame this with some context."). (4) Physiological Reset — a 4-7-8 breathing pattern to use in the 2 minutes before entering.
- Conduct a dress rehearsal: the client delivers their opening statement standing, with eye contact, in their intended attire.
- Install a "pre-room ritual": a 3-step sequence (e.g., music track + power pose + anchor fact review) completed in the 10 minutes before entry.
- Debrief tool: a 5-question post-event reflection to capture what went well, what felt shaky, and what surprised them.

### Control
- Track pre-event confidence vs. post-event confidence across 3 events — target upward trend.
- Refine the Room Ownership Kit after each event: keep what worked, swap what didn't.
- Integrate with PA-25-01: add a "Room Ownership" evidence category documenting successful high-stakes appearances.

## 4. Deliverables
- Custom Room Ownership Kit (Opening Statement, Anchor Facts, Bridge Phrases, Physiological Reset).
- Pre-Room Ritual Card (physical or digital, client carries to events).
- Post-Event Debrief Template.

## 5. Integration
- Feeds from PA-25-01 (Evidence Ledger) for Anchor Facts selection.
- Feeds from PA-25-02 (Thought Audit) — ensures imposter thoughts are processed BEFORE entering.
- Complements PA-25-04 (Credentials-to-Confidence) — credential anchoring is part of the Room Ownership Kit.

## 6. Outcome Metrics
- Pre-event confidence scores increase ≥2 points across 3 consecutive events.
- Client reports reduced pre-event anxiety duration (from days/hours to minutes before).
- Client can deliver their opening statement without notes, with sustained eye contact.
