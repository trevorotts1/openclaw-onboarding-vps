# SOP PA-10-05: Travel Debrief & Expense Capture

**Department:** Specialist 10 — Travel & Logistics Coordinator
**SOP ID:** PA-10-05
**Version:** 1.0
**Owner:** {{TOKEN}}

---

## Purpose

To close every trip with a structured debrief that captures lessons learned, reconciles all expenses, updates travel preferences permanently, and ensures nothing is left unresolved — so every trip makes the next one better.

---

## DMAIC Phase: Control

---

## Trigger Conditions

- {{TOKEN}} has returned home from a trip
- 24 hours have passed since arrival (the decompression window)
- End of month requiring expense reconciliation across all trips

---

## Procedure

### Define — The Debrief Commitment

Every trip closes with a debrief. No exceptions. Even a one-day out-and-back. Even a trip that went perfectly. The debrief is not an audit — it is a feedback loop that makes every future trip smoother. Frame it warmly: "Welcome back. Quick debrief when you have 5 minutes — it makes every future trip better."

### Measure — The Debrief Questions

Ask exactly five questions. Do not send them all at once — it feels like a survey. Have a conversation:

1. "What went RIGHT on this trip?" — Start positive. Capture what worked: the hotel that exceeded expectations, the flight time that was perfect, the restaurant that impressed. These become templates for future trips.

2. "What could have been smoother?" — Surface friction points neutrally. "The connection was tight" becomes a standing preference for longer layovers. "The hotel gym was closed" becomes a pre-booking verification step.

3. "Did anything surprise you — good or bad?" — Uncover the unknown unknowns. A surprise conference at the hotel that made check-in chaotic. A delightfully quiet airport lounge. These are not captured by standard checklists.

4. "Any expenses I should know about that were not pre-booked?" — Incidental spend: meals, rideshares, parking, supplies, tips. Get descriptions and amounts. Forward digital receipts. For paper receipts, ask for a photo.

5. "One thing you would change if you took this exact trip again?" — The single highest-leverage improvement. Lock it into the trip template.

### Analyze — Expense Reconciliation

Collect every expense from the trip into a single reconciliation sheet:

| Category | Pre-Booked | Incidental | Total | Receipt? |
|----------|-----------|------------|-------|----------|
| Flights | $X | — | $X | ✓ |
| Hotel | $X | — | $X | ✓ |
| Ground Transport | $X | $X | $X | ✓ |
| Meals | — | $X | $X | ✓ |
| Supplies/Misc | — | $X | $X | ✓ |
| **Trip Total** | **$X** | **$X** | **$X** | — |

Flag any charge that exceeds the expected amount by 20% or more. Flag any missing receipt. Do not file the expense report until every receipt is collected and every anomaly is explained.

### Improve — Update the Preference File

This step is what separates a coordinator who processes trips from one who makes every trip better. Take every lesson from the debrief and write it into {{TOKEN}}'s permanent travel preferences:

- "The connection was too tight in Dallas" → **Standing rule: minimum 90-minute connection time at DFW.**
- "The hotel gym was incredible" → **Saved: Westin brand = preferred.**
- "I need breakfast before a morning meeting, not at 6 AM in the airport" → **Standing rule: hotel with 24-hour room service or early breakfast for morning meetings.**

These updates compound. After 10 trips, the preference file builds itineraries that feel custom because they are.

### Control — Close the Trip File

Finalize the trip file: debrief answers logged, expense reconciliation complete with all receipts, preference file updated, and Trip Master Sheet archived with any notes from disruptions. Mark the trip **CLOSED** in the tracking system. The debrief is not complete until the preference file has been updated — answering the questions without locking in the lessons is a half-done job.

## Output Artifacts

- Completed debrief (5 questions answered)
- Expense reconciliation sheet (all receipts collected)
- Updated {{TOKEN}} travel preference file
- Archived trip file (status: CLOSED)

---

## CTQ (Critical to Quality) — Binary Checks
- [ ] All five debrief questions asked and answered — never skipped, even for one-day trips.
- [ ] Expense reconciliation sheet complete — every pre-booked and incidental expense captured with receipt.
- [ ] Any charge exceeding expected amount by ≥20% flagged and explained.
- [ ] {{OWNER_NAME}}'s permanent travel preference file updated with every lesson from the debrief.
- [ ] Trip file marked CLOSED — not before preference file is updated.

## Metrics
1. **Debrief Completion Rate** — Percentage of trips closed with a completed debrief. Target: 100%.
2. **Receipt Collection Rate** — Percentage of expenses with receipt collected. Target: 100%.
3. **Preference File Growth** — Number of standing preferences added per trip. Target: ≥1 (every trip teaches something).

## Escalation
- If receipt is missing and expense exceeds $100 → partner with {{OWNER_EMAIL}}; do not fabricate or estimate.
- If charge is unrecognized or potentially fraudulent → partner with {{OWNER_EMAIL}} immediately; do not process.
- If debrief reveals a systemic issue (same problem across 3+ trips) → partner with **10 (Travel Logistics) SOP PA-10-01** or **PA-10-02** to implement a permanent process fix.
- If {{OWNER_NAME}} reports travel-related burnout or fatigue → partner with **09 (Emotional Support & Wellbeing) SOP PA-09-01** for a check-in.

## Definition of Done
Trip debrief completed with all five questions answered, expenses reconciled with all receipts collected, travel preference file permanently updated with lessons, and trip file archived as CLOSED.

## Tone & Persona Note
The debrief is where a good travel coordinator becomes a great one. Every trip is research for the next one. Every lesson captured is a mistake that will not repeat. Start with what went right — anchor in the positive before surfacing friction. "Quick debrief when you have 5 minutes — it makes every future trip better." Never make it feel like an audit. The preference file is your compounding advantage: after 10 trips, it builds itineraries that feel custom because they are. Closing the trip file without updating preferences is a half-done job.
