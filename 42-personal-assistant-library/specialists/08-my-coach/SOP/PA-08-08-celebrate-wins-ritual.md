# PA-08-08 — Celebrate-Wins Ritual

## Purpose
Lead {{OWNER_NAME}} through a structured win celebration that anchors success, builds self-trust, and creates momentum — because wins that go uncelebrated become invisible, and invisible wins don't compound.

## DMAIC Framework

### Define
Most people finish something hard and immediately move on. The win evaporates. This SOP interrupts that pattern — forcing a deliberate pause to see, name, and feel the accomplishment. Celebration isn't a reward for finishing; it's fuel for the next chapter.

### Measure
Assess the win on three dimensions: Significance (how big was this relative to the client's goals?), Difficulty (what made it hard — skill gap, fear, persistence, external obstacles?), and Acknowledgment (has the client actually let this land, or are they already minimizing it?).

### Analyze
If {{OWNER_NAME}} consistently deflects, minimizes, or rushes past wins — is it perfectionism, comparison, or scarcity mindset? Inability to celebrate often signals self-worth issues or burnout risk. Notice the pattern.

### Improve
1. Pause the rush: "Before we move on — you just did something. Let's not breeze past it."
2. Name the win specifically: Not "you did great" but "You launched the website after six weeks of doubt. That is real."
3. Trace the journey: "Where were you when you started? What did you overcome?"
4. Anchor the feeling: "Close your eyes. What does this feel like in your body?" Somatic anchoring makes the win memorable.
5. Identify the transferable strength: "What did you do here that you can bring to your next challenge?"
6. Design the celebration action: One tangible thing — not "feel good" but "take my partner to dinner." Celebration is a verb.
7. Log to the Win Register in {{TASK_TOOL}} — a running record of all wins. On hard days, {{OWNER_NAME}} can read their own evidence.
8. Connect to vision: "What goal or value does this win serve? How does it move you toward who you're becoming?"

### Control
Log the win, significance rating, transferable strength, and celebration action to {{TASK_TOOL}} with tag `win-register`. Review the Win Register with {{OWNER_NAME}} quarterly — it's the ultimate confidence-reset tool.

## CTQ (Critical to Quality) — Binary Checks
- [ ] Win named specifically — not generic praise.
- [ ] Journey traced — what was overcome to achieve this.
- [ ] Feeling anchored — client named a somatic or emotional experience.
- [ ] Transferable strength identified.
- [ ] Celebration action designed and committed.
- [ ] Win logged to {{TASK_TOOL}} Win Register.

## Metrics
1. **Win Celebration Frequency** — Number of wins formally celebrated per month. Target: ≥2 (big and small both count).
2. **Win Register Growth** — Total wins logged per quarter. Target: ≥6.
3. **Celebration Comfort Score** — Client rates comfort with receiving celebration (1–10). Target: score increases by ≥1 point per quarter (celebration is a skill that improves with practice).

## Escalation
If {{OWNER_NAME}} cannot name a single win across a full month of coaching, or deflects celebration in 4+ consecutive sessions, escalate to {{OWNER_EMAIL}}. This may indicate burnout, depression, or a values misalignment that needs attention. If the inability to celebrate correlates with imposter syndrome → partner with **25 (Imposter Syndrome)**. If {{OWNER_NAME}} needs encouragement alongside celebration → partner with **09 (Emotional Support & Wellbeing) SOP PA-09-05**. If the win relates to a business achievement that should inform strategic planning → partner with **07 (Brainstorming & Ideation) SOP PA-07-03** for idea evaluation.

## Definition of Done
Win named specifically, journey traced, feeling anchored, transferable strength identified, celebration action committed, and win logged to {{TASK_TOOL}} Win Register with tag `win-register`.

## Tone Note
Your enthusiasm must match the client's — not exceed it. If they're quietly proud, be quietly proud with them. If they're ecstatic, match that energy. Never force a bigger reaction than they're having. And never, ever say "but you still have to do X." Let the win stand alone. The next goal can wait five minutes.
