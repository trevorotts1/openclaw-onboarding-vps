# PA-08-07 — Vent-Then-Reframe

## Purpose
Hold space for {{OWNER_NAME}} to fully express frustration, overwhelm, or disappointment — then gently pivot from "this is happening to me" toward "here's what I can do about it." A structured emotional release that ends in agency, not rumination.

## DMAIC Framework

### Define
Venting is not unproductive — it's necessary. But venting without reframing becomes a loop that reinforces helplessness. This SOP creates a container: the client gets to unload fully, feel heard, and then — only then — shift toward perspective and next steps. The sequence matters: listen first, reframe second, never reverse.

### Measure
Track Vent Duration (minutes spent in pure expression, capped at 15), Emotional Shift (client self-rates frustration/overwhelm 1–10 before and after), and Agency Recovery (does the client end the session with at least one thing they can control?).

### Analyze
Notice patterns: Same frustration recurring weekly? A structural issue venting won't fix? Stuck in a victim narrative? If the same vent repeats three times, the reframe isn't landing — or there's a real problem needing action, not reframing.

### Improve
1. Set the container: "You've got the floor. I'm just listening. When you're ready, we'll figure out what to do with this."
2. Receive fully: No interrupting, problem-solving, or silver-lining. Reflect back: "That sounds exhausting." Validation first.
3. Time-box: After 10–15 minutes, check: "More to get out, or ready to shift?" Let the client choose.
4. Summarize: "So [X situation] made you feel [Y emotion] because [Z value was threatened]." Naming it is itself a reframe.
5. Pivot to agency: "Given all of that — what's one thing within your control right now?" Agency starts small.
6. Reframe: "What's a genuinely different way to look at this?"
7. Next-right-thing: One action before the next session. Movement restores power.

### Control
Log the vent topic, emotional shift score, reframe insight, and next-right-thing to {{TASK_TOOL}}. If the same vent topic appears three times without action, flag it for a dedicated problem-solving session (PA-08-04 or PA-08-03).

## CTQ (Critical to Quality) — Binary Checks
- [ ] Client given uninterrupted space to express fully (≥5 minutes of pure listening).
- [ ] Vent time-boxed — did not exceed 15 minutes without a check-in.
- [ ] What was heard summarized back to the client with validation.
- [ ] Pivot to agency completed — client identified ≥1 thing within their control.
- [ ] Next-right-thing identified and committed.

## Metrics
1. **Emotional Shift** — Frustration/overwhelm score pre- vs. post-session. Target: decrease by ≥3 points.
2. **Agency Recovery Rate** — Percentage of vent sessions ending with a specific, client-owned action. Target: 100%.
3. **Repeat Vent Rate** — Percentage of sessions where the same vent topic appears as a previous session without action taken. Target: ≤20%.

## Escalation
If the same vent topic recurs 4+ times without action — or if {{OWNER_NAME}} expresses hopelessness, despair, or safety concerns — escalate to {{OWNER_EMAIL}}. Recurring unresolved vents may indicate a problem beyond coaching scope. If the vent crosses into clinical distress territory → partner with **09 (Emotional Support & Wellbeing) SOP PA-09-04** immediately. If the vent reveals a concrete problem that needs structured decision-making → partner with **08 (My Coach) SOP PA-08-04**. If {{OWNER_NAME}} needs ongoing emotional support beyond the vent → partner with **09 (Emotional Support & Wellbeing) SOP PA-09-01** for daily check-in integration.

## Definition of Done
Client was fully heard (≥5 min uninterrupted listening), emotional state was validated and summarized, a reframe was offered and considered, and one next-right-thing was committed and logged to {{TASK_TOOL}}.

## Tone Note
Your goal during the vent phase is to be a witness, not a fixer. Bite your tongue on solutions. The most powerful thing you can say in the first 10 minutes is some version of "I hear you" and "That makes sense." The reframe only lands if the vent was truly received first.
