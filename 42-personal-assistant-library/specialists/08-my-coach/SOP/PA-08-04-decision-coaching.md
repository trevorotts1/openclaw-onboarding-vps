# PA-08-04 — Decision Coaching

## Purpose
Coach {{OWNER_NAME}} through a high-stakes decision using structured frameworks that surface hidden assumptions, clarify tradeoffs, and build confidence — without ever making the decision for them.

## DMAIC Framework

### Define
Decision coaching turns "I don't know what to do" into "Here's what I'm choosing and why." The coach provides structure, questions, and perspective — never the answer. The goal is clarity and ownership.

### Measure
Track three dimensions: Decision Clarity (self-rated 1–10), Regret Preparedness (did the client consider what they'd lose with each option?), and Ownership (did the client choose, or did the coach nudge?).

### Analyze
Decision paralysis has a root cause beyond the options. Diagnose: Missing information? Fear of the "wrong" choice? A hidden third option? Avoiding disappointing someone? Uncover the blocker before evaluating options.

### Improve
1. Name the decision: "In one sentence, what are you actually deciding?" Force precision.
2. List every option, including "do nothing" and one creative alternative. Ban "I can't" — reframe as "I'm choosing not to."
3. Build a weighted decision matrix: Options as rows, criteria as columns (values alignment, impact, cost, risk, regret potential). Score each 1–5.
4. Surface hidden assumptions: "What would have to be true for this option to win? Is that true?"
5. Run the Regret Test: "If Option A failed, what would you regret more — trying or not trying?"
6. Close with a choice and next step. If the client can't decide, schedule a follow-up — never force a decision.

### Control
Log the decision, chosen option, rationale, and first action to {{TASK_TOOL}}. Schedule a decision retro check-in 30 days out: "How does the decision feel now?"

## CTQ (Critical to Quality) — Binary Checks
- [ ] Decision stated in one specific sentence.
- [ ] All options listed, including "do nothing" and a creative third option.
- [ ] Decision matrix completed with weighted criteria.
- [ ] Regret Test run for the top two options.
- [ ] Client made the final choice — not the coach.
- [ ] First action and 30-day retro scheduled in {{TASK_TOOL}}.

## Metrics
1. **Decision Clarity Shift** — Pre- vs. post-session clarity score (1–10). Target: increase by ≥4 points.
2. **Decision Follow-Through** — First action completed within 7 days. Target: ≥80%.
3. **30-Day Satisfaction** — Client satisfaction at retro check-in. Target: ≥4/5.

## Escalation
If {{OWNER_NAME}} is facing a decision with significant financial, legal, or health consequences that exceeds coaching scope, pause the session and recommend consulting a qualified professional (financial advisor, attorney, doctor). Flag to {{OWNER_EMAIL}}. If decision paralysis stems from fear or imposter syndrome → partner with **08 (My Coach) SOP PA-08-05** for a confidence reset. If the decision would benefit from structured comparison research → partner with **06 (Research & Answers) SOP PA-06-02**. If {{OWNER_NAME}} needs emotional support through decision stress → partner with **09 (Emotional Support & Wellbeing) SOP PA-09-02**.

## Definition of Done
Decision clarified into one sentence, all options evaluated via weighted matrix, Regret Test completed, client owns the choice with documented rationale, and first action + 30-day retro are scheduled in {{TASK_TOOL}}.

## Tone Note
Resist the urge to solve. Ask the question that makes them pause, then be quiet. The best decision coaching sentence: "What do you already know, that you're hoping I won't confirm?"
