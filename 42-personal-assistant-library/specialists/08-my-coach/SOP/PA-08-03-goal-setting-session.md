# PA-08-03 — Goal-Setting Session

## Purpose
Guide {{OWNER_NAME}} through a structured goal-setting conversation that turns a broad ambition into a specific, measurable goal with milestones, weekly actions, and built-in accountability — so vision becomes execution, not just inspiration.

## DMAIC Framework

### Define
A goal-setting session takes a desire ("I want to grow my business," "I want to get healthier") and converts it into a Goal Statement with success criteria, a timeline, and a first action. The output is not a dream — it's a plan.

### Measure
Rate each goal on the SMART-CS scale: Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound, Client-Owned, and Scored (success criteria defined). Score 1–7. A goal scoring below 5 needs more definition before the session ends.

### Analyze
Goals fail for predictable reasons. Before building the plan, diagnose potential failure points: Is this goal {{OWNER_NAME}}'s or someone else's? Is it too big for the timeline? Are the success criteria vague ("do better" = unmeasurable)? Is there a hidden conflict with another goal or commitment? Surface these now — not after the client has already stalled.

### Improve
1. Start with vision: "Paint me a picture. Twelve months from now, what's different because you achieved this?"
2. Distill the vision into a one-sentence Goal Statement: "By [date], I will [outcome], measured by [success criteria]."
3. Back-cast three milestones: At 3 months, 6 months, and 9 months — what has to be true for the goal to be on track?
4. Build the first-week action: One specific, doable thing the client will complete before the next session. Small enough to be impossible to fail.
5. Identify the first obstacle: "What's most likely to get in your way this week?" Pre-solve it together.
6. Install the accountability rhythm: How will progress be tracked? Weekly check-in? {{TASK_TOOL}} task? Agree on the mechanism and cadence.
7. Close with commitment: "On a scale of 1–10, how committed are you to this first action?" If below 8, the action is wrong — adjust it until commitment rises.

### Control
Log the Goal Statement, three milestones, first-week action, and accountability mechanism to {{TASK_TOOL}} with tag `goal-active`. Schedule the first progress review (PA-08-06) for next week. Set a milestone check reminder at 30/60/90 days.

## CTQ (Critical to Quality) — Binary Checks
- [ ] Goal Statement is written, specific, and includes measurable success criteria.
- [ ] Three milestones (3/6/9 month) are defined.
- [ ] First-week action is specific and small enough to guarantee completion.
- [ ] Primary obstacle identified with pre-solved strategy.
- [ ] Accountability mechanism agreed upon and scheduled.
- [ ] Commitment score is ≥8/10.

## Metrics
1. **Goal Clarity Score** — Client rates goal clarity pre- and post-session (1–10). Target: increase by ≥3 points.
2. **First-Week Action Completion** — Percentage of first actions completed by next session. Target: ≥90%.
3. **90-Day Milestone Attainment** — Percentage of 90-day milestones achieved on or near schedule. Target: ≥70%.

## Escalation
If {{OWNER_NAME}} sets the same goal across three goal-setting sessions without making measurable progress, or commitment scores stay below 7 despite action adjustment, escalate to {{OWNER_EMAIL}} with the pattern summary. If the goal stagnation correlates with emotional blocks (fear, imposter syndrome) → partner with **08 (My Coach) SOP PA-08-05** for a confidence reset. If the goal involves significant financial, legal, or health implications beyond coaching scope → partner with **08 (My Coach) SOP PA-08-04** and flag for professional consultation. If the goal requires travel or event logistics → partner with **10 (Travel Logistics)**.

## Definition of Done
Goal Statement is written with SMART-CS criteria, three milestones are mapped, first-week action is committed with ≥8/10 confidence, and everything is logged to {{TASK_TOOL}} with accountability rhythm scheduled.

## Tone Note
Goal-setting should feel expansive, not constricting. "Let's dream big for a minute, then get practical." If the client tenses up or gets perfectionistic, slow down. A messy but committed goal beats a perfect but paralyzed one every time.
