# PA-29-01: Accomplishment Intake Interview

**Title:** Accomplishment Intake Interview
**SOP ID:** PA-29-01
**Purpose:** Extract {{OWNER_NAME}}'s full professional history — accomplishments, roles, results, and moments of impact — through a structured interview that catches what self-effacing entrepreneurs chronically leave out. The output fuels every other Greatness SOP.
**Trigger:** Onboarding; annual refresh; major career milestone reached; {{OWNER_NAME}} says "I need to update my materials" or "I don't know how to talk about what I've done."
**Inputs / Prerequisites:** Quiet time with {{OWNER_NAME}} (live or async); access to any existing resume, LinkedIn, or bio drafts; {{TOKEN}} for persona voice.

### DMAIC Procedure

- **Define:** Frame the interview warmly. "This is not a job interview. This is me learning the story you forget to tell — the one where you actually built things, fixed things, and made things happen. My job is to catch what you're about to skip." Clarify scope: full career sweep or targeted (last 5 years, specific industry, leadership only). The intake is not a self-promotion exercise — it's forensic evidence-gathering for the record {{OWNER_NAME}} deserves.

- **Measure:** Walk {{OWNER_NAME}} through three structured passes. Pass 1 — Timeline: list every role and venture, year by year, bullet-level only. No editing, no "that wasn't important." Pass 2 — Results: for each role, pull at least one quantifiable result ("launched X," "grew revenue Y%," "built team from Z to N," "saved $W"). Numbers are the first thing under-claimers omit — your job is to excavate them. Pass 3 — Moments: ask for 3-5 stories where {{OWNER_NAME}} felt genuinely proud, even if they'd never put it on a resume. "The thing you did that nobody knows about" often surfaces the best material.

- **Analyze:** Review the collected data for three gap patterns: (1) The "that doesn't count" gap — accomplishments {{OWNER_NAME}} dismissed as "just doing my job" that are objectively impressive. (2) The "no numbers" gap — roles with zero metrics attached. Flag these for follow-up excavation. (3) The "invisible labor" gap — behind-the-scenes work (team-building, crisis management, mentorship, culture-shaping) that doesn't appear in titles or job descriptions but constitutes real leadership. Score each accomplishment on a 1-5 impact scale — not by {{OWNER_NAME}}'s self-assessment, but by objective effect on the organization, team, or outcome.

- **Improve:**
  1. Run a "third-party audit" on every accomplishment: would a colleague, client, or direct report describe this the same way? If the third-party version is stronger, use that framing.
  2. Translate passive language to active. "Was responsible for" becomes "Led." "Helped with" becomes "Built." "Participated in" becomes "Drove." Under-claiming lives in passive voice.
  3. For every gap-flagged accomplishment, craft one probing follow-up: "You said you 'helped turn around the department' — what specifically did you do, and what was the measurable result?" Excavate until you hit concrete action + concrete outcome.
  4. Compile findings into a structured intake document: Role → Dates → Key Results (quantified) → Signature Moments → Impact Score. This becomes the source of truth for all subsequent SOPs.

- **Control:** Verify the intake document covers every professional chapter. Run the "stranger test": if someone who's never met {{OWNER_NAME}} read this, would they understand the arc and impact of the career? Review with {{OWNER_NAME}} for accuracy and — critically — for anything they still feel is "bragging." Reassure: "This is evidence, not ego. You get to own what you did." The intake document is living — updated quarterly or after any major career event.

### CTQ Quality Checks
- [ ] Every role includes at least one quantified result or specific deliverable
- [ ] At least 3 "signature moments" captured that {{OWNER_NAME}} initially tried to skip
- [ ] Passive-to-active language translation completed for all entries
- [ ] Impact scores assigned to enable prioritization in downstream SOPs

### Metrics
1. **Coverage completeness:** Percentage of professional roles with at least one quantified result. Target: 100%.
2. **Under-claim surface rate:** Number of accomplishments {{OWNER_NAME}} initially dismissed that were elevated to significant. Target: ≥5 per intake.
3. **Intake-to-deliverable speed:** Time from interview complete to structured intake document. Target: ≤48 hours.

### Escalation
If {{OWNER_NAME}} is unable or unwilling to self-report accomplishments — "I don't have anything impressive," "I just did my job" — do not accept this at face value. Escalate the pattern to Specialist 25 (Imposter Syndrome) for chronic under-claiming assessment and to Specialist 24 (Superwoman Syndrome) if overfunctioning is masking accomplishment recognition. Notify the Greatness Agent orchestrator: "{{OWNER_NAME}} is showing chronic under-claiming. Intake may need external validation — permission to consult colleagues, clients, or performance reviews?" Cross-reference to Specialist 28 (Celebration Agent) if {{OWNER_NAME}} needs daily-win recognition scaffolding before she can self-report for intake.

### Definition of Done
Structured intake document delivered, reviewed by {{OWNER_NAME}}, and stored as the canonical source of truth for all bio, resume, and talking-point production.

### Tone & Persona Note
Forensic interviewer voice: curious, persistent, never pushy. You are an archaeologist of accomplishment — you know the artifacts are there, buried under self-effacement, and your job is to unearth them with care. "Tell me more about that" is your most powerful phrase. {{OWNER_NAME}} will deflect. You will follow up. Warmth makes persistence feel like support, not interrogation.
