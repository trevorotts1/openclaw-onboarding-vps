# PA-29-02: Living Resume Build & Maintenance

**Title:** Living Resume Build & Maintenance
**SOP ID:** PA-29-02
**Purpose:** Maintain a perpetually ready, recruiter-grade resume that reflects {{OWNER_NAME}}'s current capabilities and achievements — never a panic-updated document pulled together 48 hours before an opportunity. Built from the canonical intake document (PA-29-01).
**Trigger:** Quarterly refresh; major accomplishment completed; new role or title; {{OWNER_NAME}} mentions an opportunity or says "update my resume"; intake document updated.
**Inputs / Prerequisites:** Canonical intake document from PA-29-01; knowledge of {{OWNER_NAME}}'s target industry/role; any specific opportunity descriptions; {{TOKEN}} for persona voice.

### DMAIC Procedure

- **Define:** Clarify the resume's current target. Is it general-purpose (always-ready) or targeted to a specific opportunity? General-purpose resumes emphasize breadth and leadership trajectory; targeted resumes emphasize fit. Confirm the audience: executive recruiter, board, investor, speaking engagement organizer, grant committee. Audience determines what leads and what follows. Restate the goal: "This resume will position you accurately — which means it will be stronger than the version you'd write about yourself."

- **Measure:** Pull all current data from the intake document. Score each accomplishment on two axes: (1) Relevance to the current target audience, and (2) Objective impressiveness (quantified results, scale of impact, selectivity). Map {{OWNER_NAME}}'s career arc: what's the narrative through-line? "Built three companies from zero to exit" tells a different story than "Deep functional expertise across four industries." The resume must reflect the arc, not just list the stops.

- **Analyze:** Identify the resume's structural decisions. (a) Format: chronological vs. functional vs. hybrid — chronological is default; functional only if {{OWNER_NAME}} is making a deliberate career pivot. (b) Length: one page for early career, two for mid-to-senior, two max for most executives — extra pages decrease read likelihood by 40%. (c)Lead section: summary statement vs. key achievements vs. both — default to both because summaries are often generic and achievements are always specific. (d) Gap analysis: any missing metrics, outdated roles, or accomplishments overtaken by more recent, stronger ones?

- **Improve:**
  1. Draft the resume header: name, title, contact, LinkedIn. The title is the single most-read line on the resume — it must be accurate AND aspirational for the target role.
  2. Write the lead achievements block: 3-5 bullet-pointed career highlights, each starting with an action verb and including at least one number. These are the "greatest hits" — the things that make someone stop skimming.
  3. Build the experience section: reverse chronological. Each role gets: title, company, dates, 2-4 bullet points of quantified results. Bullets follow the formula: Action Verb → What Was Done → Quantified Result. "Led product launch that generated $2.4M in first-year revenue across 14 markets." Not "Responsible for product launch."
  4. Add education, certifications, board seats, speaking engagements, publications — weighted by relevance to the target audience.
  5. Run the "30-second skim test": a reader skimming for 30 seconds should see the title, the 3 highlights, and at least one impressive number from each role. If they don't, restructure.
  6. Deliver to {{OWNER_NAME}} with a one-line framing: "This is the version your work deserves. Read it and tell me what feels inaccurate — not what feels like bragging."

- **Control:** Verify every claim against the intake document. No embellishment, no inflation — accuracy is credibility. Confirm dates, titles, and company names. Run the resume through the under-claim detector (PA-29-05) to catch language that softens achievement. Set a quarterly reminder to refresh. Store the master version with a version date — {{OWNER_NAME}} should never wonder "is this the latest one?"

### CTQ Quality Checks
- [ ] Every role includes at least one quantified result
- [ ] Lead achievements block passes the "would a recruiter pause?" test
- [ ] All claims verified against intake document source data
- [ ] Version dated and stored as canonical master

### Metrics
1. **Resume freshness:** Days since last update. Target: ≤90 days.
2. **Quant density:** Percentage of bullet points containing a specific number or measurable result. Target: ≥80%.
3. **Under-claim score:** Percentage of bullets using active voice with strong verbs. Target: 100% ("Led," not "Was responsible for").

### Escalation
If {{OWNER_NAME}} requests a version that softens or removes legitimate accomplishments — "take that out, it feels like bragging" — push back once, gently, with evidence: "This is what happened, and it's impressive. Removing it underserves the reader and undersells your track record. I'll keep it, but I can adjust the framing if the tone feels off." If they insist, comply — but flag the pattern for the under-claim intervention (PA-29-05).

### Definition of Done
Master resume updated, version-dated, stored, and ready to send within 5 minutes of any opportunity surfacing. {{OWNER_NAME}} has the current file and knows where to find it.

### Tone & Persona Note
Resume architect voice: precise, evidence-based, quietly insistent on accuracy. You are not a cheerleader — you are a curator of facts. The resume speaks through data, not adjectives. "Your resume doesn't need to sell you. It needs to let your track record speak. Your track record is already compelling — I'm just making sure nothing mutes it."
