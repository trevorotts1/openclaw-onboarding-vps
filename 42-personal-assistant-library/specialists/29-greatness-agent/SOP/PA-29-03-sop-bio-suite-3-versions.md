# PA-29-03: Bio Suite (3 Versions)

**Title:** Bio Suite — 3 Versions
**SOP ID:** PA-29-03
**Purpose:** Produce and maintain three professionally distinct bios — Short (50 words), Standard (150 words), and Full (400+ words) — so {{OWNER_NAME}} never has to scramble when a speaking opportunity, media request, or partnership asks for a bio. Built from the canonical intake document (PA-29-01) and the Living Resume (PA-29-02).
**Trigger:** Intake document updated; new role, book, or major achievement; media or speaking opportunity surfaces; annual refresh; {{OWNER_NAME}} says "they need my bio."
**Inputs / Prerequisites:** Intake document (PA-29-01), Living Resume (PA-29-02), knowledge of {{OWNER_NAME}}'s current positioning, {{TOKEN}} for persona voice.

### DMAIC Procedure

- **Define:** Confirm the audience and context for each version. Short bio: event program, social profile, panel introduction — one paragraph, pure impact. Standard bio: website, speaker page, media kit, partnership pitch — two paragraphs, arc + credibility. Full bio: about page, book jacket, award submission, board nomination — multiple paragraphs, full narrative. Clarify any specific keywords, affiliations, or calls-to-action that must appear. Restate: "Three versions, one truth — each scaled to the space it fills, each making you impossible to under-describe."

- **Measure:** Audit the existing bio(s) if any. What's missing? What's outdated? What's phrased passively? Map the raw material: headline accomplishments (quantified results, from PA-29-02), career arc narrative (the "story" of the career, from PA-29-01), signature moments (3-5 stories of impact), current positioning (what {{OWNER_NAME}} is doing NOW), and credibility markers (degrees, certifications, board seats, awards, publications, media appearances). Every bio claim must trace back to the intake document.

- **Analyze:** The three versions serve different functions — don't just truncate the full bio. Short bio = ESSENCE. What one sentence captures who {{OWNER_NAME}} is and why it matters? Standard bio = ARC + CREDIBILITY. What's the journey, and what proves the expertise? Full bio = NARRATIVE + DEPTH. What's the story, the philosophy, the vision? Also analyze for under-claiming patterns: does the bio start with credentials (safe) rather than impact (bold)? Are results specific or vague? Does it end with a period or a statement of purpose?

- **Improve:**
  1. Short bio (50 words): "{{OWNER_NAME}} is [primary identity] who [most significant accomplishment in numbers]. [Current role/venture]. Previously [one credibility anchor]. [One humanizing detail or mission statement]." Edit relentlessly — every word earns its place.
  2. Standard bio (150 words): Paragraph 1 — who {{OWNER_NAME}} is now, what they've built, why it matters, with 2-3 quantified results. Paragraph 2 — career arc (where they came from), credibility markers, and a forward-looking statement of purpose. End with current focus.
  3. Full bio (400+ words): Open with impact — not "{{OWNER_NAME}} was born in..." but the most compelling result or story. Build the narrative arc with specific moments from the intake document. Layer in philosophy, approach, and vision. Include 5+ quantified results. Close with a forward-looking statement that makes the reader want to connect.
  4. For all three: third-person, active voice, present tense for current roles, past tense for previous. No adjectives that aren't proven by the facts that follow. No "passionate about," "dedicated to," "believes in" — show the action, skip the self-labeling.
  5. Deliver all three versions together with a brief framing: "Three bios, scaled to need. Read them as a reader would — not as someone wondering if you're bragging."

- **Control:** Verify every claim in every bio against the intake document. Run the under-claim scan (PA-29-05) on each version. Read each aloud — does it sound like {{OWNER_NAME}} or like generic bio language? Test the short bio: if it appeared under {{OWNER_NAME}}'s name on a conference website, would it make someone want to attend their session? Set calendar reminders for quarterly refresh.

### CTQ Quality Checks
- [ ] Short bio is ≤50 words and communicates primary impact in one sentence
- [ ] Standard bio includes 2-3 quantified results and a career arc
- [ ] Full bio includes 5+ specific accomplishments with numbers and closes with forward-looking purpose
- [ ] All three versions pass the under-claim scan (no passive-voice achievements, no self-diminishing language)

### Metrics
1. **Bio freshness:** Days since last update. Target: ≤180 days for full, ≤90 days for standard and short.
2. **Claim density:** Quantified or specific results per bio version. Target: Short ≥1, Standard ≥3, Full ≥5.
3. **Delivery speed:** Time from request to bio delivery. Target: ≤2 hours for existing bios, ≤24 hours for new versions.

### Escalation
If {{OWNER_NAME}} rejects a bio that accurately reflects their accomplishments — "this makes me sound too important" — escalate to the under-claim intervention (PA-29-05). The bio is evidence, not ego. If a specific opportunity requires a bio angle that conflicts with the standard versions (e.g., emphasizing community work over business for a nonprofit board), create a targeted variant — document it, but do not replace the master versions.

### Definition of Done
Three bio versions exist in canonical form, version-dated, stored, and ready to copy-paste into any request. {{OWNER_NAME}} has reviewed and approved all three. A quarterly refresh reminder is set.

### Tone & Persona Note
Bio writer voice: authoritative, precise, narrative-driven. You write {{OWNER_NAME}}'s story the way a journalist would — facts first, impact always, no filler. "A bio is not a biography. It's a handshake in text form. This handshake should feel warm, confident, and memorable — because the person it describes is all three."
