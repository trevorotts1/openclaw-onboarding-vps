# PA-29-06: Skills & Experience Gap Analysis

**Title:** Skills & Experience Gap Analysis
**SOP ID:** PA-29-06
**Purpose:** Identify the specific gaps between {{OWNER_NAME}}'s current capability profile and the requirements of a target role, board seat, speaking opportunity, or career aspiration — then produce a concrete, prioritized development plan to close those gaps.
**Trigger:** {{OWNER_NAME}} identifies a target opportunity; annual career planning; post-interview or post-rejection debrief; {{OWNER_NAME}} says "what am I missing to get to the next level?"; quarterly skills audit.
**Inputs / Prerequisites:** Intake document (PA-29-01), Living Resume (PA-29-02), target opportunity description or job specification, industry benchmarks for the target level, {{TOKEN}} for persona voice.

### DMAIC Procedure

- **Define:** Anchor the analysis in a specific target. "Gap analysis without a destination is just self-criticism. What's the chair you want to be in — specifically?" The target can be a job title ("Chief Revenue Officer at a Series B SaaS"), a board seat ("independent director, public company audit committee"), a speaking tier ("TED main stage"), or a capability milestone ("ready to raise a Series A"). A vague target produces a vague analysis. Clarify until the destination is concrete enough to measure against.

- **Measure:** Build two profiles side by side. Profile A — {{OWNER_NAME}}'s Current State: skills (technical, leadership, domain), experiences (roles, industries, company stages, deal sizes), credentials (degrees, certifications, board service, publications), network (relevant relationships in the target space), and demonstrated results (quantified accomplishments mapped to the target's requirements). All sourced from the intake document. Profile B — Target State: the explicit and implicit requirements of the target. Explicit = stated in the job description, board qualifications, or speaker criteria. Implicit = what's actually required to succeed, drawn from industry norms, peer benchmarks, and pattern analysis of who holds similar positions.

- **Analyze:** Map the gaps across five dimensions: (1) Hard Skills — technical, functional, or domain expertise {{OWNER_NAME}} lacks or hasn't demonstrated at the required level. (2) Leadership Scope — the largest team, budget, or P&L {{OWNER_NAME}} has managed vs. what the target requires. (3) Industry/Stage Experience — sectors, company stages (startup, scale-up, public), or business models where {{OWNER_NAME}} has no direct experience. (4) Credential & Network Gaps — degrees, board seats, affiliations, or relationships expected at the target level. (5) Narrative Gaps — not missing skills, but missing PROOF: {{OWNER_NAME}} has the capability but hasn't documented or articulated it in a way the target audience recognizes. This is the most common gap — and the one the Greatness Agent is uniquely positioned to close. For each gap, classify: Must-Close (dealbreaker for the target), Should-Close (significant advantage), or Nice-to-Close (marginal improvement).

- **Improve:**
  1. For Must-Close capability gaps: design a concrete acquisition plan. "Gain public company board experience" → specific path: join a nonprofit board with audit committee within 6 months, complete NACD certification within 12 months, target first public board within 18-24 months. Every plan has a timeline, a measurable milestone, and a next action due this week.
  2. For Must-Close narrative gaps: these are the Greatness Agent's sweet spot. {{OWNER_NAME}} HAS the capability; the evidence isn't packaged. Assign specific Greatness SOPs: intake document update to surface missing proof, bio revision to feature it, talking points to articulate it. Narrative gaps close in days, not months.
  3. For Should-Close gaps: prioritize by leverage — which gap, if closed, would make the biggest difference in {{OWNER_NAME}}'s positioning? Address the top 2; park the rest for quarterly review.
  4. Deliver the analysis as a gap matrix: Gap → Current State → Target State → Classification (Must/Should/Nice) → Close Plan → Timeline → Next Action. End with a one-page summary: "Here's where you are, here's where you want to be, here's the shortest path between them."
  5. Frame the delivery for momentum, not deficit: "You're closer than you think. Of the [X] gaps identified, [Y] are narrative gaps — you already have the capability; we just need to package the proof. The remaining [Z] are development gaps with clear paths to close."

- **Control:** Revisit the gap matrix quarterly. Mark gaps as: closed (capability acquired and demonstrated), in progress (on plan), stalled (no progress in 90 days — needs intervention), or reclassified (target changed). The analysis is only useful if it drives action. If no Must-Close gaps have moved in 90 days, escalate: the plan needs recalibration or {{OWNER_NAME}} needs support. After any major accomplishment, check whether it closes a previously identified gap — and celebrate the closure explicitly.

### CTQ Quality Checks
- [ ] Target is specific enough to measure against (title, level, sector, or milestone named)
- [ ] Gap matrix covers all five dimensions (Hard Skills, Leadership Scope, Industry/Stage, Credential/Network, Narrative)
- [ ] Every Must-Close gap has a concrete close plan with timeline and next action
- [ ] Narrative gaps are prioritized for immediate Greatness Agent action (days, not months)

### Metrics
1. **Gap closure rate:** Percentage of Must-Close gaps closed within projected timeline. Target: ≥70%.
2. **Narrative gap resolution speed:** Average days from narrative gap identification to proof packaging complete. Target: ≤14 days.
3. **Matrix freshness:** Days since last gap analysis update. Target: ≤90 days.

### Escalation
If the gap analysis reveals that {{OWNER_NAME}} is fundamentally under-qualified for a stated target — the Must-Close gaps are years of development, not months — surface this directly and supportively: "Based on the current profile, this target is a 3-5 year trajectory, not a next-move opportunity. Here's the intermediate step that gets you there." Escalate to Specialist 16 (Motivation & Momentum) if {{OWNER_NAME}} needs engagement and goal-reset support after this recalibration. If {{OWNER_NAME}} consistently selects targets far above current capability without engaging the development plan, escalate to Specialist 24 (Superwoman Syndrome) for overfunctioning-pattern evaluation. If narrative gaps dominate and {{OWNER_NAME}} resists packaging the proof, escalate to Specialist 25 (Imposter Syndrome) for belief-level intervention and to Specialist 28 (Celebration Agent) for win-recognition scaffolding. Notify Master Orchestrator if Must-Close gaps show no movement for 2+ consecutive quarters. Never inflate the profile to fit the target. Accuracy serves {{OWNER_NAME}} better than false encouragement.

### Definition of Done
Complete gap matrix delivered with close plans for all Must-Close and Should-Close gaps, narrative gaps routed to appropriate Greatness SOPs for immediate action, and a quarterly review reminder set.

### Tone & Persona Note
Strategic advisor voice: honest, constructive, forward-looking. You are not a gatekeeper — you are a navigator who says "here's where you are, here's where you want to go, and here's the map." The gap analysis should feel like a bridge, not a wall. "Gaps are not deficits. They're just the difference between what you've built so far and what you're building next. This analysis tells you exactly what to build — and in what order."
