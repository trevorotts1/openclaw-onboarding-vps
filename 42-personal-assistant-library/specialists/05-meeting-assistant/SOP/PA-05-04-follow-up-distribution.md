# PA-05-04 — Follow-Up Distribution

**SOP ID:** PA-05-04 | **Owner:** Meeting Assistant (S05) | **Version:** 2.0.0

---

## Purpose
Deliver meeting outcomes — notes, action items, and next-meeting preview — to every participant through their preferred channel within 2 hours of host approval, closing the loop so everyone is aligned and accountable.

## Trigger
Host approves final meeting package (notes + action items from PA-05-02 and PA-05-03) · host says "send the notes out," "distribute the follow-up," "let everyone know what we decided," "share the results."

## Inputs / Prerequisites
Approved meeting notes, action-item table, attendee list with preferred communication channels, host distribution preferences, next meeting date (if scheduled).

## DMAIC Procedure

**Define** — Two distribution tiers: full package (notes + all action items, for attendees and stakeholders) and individual summaries (personalized per-owner action lists). Confirm with host whether non-attendee stakeholders should receive the full package.

**Measure** — For each recipient, identify their best communication channel from contact records in {{INBOX_TOOL}}. Leadership may prefer {{MESSAGING_TOOL}}; clients may require {{INBOX_TOOL}} messaging; external partners may be email-only. Map every recipient to a channel before sending anything.

**Analyze** — Draft the distribution message: thank participants for their time, link to full notes, surface the top 2–3 decisions for immediate value. For individual lists, personalize with one line of context: "From our [meeting name] — here are your [N] action items with deadlines." Include next meeting date if scheduled so people have one less thing to track down.

**Improve** (numbered steps):
1. Apply host's voice and format preferences — mirror their sign-off style, level of formality, and channel habits.
2. Scan for conflicts: deadlines on holidays, owners out of office, references to renamed projects.
3. Send full package first, then individual action-item lists.
4. Log every delivery: what was sent, to whom, channel used, timestamp.
5. For any failed delivery, retry on an alternate channel within 30 minutes.

**Control** — Distribute within 2 hours of host approval. Set a 48-hour acknowledgment check for high-stakes meetings only; do not nag on routine standups. Track delivery success rate weekly.

## CTQ Checks
- [ ] All recipients received their package within 2 hours of host approval
- [ ] ≥95% first-attempt delivery success rate
- [ ] Host voice and format preferences correctly applied
- [ ] Zero instances of "I never got the notes" reported post-meeting

## Metrics
- First-attempt delivery success rate
- Average time from host approval to full distribution
- Host satisfaction confirmation rate within one business day

## Escalation
- **04-task-priority-manager** — for client-facing message tone, formatting, and brand voice alignment
- **Specialist 09 (Emotional Support & Wellbeing)** — if a client contact's delivery fails on all known channels, for relationship-level outreach

## Definition of Done
Every participant received their meeting outcomes through their preferred channel; distribution log is complete.

## Tone & Persona Note
Warm closure, not a transaction. Every follow-up message communicates "your time mattered." Match the host's warmth level — if they're informal and personal, you are too. For faith-centered communities, a simple "grateful for our time together" can be appropriate when it mirrors the host's voice. Never add religious language the host hasn't modeled themselves.
