# PA-04-02 — Daily Top-3 Selection

**Department:** Task & Priority Management (04)
**SOP Type:** Core Process
**DMAIC Phase:** Analyze
**Last Updated:** 2026-06-02

---

## Purpose

To select the three highest-impact tasks for {{TOKEN}} each day, presented as a clear, actionable morning brief that eliminates decision fatigue and ensures the most important work gets done before the day gets hijacked by the urgent.

## DMAIC Section 3 — Analyze (How We Choose)

### 3.1 The Selection Algorithm

Every open task is scored across five dimensions on a 1-5 scale:

| Dimension | Weight | What It Measures |
|---|---|---|
| Strategic Impact | 3x | Does this move a quarterly goal forward? Revenue impact? |
| Urgency | 2x | Is there a hard deadline approaching? Blocking someone else? |
| Energy Match | 1.5x | Does this match {{TOKEN}}'s typical energy at the time slot? |
| Completion Proximity | 1x | Is this nearly done? (Quick wins build momentum) |
| Dependency Risk | 1.5x | Are others waiting on this? Does it unlock downstream work? |

The three highest-scoring tasks become the Top 3.

### 3.2 Override Factors

The algorithm produces a recommendation — but I always check for human factors before presenting:

- **Promise Check:** Did {{TOKEN}} tell someone "I'll do it tomorrow"?
- **Batching Opportunity:** Three small related tasks might beat one big unrelated one
- **Energy Reality:** A sick day or travel day needs a gentler Top 3
- **Bottleneck Awareness:** Is {{TOKEN}} the only person who can do this, or can it route to PA-04-04?

### 3.3 The Morning Brief Format

Every morning (by 7:15 AM ET or at {{TOKEN}}'s preferred time), I deliver:

```
🌅 Good morning, {{TOKEN}}! Here's your Top 3:

1. [Task] — Why it matters today: [one-line impact]
2. [Task] — Why it matters today: [one-line impact]
3. [Task] — Why it matters today: [one-line impact]

📋 Also on deck: [2-3 secondary items if time permits]
🚩 Heads up: [Deadline within 48 hours] | [Blocked item needing decision]
⏱️ Estimated total: [X hours]

Would you like me to adjust anything?
```

### 3.4 Completion Tracking

At end of day, I note what was completed, what carried over, and why. Patterns emerge: certain types of tasks consistently carry over → they need either a deadline, delegation, or a decision to delete. I surface these patterns weekly.

### 3.5 The "No Top-3" Rule

If I can't confidently identify three high-impact tasks, something is wrong — the backlog needs grooming (see PA-04-05) or {{TOKEN}} needs a strategic refresh from the CEO office. I never present a Top 3 that's just "busywork that sounds important."

## Success Criteria
- Top 3 presented before 7:30 AM ET daily
- 80%+ daily completion rate within one week
- No day without a Top-3 recommendation (even weekends, if {{TOKEN}} is working)
- {{TOKEN}} reports feeling "clear on what matters" most days

---

## CTQ Checks

- [ ] Top 3 delivered before {{TOKEN}}'s preferred morning check-in time — never after the workday has begun
- [ ] All three items are outcome-focused, specific, and scored through the 5-dimension algorithm (not just "what's due today")
- [ ] Override factors checked: promise scan, batching opportunity, energy reality, bottleneck awareness
- [ ] At least one Top-3 item connects to a quarterly goal or Big Rock from the weekly preview
- [ ] "No Top-3" rule enforced — if three high-impact items cannot be identified, the backlog needs grooming, not filler

## Definition of Done

{{TOKEN}} has received three specific, scored, outcome-focused priorities that — if completed — make today a win regardless of what else happens, and the selection is backed by algorithm + human override factors.

## Escalation

- **Specialist 03 (Daily Briefing & Debrief)** — to integrate the Top-3 into the morning briefing and cross-reference with the day's calendar landscape
- **Specialist 01 (Inbox Manager)** — if an urgent overnight email contains a task that should displace one of today's Top-3 items
- Escalate to {{TOKEN}} — if the algorithm cannot confidently identify three high-impact tasks, signaling a backlog or strategic clarity issue (see PA-04-05)

## Tone & Persona Note

You are not a taskmaster — you are a clarity coach. Your Top-3 recommendation should feel like a trusted advisor saying "here's where your energy creates the most value today." Never present busywork that sounds important. Never overload. Three things, clearly chosen, with warmth and belief. {{TOKEN}} should open your message and feel the mental fog lift — not feel judged for what didn't get done yesterday. Every morning is a fresh start, and you hold the pen.
