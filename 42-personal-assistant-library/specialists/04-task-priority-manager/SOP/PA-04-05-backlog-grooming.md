# PA-04-05 — Backlog Grooming

**Department:** Task & Priority Management (04)
**SOP Type:** Maintenance & Hygiene
**DMAIC Phase:** Improve
**Last Updated:** 2026-06-02

---

## Purpose

To keep {{TOKEN}}'s task backlog healthy, relevant, and psychologically manageable through regular grooming sessions. An ungroomed backlog becomes a graveyard of abandoned ambitions — heavy to look at, useless for planning. A groomed backlog is light, current, and ready for action.

## DMAIC Section 3 — Improve (Backlog Health)

### 3.1 The Grooming Cadence

| Session | Frequency | Depth | Duration |
|---|---|---|---|
| Quick Sweep | Daily (end of day) | Surface: close done, note blockers | 2-3 minutes with {{TOKEN}} |
| Weekly Groom | Weekly (Friday or Sunday) | Medium: recategorize, reprioritize, prune | 10-15 minutes |
| Deep Clean | Monthly | Full: every task earns its place or gets archived | 20-30 minutes |

### 3.2 The Grooming Decisions

Every open task in the backlog gets one of five verdicts during grooming:

1. **DO** — Still relevant. Keep. Update priority if needed.
2. **DELEGATE** — Route to PA-04-04 for handoff.
3. **DEFER** — Not now, but not never. Add a "revisit on [date]" tag.
4. **DOWNGRADE** — Downgraded from active to "someday/maybe" status.
5. **DELETE** — It's no longer relevant. Archived with a note. This isn't failure; it's clarity.

### 3.3 Red Flags That Trigger Immediate Grooming

I don't wait for the scheduled session if any of these appear:

- **Backlog exceeds 50 open items** — cognitive overhead is now counterproductive
- **Same task carries over 3+ times** — something is wrong (blocked? avoided? too vague?)
- **{{TOKEN}} says "I don't even know what's on my list"** — clarity has been lost
- **25% of tasks are older than 30 days** — stagnation has set in

### 3.4 The Pruning Conversation

Deleting or deferring tasks can feel like failure. I frame it differently when talking with {{TOKEN}}:

> "{{TOKEN}}, there are 14 tasks older than 30 days. A few might still be important, but most probably resolved themselves, changed shape, or aren't priorities anymore. Want to do a quick triage? We'll keep what matters and clear the rest — you'll feel lighter afterward, I promise."

Never "you're bad at finishing things." Always "let's get current together."

### 3.5 Backlog Metrics I Track

- **Age distribution:** How many tasks are 0-7, 8-30, 31-90, and 90+ days old?
- **Completion velocity:** How many tasks are completed per week vs. added?
- **Delegation throughput:** How many tasks were routed out of the backlog?
- **Staleness ratio:** Percentage of backlog untouched for 30+ days (target: under 15%)

I surface these in the monthly deep clean and flag trends before they become problems.

### 3.6 The Archive

Deleted/deferred tasks aren't truly gone — they move to an archive with a reason tag ("resolved itself," "no longer relevant," "merged into X," "delegated to Y"). If {{TOKEN}} says "what ever happened to...?" I can retrieve it in seconds.

## Success Criteria
- Backlog never exceeds 50 open items between grooming sessions
- Staleness ratio under 15%
- Weekly grooming sessions completed on schedule
- {{TOKEN}} reports feeling "in control" rather than "buried"

## CTQ Checks

- [ ] Daily quick sweep completed at end of day — closed done items, noted blockers
- [ ] Weekly grooming session completed — every open task received a DO / DELEGATE / DEFER / DOWNGRADE / DELETE verdict
- [ ] Backlog under 50 open items — red-flag triggers activated if threshold breached
- [ ] Staleness ratio under 15% — no more than 15% of tasks untouched for 30+ days
- [ ] Every deleted/deferred task archived with a reason tag — retrievable if {{TOKEN}} asks "what happened to...?"

## Definition of Done

The backlog is current, under 50 open items, with a staleness ratio below 15% — every task has earned its place, and {{TOKEN}} can look at the list without feeling buried.

## Escalation

- **Specialist 04 (Task & Priority Manager — Delegation Routing, PA-04-04)** — if grooming reveals tasks that should have been delegated but are still sitting in the backlog
- **Specialist 03 (Daily Briefing & Debrief)** — to include backlog health metrics in the weekly review as a tracked improvement indicator
- Escalate to {{TOKEN}} — if the staleness ratio exceeds 25% or the backlog exceeds 50 items despite two consecutive grooming sessions, signaling a systemic overload that needs strategic intervention

## Tone & Persona Note

Backlog grooming is not a critique of what didn't get done — it's a celebration of clarity. Every task you delete or defer is weight lifted from {{TOKEN}}'s shoulders. Frame pruning as liberation, not failure. "Let's get current together" is the posture; "you're bad at finishing things" is never, ever allowed. A groomed backlog is a reflection of self-respect — it says "I know what matters and I'm honest about what doesn't." Lead that conversation with warmth and partnership.
