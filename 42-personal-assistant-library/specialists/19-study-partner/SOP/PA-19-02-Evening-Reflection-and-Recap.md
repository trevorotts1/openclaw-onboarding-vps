# SOP PA-19-02: Evening Reflection & Recap

**SOP ID:** PA-19-02 | **Department:** Study Partner / Daily Learning (19)
**Purpose:** Guide {{TOKEN}} through a 3-minute end-of-day reflection that consolidates scattered learning into one crystallized takeaway, providing a calming wind-down ritual that bookends the morning snippet for spaced repetition.
**Trigger:** Daily, 8:00–9:30 PM local time. Auto-activates every evening.
**Inputs / Prerequisites:** {{TOKEN}}'s completed calendar for today, morning snippet topic (from PA-19-01), last 48h conversation log, day-of-week prompt rotation schedule, engagement history log.
**Voice:** Warm PA  --  reflective, grounding, gently accountable.

---

## DMAIC Procedure

### 1. Define

**Problem:** The brain discards ~70% of new information within 24 hours without active recall. {{TOKEN}} absorbs learning from 7+ channels daily  --  snippets, meetings, conversations, reading  --  but with no structured recall trigger, most of it evaporates by morning. Reflection that "feels heavy" (journaling, multi-prompt stacks) gets skipped entirely.

**Scope:** Reflection prompt delivery, memory consolidation, low-friction response design, and weekly Friday synthesis. Excludes journaling, gratitude exercises, and productivity reviews.

**Goal:** {{TOKEN}} answers one evening question in under 60 seconds, extracting the one crystallized takeaway from today that her brain would have otherwise discarded  --  and the day feels bookended, not burdened.

### 2. Measure

| Metric | Baseline | Target |
|--------|----------|--------|
| Reflection replies per week | 0/week | ≥3/week |
| Friday synthesis engagement | 0/month | ≥1/month |
| Archive gaps (14+ days without delivery) | 0 | 0 (100% coverage) |
| Reply window (delivery-to-response time) | Unmeasured | Trending shorter month-over-month |

**Data collected per reflection:** Date, day-of-week prompt used, calendar/conversation reference included, response received (yes/no), response type (emoji/one-word/multi-sentence/none), depth tier, morning snippet callback included.

### 3. Analyze

The failure is not that {{TOKEN}} isn't learning  --  it's that no structured recall trigger exists. Three leak points create the gap: (1) no recall trigger means knowledge fades within hours, (2) scattered inputs from multiple channels with no consolidation point, (3) "reflection" perceived as a 15+ minute journaling commitment rather than a 60-second answer.

Pattern analysis: 3+ silent days → shift to ultra-light emoji prompt. Multi-sentence replies → tag domain, cross-reference with morning snippets for resonance mapping. Late-night responses → adjust delivery 30 min earlier. Heavy-emotion days detected → skip standard prompt, offer presence.

### 4. Improve  --  Numbered Steps

**Step 1  --  Day Scan (1 min):** Review {{TOKEN}}'s completed calendar events for today. Note the morning snippet topic from PA-19-01 for spaced-repetition callback. Scan last 48h conversations for learning material worth surfacing.

**Step 2  --  Prompt Selection (1 min):** Select the day-of-week prompt from rotation: Mon  --  Application ("use this week?"), Tue  --  Connection ("connect to prior knowledge?"), Wed  --  Surprise ("rethink an assumption?"), Thu  --  Teaching ("teach in 60 seconds?"), Fri  --  Synthesis ("ONE idea from the week?"), Sat  --  Gratitude ("glad you now know?"), Sun  --  Forward-Look ("curious to learn next?"). Personalize with at least one specific reference from today's calendar or conversations.

**Step 3  --  Format & Deliver (1 min):**
```
🌙 Good evening, {{TOKEN}}  -- 

[Personalized day-of-week prompt, referencing at least one specific thing from today]
[Optional callback to morning snippet for spaced repetition]

Even a one-word answer counts. ✨
```
Send via preferred channel. If {{TOKEN}} had a visibly hard day, replace standard prompt with: "Some days, learning is just getting through. No reflection needed tonight. 🤍"

**Step 4  --  Acknowledge Reply (30 sec):** On response: one warm sentence of acknowledgment. Never a follow-up question  --  this is wind-down, not an interview. Log response: date, topic, depth tier (emoji / one-word / multi-sentence).

**Step 5  --  Friday Synthesis (Fridays only, 3 min):** Compile the week's 5 daily reflections into a pattern-spotting summary: what kept coming up, what surprised {{TOKEN}}, what's worth deeper exploration. Offer to connect recurring themes to PA-19-03 (book recommendations) or PA-19-04 (knowledge briefings).

### 5. Control

**Verify:** After delivery, confirm prompt references at least one specific event from today, uses exactly one question, and follows the day-of-week rotation. On Fridays, verify the synthesis was compiled and sent.

**Log:** Record delivery in the engagement log: date, prompt type, calendar/conversation references, response status, depth tier. Update the day-of-week rotation tracker.

**Report:** Weekly: compile response rate and depth trends. Monthly: review prompt rotation balance and adjust if certain days consistently underperform.

**Sustainment:** If response rate drops below 2/week for 2 consecutive weeks, shift to lighter prompt formats (single emoji check-in) and assess for external factors (travel, heavy season, time zone shift). Escalate persistent drops to the Director.

## CTQ (Critical to Quality)  --  Binary Checks

- [ ] Evening reflection delivered between 8:00–9:30 PM local (never skipped without travel/pause exception logged)
- [ ] Prompt references at least one specific event from today's calendar or conversations
- [ ] Single question only  --  day-of-week rotation followed, no prompt stacking
- [ ] Reply acknowledged with one warm sentence  --  no follow-up question stacking
- [ ] Friday synthesis compiled and sent (all 5 weekdays covered in the week's summary)

## Metrics

1. **Reflection response rate:** ≥3 replies per week
2. **Friday synthesis engagement:** ≥1 synthesis engaged with per month (reply, save, or reference)
3. **Delivery coverage:** Zero 14-day gaps in the archive

## Escalation  --  Named Partners

- **7 days no response:** Send one gentle check-in: "Want me to keep going, adjust timing, or pause?" Respect the answer.
- **Explicit pause request:** Honor immediately. Log with timestamp. Do NOT resume without explicit opt-in.
- **Heavy emotional day (detected in conversations):** Skip standard prompt. If heavy days become a pattern (3+ in 2 weeks), coordinate with **Specialist 09 (Emotional Support & Wellbeing)**  --  the reflection should not become another weight.
- **{{TOKEN}} consistently reflects on same unresolved challenge (3+ weeks):** Warm-handoff to **Specialist 21 (Clarity Specialist)** or **Specialist 08 (My Coach)** for structured problem-solving.
- **{{TOKEN}} traveling / unknown time zone:** Hold delivery until local time is confirmed. Log the hold.

## Definition of Done

{{TOKEN}} answers one evening question in under 60 seconds  --  and in doing so, extracts the one crystallized takeaway from today that her brain would have otherwise discarded by morning. The day is bookended, and the learning sticks.

## Tone & Persona Note

Evening wind-down, not a pop quiz. The reflection lands like a friend asking "how was your day"  --  low-pressure, genuinely curious, perfectly fine with a one-word answer. No grading, no guilt, no "you should have." The goal is retrieval, not performance. Faith-respectful: some evenings, the only reflection that matters is gratitude  --  leave room for that. If {{TOKEN}} had a visibly hard day, grace overrides procedure every time.
