## Purpose

When {{TOKEN}} names a skill to learn or a problem to solve, this SOP finds the YouTube channels most likely to close that gap — curated, reasoned, and fast. Curated, reasoned, and fast.

---

## Section 3 — DMAIC Framework

### 3.1 Define

**Trigger:** {{TOKEN}} expresses a learning goal or skill gap. Examples: "I want to understand AI agents," "Show me the best sales psychology channels."

**What this SOP does:** Identifies 3–5 top YouTube channels matched to the stated goal with high signal-to-noise ratio. Does NOT build the playlist (PA-22-02) or judge whether the goal is worth pursuing.

**Success:** {{TOKEN}} opens a recommended channel and finds a directly relevant video within 5 minutes.

### 3.2 Measure

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Channels per match | 3–5 |
| Relevance score | Every recommendation tied to the goal |
| Time to deliver | Under 25 minutes |
| Off-target rate | 0% |

### 3.3 Analyze

**The Matching Path:**

1. **Clarify the goal in one sentence** — restate what {{TOKEN}} said. "You want channels that teach closing techniques for high-ticket sales — is that right?"

2. **Break into sub-skills** — "public speaking" becomes delivery mechanics, storytelling structure, slide design, managing nerves. Prevents matching a channel covering only 10% of the need.

3. **Search with precision** — Brave or Tavily: `best YouTube channels for [sub-skill] [level]`. Search each sub-skill separately.

4. **Evaluate each channel** on three dimensions:
   - **Authority:** Demonstrated expertise, production consistency, niche subscriber count.
   - **Relevance:** Watch 60 seconds of 2 recent videos. Teaching or entertaining?
   - **Recency:** Last upload within 3 months preferred.

5. **Format the recommendation** with channel name, direct link, 1-sentence match reason, best starting video, and sub-skill covered.

6. **Rank the list** — top pick first. Give {{TOKEN}} a clear starting point.

### 3.4 Improve

- Track which recommendations {{TOKEN}} actually watched. Underperformers get removed from future rotations.
- When {{TOKEN}} discovers a gem independently, add it to the master channel registry for faster matching next time.
- If a goal repeats quarterly, save the match results so the SOP becomes a lookup, not a re-search.

### 3.5 Control

- Every recommendation must map to a specific sub-skill. Cannot articulate the connection in one sentence? It does not belong.
- Never recommend based on subscriber count alone. A 12K specialist still teaching the craft beats a 450K-subscriber channel that drifted into vlogging.
- If fewer than 3 quality channels surface, say so. {{TOKEN}} deserves the truth, not padding.

## CTQ Checks

☐ Learning goal confirmed in one sentence with {{TOKEN}}
☐ Goal broken into sub-skills before channel search
☐ Every channel evaluated on Authority, Relevance, and Recency
☐ 3–5 channels recommended with direct links and one-sentence rationales
☐ Top pick identified and ranked first

## Escalation

| Signal | Route To |
|---|---|
| Fewer than 3 quality channels found | Tell {{TOKEN}} honestly; do not pad |
| Goal is vague — cannot break into sub-skills | Specialist 21 (Clarity Specialist), PA-21-01 |
| Learning goal involves clinical or therapeutic content | Specialist 26 (Therapeutic Support) |
| {{TOKEN}} disengages from all recommendations | Specialist 08 (My Coach) — goal alignment check |

## Definition of Done

{{TOKEN}} opens a recommended channel and finds a directly relevant video within 5 minutes, with a clear understanding of why this channel was matched to their goal.
