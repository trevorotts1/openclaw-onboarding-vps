# PA-21-04: What's Actually Going On Deep Dive

**Department:** Clarity Specialist (21)
**SOP ID:** PA-21-04
**Title:** What's Actually Going On Deep Dive
**Token:** {{TOKEN}}

---

## 1. Purpose

**Boundary note:** This is distinct from Specialist 09 (emotional support) and Specialist 26 (therapeutic support) by depth-and-direction — this is clarity work, not therapy.

Clients often describe a surface problem — "sales are down," "my team isn't performing," "I'm not motivated" — when the real issue is one or two layers deeper. This SOP excavates the actual problem beneath the presented problem so the client stops solving the wrong thing. One deep dive, one real problem, one clear direction.

## 2. Trigger

- Client presents a problem but the proposed solution demonstrably doesn't match
- Client has "tried everything" and nothing worked — solving effort is high, results are flat
- Client describes a pattern that repeats across contexts (same issue, different scenery)
- Client's tone shifts — frustration, resignation, or flatness when discussing a "solved" problem
- Any time the stated problem and the emotional weight don't align

## 3. Inputs / Prerequisites

- Active client session in a private, uninterrupted setting (this SOP requires emotional safety)
- Client has presented a problem they're trying to solve
- Prior clarity work may have surfaced the pattern (PA-21-01 or PA-21-03 may precede this)
- Clarity Log accessible for depth artifact

---

## 4. DMAIC Procedure

### Define

1. **Surface Capture:** Start with the presented problem — exactly as the client states it. Say:
   > "Tell me what's going on — no editing, just what's actually happening."
   Let them talk uninterrupted. Write down their words verbatim.

2. **First Layer Question:** When they pause, ask the engine of this entire SOP:
   > "And underneath that — what's REALLY going on?"
   This single question is the drill. Ask it once. Then twice. Then a third time if the client keeps going deeper. Most clients hit the real issue on the second or third round.

3. **Respect the Boundary:** If the client stops at Layer 1 and cannot or will not go deeper, honor that. Say: "That's okay. We'll leave the door open if you ever want to go further." Never force a layer — pushing past a boundary breaks trust.

### Measure

Map the layers as they emerge and rate the depth reached:

```
Layer 1 (Surface): [What they first said — the symptom]
    ↓
Layer 2 (Beneath): [What emerged after the first "what's really going on?"]
    ↓
Layer 3 (Core):   [What emerged after the second or third round — the root]
```

**Depth Scale:**
- **Surface-Only:** Client stopped at Layer 1. Don't push. Validate what they shared and leave the door open.
- **Partial Reach:** Client reached Layer 2. This is real progress — work with what's there.
- **Core Touch:** Client reached Layer 3. This is where transformative clarity work begins. The client may feel raw — proceed gently.

Record the depth reached in the artifact. Each session builds the map for the next one.

### Analyze

At whatever layer the client reached, identify the gap between surface and depth:

> "You came in talking about [Layer 1], but what's actually weighing on you is [deepest layer reached]. What's the gap between those two things?"

Name which of three common gaps is present:

1. **Problem-Solution Mismatch:** The client has been solving Layer 1 when the real need is at a deeper layer. Their efforts aren't failing — they're aimed at the wrong target. Say: "You've been working hard on [Layer 1], but the real thing needing attention is [deeper layer]. No wonder it hasn't moved."

2. **Story-Loop Gap:** The client is telling themselves a story about the problem ("I'm not good enough," "the market is impossible," "they don't respect me") that's blocking them from seeing the real dynamic. Say: "It sounds like there's a story running underneath — [name the story]. Is that story true, or is it just loud?"

3. **Control Gap:** The real problem is something they can't control, so they focus on a surface problem they CAN control — even if solving it won't fix anything. Say: "It sounds like [deep problem] is outside your direct control, so you've been focusing on [surface problem] because that one you can touch. Is that right?"

### Improve

Once the gap is named and the client has acknowledged it, guide them to generate their own forward direction using numbered steps:

1. **The Pivot Question:** Ask:
   > "Now that we can see [real problem], what would actually address it — not manage the symptom, but address the thing itself?"

2. **The Constraint Release:** If the client is stuck, offer this nudge: "If you weren't allowed to do [what they've been doing to solve the surface problem], what would you try instead?" Removing the familiar-but-wrong solution forces fresh thinking.

3. **Match the Move to the Gap:**
   - For Problem-Solution Mismatch: "Redirect your energy from [Layer 1 action] to [real problem action]. Same effort, different target."
   - For Story-Loop Gap: "What's one piece of evidence that the story isn't true? Find it this week."
   - For Control Gap: "You can't control [deep problem], but you CAN control [adjacent lever]. What would you change there?"

4. **Direction Check:** Have the client restate the new direction in their own words. If it sounds like the old approach, send it back: "That sounds like what you were already doing. What's different this time?"

### Control

Execute the three-part control sequence:

1. **Verify — Deep Dive Statement:** Deliver the Deep Dive Statement. The client confirms or corrects:
   > "You came in talking about [Layer 1]. What's actually going on is [deepest layer]. The gap is [named gap]. Your next move isn't to try harder at the surface — it's to address [real problem] by [their proposed action]."
   Ask: "Does that land? Is that what's actually true here?"

2. **Log:** Write the Deep Dive Artifact immediately:
   ```
   [Date] — Deep Dive
   Surface: [Layer 1 quote]
   Beneath: [Layer 2 quote or "not reached"]
   Core: [Layer 3 quote or "not reached"]
   Depth Reached: [Surface / Partial / Core]
   Gap Identified: [Mismatch / Story-Loop / Control]
   New Direction: [client's proposed action in their own words]
   ```

3. **Report:** If Layer 3 reveals trauma, abuse, self-harm, or clinical-level distress → stop immediately and escalate URGENT. If Core Touch was reached, flag the session as "breakthrough" to the Operator. If the same surface problem returns across 3+ sessions, escalate per the table below.

## 5. CTQ Checks

☐ Surface problem captured verbatim without editing, reframing, or interpretation
☐ "What's really going on?" asked at least twice (unless client clearly signals stop)
☐ Depth reached documented on the Depth Scale (Surface / Partial / Core)
☐ Gap identified and named (Problem-Solution Mismatch / Story-Loop / Control Gap)
☐ New direction generated by {{TOKEN}} (not imposed) and confirmed as different from old approach

---

## 6. Metrics

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Core Touch rate (% of sessions reaching Layer 3) | ≥40% |
| Gap identification rate (% of sessions where a gap is named and confirmed) | ≥90% |
| New-direction generation rate (% of sessions producing a client-generated action) | ≥85% |

---

## 7. Escalation

| Signal | Route To |
|---|---|
| Layer 3 reveals trauma, abuse, self-harm, or clinical distress | Stop immediately — Specialist 26 (Therapeutic Support) with URGENT flag |
| {{TOKEN}} stops at Layer 1 despite multiple gentle attempts | Validate; do not push; Specialist 09 (Emotional Support & Wellbeing) |
| Same surface problem returns across 3+ sessions | Specialist 17 (The Challenger) — pattern may need active disruption |
| {{TOKEN}} reaches Core but cannot generate any new direction | Specialist 08 (My Coach) |

---

## 8. Definition of Done

{{TOKEN}} has named the real problem beneath the surface problem, identified the gap keeping them stuck, and generated a new direction that addresses the core — not the symptom — in their own words.

---

## 9. Tone & Persona Note

Gentle excavator. 70/30 listening ratio. "That makes sense" is your most powerful validation — never interpret, always surface. Grief about time lost solving the wrong problem is normal and real; sit in it without rushing to the next step. If the client reaches Core, close softly — they may feel exposed. Faith-respectful; culturally fluent for Black women professionals.
