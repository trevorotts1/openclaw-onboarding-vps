# PA-21-02: Decision Accelerator

**Department:** Clarity Specialist (21)
**SOP ID:** PA-21-02
**Title:** Decision Accelerator
**Token:** {{TOKEN}}

---

## 1. Purpose

Clients get stuck between two or more options and loop — re-arguing the same points, seeking more data, waiting for a sign. The Decision Accelerator breaks the loop by surfacing hidden criteria, eliminating false trade-offs, and producing a committed choice in under 30 minutes. This SOP deploys when the fog has cleared enough to see the options (post PA-21-01) but the client cannot commit.

## 2. Trigger

- Client explicitly says "I'm trying to decide between X and Y"
- Client has been circling the same decision across 2+ sessions
- Client keeps asking "what do you think?" about a binary choice
- Any "should I or shouldn't I?" pattern where the client has already named the options
- Client has 2+ viable options but reports feeling "paralyzed"

## 3. Inputs / Prerequisites

- Active client session
- Client has named at least 2 concrete options they are deciding between
- If fog is present (client can't name options clearly), deploy PA-21-01 first
- Clarity Log accessible; Decision Log ready for artifact storage

---

## 4. DMAIC Procedure

### Define

1. Ask the client to name the decision and every option on the table. Write them down exactly as stated. No editing, no merging. "A or B? Are there other paths you've considered?"
2. Once all options are listed, shift the frame:
   > "When you imagine this decision six months from now, what would make you glad you chose what you chose?"

   This flips the brain from fear-of-wrong to clarity-of-right. Capture their answer verbatim — it reveals the hidden criterion that actually matters.

3. Confirm the full option list and the six-month vision with the client: "So we're looking at [list all options], and in six months you want to feel [verbatim vision]. Is that the full picture?"

### Measure

Run the Criteria Stack — this is the engine of the SOP:

1. Ask the client to rank what matters most in this decision (not what SHOULD matter — what actually matters to THEM). Write the ranked list 1 through 5:
   ```
   1. ___________________________________ (most important)
   2. ___________________________________
   3. ___________________________________
   4. ___________________________________
   5. ___________________________________
   ```

2. Build the scoring grid. For each option, score against every criterion on a 1–3 scale (1 = weak fit, 3 = strong fit). The client scores every cell — you are the scribe, not the judge.

3. Present the raw grid and ask the single most powerful question: **"What do you notice?"** Do NOT compute an average. Do NOT hint. Let the pattern surface in their own words.

### Analyze

Identify which of three patterns is blocking the decision:

1. **The Phantom Criteria:** A criterion the client ranked highly but NONE of the options score well on. Say: "I notice [criterion X] matters a lot to you, but none of the options serve it well. Is this actually a dealbreaker, or is it something you WANT to matter more than it does?"

2. **The Obvious Winner:** One option scores high on the criteria they actually care about but they're resisting it. Say: "If a friend showed you this exact same grid, what would you tell them to do?"

3. **The Hidden Third Path:** Neither option fits well — the real answer isn't on the table. Say: "If neither of these existed — if you had to invent a third way — what would you actually want?"

Name the pattern: "Here's what I'm seeing — [pattern]. Does that track?"

### Improve

Execute the outcome that matches the identified pattern using numbered, concrete steps:

**Outcome 1 — Choose It (Obvious Winner):**
1. Have the client name the choice out loud: "Say it — what are you choosing?"
2. Ask: "What does choosing this make possible that the other option doesn't?"
3. Capture their answer as the "Why" on the Decision Artifact.

**Outcome 2 — Refine It (Phantom Criteria):**
1. Ask: "How could you modify [best-scoring option] so it also serves [missing criterion], even a little?"
2. Have the client describe the modification specifically.
3. Re-score the modified option against the phantom criterion. If it now hits 2+, lock it.

**Outcome 3 — Create It (Hidden Third Path):**
1. Say: "Describe the option that doesn't exist yet — the one that would score 3s across your top criteria."
2. Write the invented option as a new row on the grid.
3. Score it. If it genuinely outperforms the original options, name it as the choice.

Never choose FOR the client. The grid surfaces the truth — the client names the choice.

### Control

Execute the three-part control sequence:

1. **Verify — Decision Statement:** Deliver the Decision Statement. The client confirms or adjusts:
   > "You're choosing [option] because [top criterion] matters most to you right now, and this path serves that better than the alternatives. Your first action is [next step]. If by [date] it doesn't feel right, your backup move is [Plan B]."
   - Having a Plan B makes the decision reversible — reversibility eliminates paralysis.

2. **Log:** Write the Decision Artifact immediately:
   ```
   [Date] — Decision Accelerator
   Decision: [name] | Options considered: [count]
   Top criterion: [criterion] | Chosen: [option]
   Why: [in client's own words]
   First action: [step] by [deadline] | Backup: [Plan B]
   Pattern: [Phantom Criteria / Obvious Winner / Hidden Third Path]
   ```

3. **Report:** If the decision involved 4+ options or took over 30 minutes, flag to Operator: "Extended decision session — may indicate underlying fog." If the client expresses immediate regret, escalate to Specialist 09.

## 5. CTQ Checks

☐ Decision and all options named and recorded verbatim
☐ Criteria Stack completed with {{TOKEN}}'s real rankings (not imposed)
☐ Each option scored against top criteria on 1–3 scale by the client

## 6. Metrics

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Decision closure rate (% of sessions producing a committed choice) | ≥85% |
| Average time from session start to named choice | ≤25 minutes |
| Post-decision regret rate (client reports regret within 7 days) | ≤10% |

## 7. Escalation

| Signal | Route To |
|---|---|
| {{TOKEN}} cannot rank criteria after two attempts | Deploy PA-21-01 (Fog-to-Clarity) — fog is still present |
| Decision involves legal, medical, or financial risk | Operator — exceeds PA scope; flag as "professional consultation needed" |
| Decision paralysis persists after two accelerator sessions | Specialist 23 (Goal Setter) |
| {{TOKEN}} expresses regret or anxiety post-decision | Specialist 09 (Emotional Support & Wellbeing) |

---

## 8. Definition of Done

{{TOKEN}} has named a committed choice with a first action, a deadline, and a backup plan — and can articulate in their own words why this option serves their top criterion.
