# PA-03-04 — Weekly Review

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **SOP ID** | PA-03-04 |
| **Title** | Weekly Review |
| **Version** | 1.0 |
| **Department** | Daily Briefing & Debrief (Specialist 3) |

---

## Purpose

To close every week with an honest, forward-looking retrospective that celebrates genuine progress, surfaces actionable patterns, and produces at least one concrete improvement for the week ahead — so {{COMPANY_NAME}} gets a little better every single week.

---

## Trigger

Friday afternoon in {{OWNER_TIMEZONE}}, between 2:00 PM and 5:00 PM. The review fires after the week's active work has wound down, before {{OWNER_NAME}} transitions into weekend mode.

---

## Inputs

- This week's Sunday preview (Big Rocks vs. actual outcomes)
- All five daily morning briefings and evening debriefs from the week
- Task completion data from {{TASK_TOOL}}
- Calendar data: total meetings, meeting hours, deep-work blocks used
- {{OWNER_NAME}}'s reflections or notes shared during the week
- Last week's Friday review (to track whether the improvement action was implemented)

---

## Define

The problem: Weeks blur together. Without a structured review, {{OWNER_NAME}} finishes Friday with a general sense of "busy" but no clear picture of what was actually accomplished, what patterns are holding them back, or what to do differently next week. The same bottlenecks repeat. The same distractions win. A weekly review turns experience into improvement by making the invisible visible.

---

## Measure

Before implementing this SOP, track: How many weeks end with {{OWNER_NAME}} feeling "I was busy but I'm not sure what I actually got done"? How often do the same bottlenecks or frustrations appear week after week? Is there a single documented improvement from last month that can be traced to a review? If not, the gap is clear.

---

## Analyze

The weekly improvement gap has three root causes: no dedicated time to reflect on the week as a whole, a focus on activity rather than outcomes, and the absence of a feedback loop that turns observations into next-week actions. The weekly review solves all three by creating a structured, data-informed retrospective that always produces one concrete change.

---

## Improve

Follow these steps every Friday:

1. **Review the Big Rocks.** Open Sunday's weekly preview. For each Big Rock, determine: completed, partially completed, or not started. For partials and misses, note what got in the way — not to blame, but to understand.

2. **Gather the data.** Pull task completion stats from {{TASK_TOOL}}: total tasks completed this week, average completion rate, any patterns in what got done vs. what didn't. From {{CALENDAR_TOOL}}: total meeting hours, deep-work blocks that were protected vs. interrupted, any days that were significantly over or under scheduled.

3. **Surface the wins.** List the week's genuine victories — not just tasks checked off, but outcomes achieved, decisions made, relationships strengthened, problems solved. Be specific. "Launched the new client portal" is a win. "Answered emails" is not. Aim for 5-7 substantive wins.

4. **Identify patterns.** Review the week's debriefs and ask: What kept recurring? What blocked progress repeatedly? What time of day was most productive? What drained energy? Name 2-3 patterns without judgment — they're data points, not criticisms.

5. **Extract one improvement.** Based on the patterns, identify exactly one thing to change for next week. Not five things. Not a resolution. One specific, actionable adjustment. Examples: "Block Tuesday and Thursday mornings for deep work — no meetings before 11 AM." "Move the team standup from 9 AM to 10 AM to protect the first hour." "Delegate invoice follow-ups to Specialist 3 starting Monday."

6. **Draft the review** in a consistent format:
   - **Big Rock scorecard:** Per-rock status with one-line explanation
   - **Week in numbers:** Tasks completed, meeting hours, deep-work blocks protected
   - **Wins:** 5-7 specific, outcome-focused victories
   - **Patterns observed:** 2-3 patterns with evidence, not opinion
   - **One thing to change:** The single improvement for next week
   - **Carryover to next preview:** Items the Sunday preview should incorporate

7. **Deliver.** Send the review through {{MESSAGING_TOOL}}. Keep it constructive and forward-looking — this is about getting better, not grading the past.

8. **Log for trend analysis.** Archive the review. Every quarter, review the "one thing to change" items — how many were actually implemented? What cumulative improvement do they represent?

---

## Control

- Compare this week's Big Rock completion to last week's. Is the trend improving?
- Track whether the "one thing to change" from last week's review was actually implemented. If not, surface the barrier — it may need to become a project of its own.
- Monthly: pick one pattern from the reviews and dig deeper with {{OWNER_NAME}}. "I've noticed afternoon energy dips in 3 of the last 4 Friday reviews — want to talk about restructuring the afternoon?"
- Quarterly: compile a "Continuous Improvement Report" from all weekly reviews — what changed, what improved, what's next.

---

## CTQ (Critical to Quality) — Binary Checks

- [ ] Review delivered Friday between 2:00–5:00 PM {{OWNER_TIMEZONE}}
- [ ] Big Rock scorecard covers every Big Rock from Sunday's preview
- [ ] Wins section is outcome-focused with at least 5 specific items
- [ ] At least one evidence-backed pattern is identified
- [ ] Exactly one concrete, actionable improvement is specified for next week

---

## Metrics

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| On-time delivery rate | ≥ 95% of Fridays |
| Big Rock completion rate (rolling 4-week average) | ≥ 70% |
| "One thing to change" implementation rate (next week) | ≥ 75% |
| {{OWNER_NAME}} reports "week felt intentional" (monthly check) | ≥ 8/10 |

---

## Escalation

Escalate to {{OWNER_NAME}} when: Big Rock completion drops below 40% for two consecutive weeks, a systemic bottleneck appears in 3+ weekly reviews without resolution, task completion rates are declining over a 4-week trend, or the same "one thing to change" fails to implement for three weeks straight (indicating a deeper barrier). Escalate with data and a recommended intervention.

**Partner escalation:**
- **Specialist 04 (Task & Priority Manager)** — if the "one thing to change" that keeps failing is task-priority-related and needs the Task Manager to restructure the workflow
- **Specialist 02 (Calendar & Scheduling Manager)** — if the bottleneck pattern is calendar-driven (too many meetings, no deep-work blocks protected)

---

## Definition of Done

A weekly review is complete when: all five CTQ checks pass, the review has been sent to {{OWNER_NAME}} via {{MESSAGING_TOOL}}, the "one thing to change" is logged as a tracked action item, and the review is archived for quarterly trend analysis. Done means next week will be better than this week — measurably.

## Tone Note

The weekly review is not a report card — it's a growth session. {{OWNER_NAME}} doesn't need a grade. They need a mirror that reflects what's actually happening and a gentle nudge toward what could be better. Celebrate the wins with genuine warmth. Name patterns with curiosity, not criticism. The "one thing to change" should feel like an exciting experiment, not a correction. Make {{OWNER_NAME}} look forward to next week, not dread the review of this one.
