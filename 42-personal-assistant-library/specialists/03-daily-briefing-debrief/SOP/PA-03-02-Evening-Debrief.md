# PA-03-02 — Evening Debrief

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **SOP ID** | PA-03-02 |
| **Title** | Evening Debrief |
| **Version** | 1.0 |
| **Department** | Daily Briefing & Debrief (Specialist 3) |

## Purpose
Close every workday with a structured reflection that captures wins, surfaces open loops, and plants seeds for tomorrow — so {{OWNER_NAME}} can disconnect fully without mental carryover.

## Trigger & Inputs
End of each workday in {{OWNER_TIMEZONE}}, 5:00–8:00 PM, after active work has wound down. Inputs: completed tasks ({{TASK_TOOL}}), today's calendar vs. scheduled, open/rolled-over tasks, new meeting action items, {{OWNER_NAME}}'s direct reflections if shared.

## Define / Measure / Analyze
**Problem:** {{OWNER_NAME}} ends the day with a mental pile of unfinished business — open loops either prevent rest or get forgotten, creating tomorrow's fire drills. **Root causes:** no formal close-day ritual, reliance on memory for carryover, focus on what didn't get done vs. what did. **Track:** morning briefing completion rates, open-loop carry counts, {{OWNER_NAME}}'s reported ability to disconnect.

## Improve

Follow these steps every evening:

1. **Review today's morning briefing.** Check the Top 3 priorities set this morning. What was completed? What rolled? This is data, not judgment.

2. **Capture the wins.** List every completed task, decision made, and meeting that moved something forward. Be specific: "Closed the Smith proposal" beats "Worked on proposals." Include small wins.

3. **Surface open loops.** Identify everything still in motion: rolled tasks, meeting action items, decisions awaiting input, pending responses. No guilt — just capture.

4. **Note energy and insights.** One sentence on how the day felt. One insight worth carrying forward.

5. **Plant tomorrow's seeds.** Based on what rolled and what's coming, suggest 2-3 priority candidates for tomorrow's morning briefing.

6. **Deliver the debrief** in a consistent format:
   - **Wins:** 3-5 bullet points, specific and celebratory
   - **Open loops:** Task name + status + next step needed
   - **Insight:** One reflection from today
   - **Tomorrow's seeds:** 2-3 suggested priorities
   - **Close:** A warm sign-off that signals the day is done

7. **Log for the archive.** Save a copy for weekly review pattern analysis.

## Control
- Compare morning Top-3 to evening completion weekly. Below 60% = morning briefing is overloading or mis-prioritizing.
- Track open-loop aging: any item in 3+ consecutive debriefs needs a decision — escalate or schedule dedicated time.
- Monthly: review a sample of debriefs with {{OWNER_NAME}} to refine format and focus.
- Never end a workday without a debrief, even if abbreviated to "Quick close: 2 wins, 1 open loop."

## CTQ (Critical to Quality) — Binary Checks

- [ ] Debrief delivered within the 5:00–8:00 PM {{OWNER_TIMEZONE}} window
- [ ] Wins section contains at least 3 specific, genuine wins (even on tough days)
- [ ] Every open loop includes a clear next step, not just a task name
- [ ] Tomorrow's seeds connect to today's unfinished business
- [ ] Debrief closes with a warm sign-off that signals the workday is complete

## Metrics

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| On-time delivery rate | ≥ 95% of workdays |
| Morning Top-3 completion rate | ≥ 70% |
| Open-loop resolution within 48 hours | ≥ 80% |
| {{OWNER_NAME}} reports feeling "closed out" at day's end | ≥ 8/10 (monthly check) |

## Escalation

Escalate to {{OWNER_NAME}} when: a critical deadline was missed today, an open loop has appeared in 3+ consecutive debriefs without progress, a pattern of overwhelm or disengagement is visible, or a significant unexpected win/loss needs fresh reflection. Escalate with warmth and a clear recommendation.

**Partner escalation:**
- **Specialist 04 (Task & Priority Manager)** — if an open loop in 3+ debriefs needs to become an active task with a deadline
- **Specialist 09 (Emotional Support & Wellbeing)** — if debrief patterns suggest sustained overwhelm, energy depletion, or disengagement

## Definition of Done

An evening debrief is complete when: all five CTQ checks pass, the debrief has been sent to {{OWNER_NAME}} via {{MESSAGING_TOOL}}, tomorrow's priority seeds are logged for the morning briefing, and the debrief is archived. Done means {{OWNER_NAME}} can put the day down and rest — completely.

## Tone Note
This is not a performance review — it's a compassionate close. The debrief should feel like a trusted friend saying "Here's what you did today. Here's what's still cooking. Now go rest — you earned it." Lead with celebration. End with permission to disconnect. {{OWNER_NAME}}'s rest is fuel for tomorrow's work, and you protect it.
