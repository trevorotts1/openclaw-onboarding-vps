# PA-03-03 — Weekly Preview

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **SOP ID** | PA-03-03 |
| **Title** | Weekly Preview |
| **Version** | 1.0 |
| **Department** | Daily Briefing & Debrief (Specialist 3) |

## Purpose
Deliver a strategic week-ahead overview every Sunday — {{OWNER_NAME}} enters Monday with confidence and Big Rocks protected.

## Trigger & Inputs
Sunday 4:00–8:00 PM {{OWNER_TIMEZONE}}. Inputs: full week calendar ({{CALENDAR_TOOL}}), active projects/deadlines ({{TASK_TOOL}}), last Friday review, travel/anomalies, {{OWNER_NAME}}'s intentions if shared.

## Diagnose
**Problem:** {{OWNER_NAME}} discovers forgotten meetings and unprepared-for deadlines on Monday. Sunday anxiety stems from no clear map. **Root causes:** no weekend calendar review, Big Rocks not identified and protected, preparation gaps surfacing too late. **Track:** Sunday anxiety frequency, Monday surprises, priorities pushed week after week.

## Improve

Follow these steps every Sunday:

1. **Pull the full week.** Open {{CALENDAR_TOOL}} and review every event from Monday through Sunday. Note: total meeting count, any days that are unusually heavy or light, any travel or out-of-office blocks, and any preparation-heavy meetings.

2. **Review active projects and deadlines.** From {{TASK_TOOL}}, pull all projects with activity in the last two weeks and all deadlines in the next 14 days. Flag anything due this week with a specific prep timeline.

3. **Identify the Big Rocks.** Based on calendar and project review, select 3-5 non-negotiable priorities for the week. These are the outcomes that — if accomplished — make the week a success regardless of what else happens. Each Big Rock should be outcome-focused, not task-focused: "Close the partnership agreement" not "Work on the partnership thing."

4. **Flag preparation gaps.** For each key meeting and deadline, ask: does {{OWNER_NAME}} have what they need? If a Tuesday presentation needs slides, flag it Sunday — not Tuesday morning. If a Friday deadline needs data from someone else, note who and when to follow up.

5. **Check for balance.** Scan the week for: at least one deep-work block, at least one personal or recharge block, and no single day with more than 5 hours of meetings. Flag imbalance as a risk.

6. **Draft the preview** in a consistent format:
   - **Week at a glance:** Total meetings, heavy days, light days, any anomalies
   - **Big Rocks:** 3-5 outcome-focused priorities, ranked
   - **Schedule highlights:** Key meetings with prep status
   - **Deadline watch:** Everything due this week or next
   - **Preparation gaps:** What needs attention before it's urgent
   - **Intention question:** One reflective question to set the week's tone ("What's the one thing you want to feel proud of by Friday?")

7. **Deliver.** Send the preview through {{MESSAGING_TOOL}}. Make it scannable — {{OWNER_NAME}} should get the full picture in under two minutes of reading.

## Control
Archive weekly previews. Compare Friday outcomes to Sunday Big Rocks; below 60% = recalibrate selection. Monthly: check Monday anxiety reduction. Track prep-gap closure. Never skip a Sunday intentionally.

## CTQ (Critical to Quality) — Binary Checks

- [ ] Preview delivered Sunday between 4:00–8:00 PM {{OWNER_TIMEZONE}}
- [ ] Big Rocks are outcome-focused (not tasks) and ranked by importance
- [ ] Every day with 4+ hours of meetings is flagged for balance risk
- [ ] At least one preparation gap is identified OR explicitly confirmed as "none needed"
- [ ] Intention question is present and genuinely reflective

## Metrics

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| On-time delivery rate | ≥ 95% of Sundays |
| Big Rock completion rate | ≥ 70% |
| Monday-morning surprise incidents | ≤ 1 per month |
| {{OWNER_NAME}} Sunday-anxiety reduction | ≥ 7/10 |

## Escalation

Escalate to {{OWNER_NAME}} when: a make-or-break deadline falls this week with incomplete preparation, the calendar shows an impossible week (e.g., 40+ hours of meetings with no deep-work blocks), or a critical stakeholder meeting lacks required materials. Escalate with a specific risk statement and your recommended mitigation.

**Partner escalation:**
- **Specialist 02 (Calendar & Scheduling Manager)** — if the calendar reveals an impossible week needing an immediate recurring-meeting audit or conflict resolution
- **Specialist 04 (Task & Priority Manager)** — if Big Rock completion from last week was below 40%, indicating priority filtering needs recalibration

## Definition of Done

A weekly preview is complete when: all five CTQ checks pass, the preview has been sent to {{OWNER_NAME}} via {{MESSAGING_TOOL}}, Big Rocks are logged for Friday review comparison, and any escalated risks have been acknowledged. Done means {{OWNER_NAME}} can go to sleep Sunday night knowing exactly what matters this week and what needs attention before it's urgent.
