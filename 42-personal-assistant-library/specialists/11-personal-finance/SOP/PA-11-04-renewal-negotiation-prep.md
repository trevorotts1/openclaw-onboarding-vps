## DMAIC Section 3 — Analyze (The Loyalty Penalty)

### 3.1 What the Data Shows

Companies bank on inertia. Here's what I've documented across dozens of renewal scenarios:

| Reality | What Companies Count On |
|---|---|
| **Existing customers pay MORE than new customers.** Cable, insurance, streaming, software — the "new customer promo" discount is funded by loyal customers who never call to renegotiate. | That {{TOKEN}} won't notice the rate creep and won't make the 15-minute call. |
| **Retention departments have unadvertised discounts.** Most companies have a 10-30% discount they can apply instantly — but only if you say the magic words: "I'm considering cancelling." | That {{TOKEN}} will accept the first offer or, worse, not ask at all. |
| **Competitor pricing is public and aggressive.** In nearly every category, at least one competitor is offering a materially better deal right now. The switching cost is the only moat. | That {{TOKEN}} won't do the 10 minutes of research to find the competitor offer. |
| **Auto-renewal terms are designed to be missed.** Cancellation windows are narrow (24-48 hours before renewal), buried in emails you didn't read, and require phone calls during business hours. | That {{TOKEN}} will notice the renewal charge on the credit card statement 3 days AFTER it processed. |
| **Bundle discounts hide individual overcharges.** "Save 20% by bundling!" — but the bundle includes services {{TOKEN}} doesn't use, and unbundling would save 35%. | That {{TOKEN}} won't do the itemized math. |

### 3.2 The Negotiation Readiness Gap

The single biggest reason people overpay on renewals: they don't know the renewal is coming until it's already processed. The second biggest: they don't have the competitor price in hand when they call. Without both — awareness AND ammunition — the conversation is "Can I get a better deal?" which gets a polite no. With both, it becomes "X is offering the same service for $Y less. Can you match it?" which gets a yes or a transfer to retention.

## Procedure

### Step 1: Build the Renewal Brief (30 Days Out)

For each upcoming renewal, I prepare a one-page briefing document:

**Section A — Current State**
- Current provider, plan name, monthly/annual cost
- Last 12 months of charges (identify any rate increases)
- Actual usage: how much of the service does {{TOKEN}} use? (e.g., "Pays for 2TB cloud storage, uses 47GB")
- Contract end date and cancellation requirements (notice period, early termination fee)

**Section B — Competitive Landscape**
- 2-3 competing offers with pricing, features, and promo terms
- Any "switch and save" or "new customer" promotions currently available
- Switching cost estimate (time, data migration, learning curve)

**Section C — Leverage Assessment**
- High leverage: competitor offers are 20%+ cheaper, switching cost is low, contract is month-to-month
- Medium leverage: competitor is 10-20% cheaper with some switching friction
- Low leverage: monopoly/utility provider, no viable alternative, locked into contract

### Step 2: Write the Script

I draft a brief phone/chat script {{TOKEN}} can use verbatim:

> "Hi, I'm reviewing my account and noticed I'm paying [current rate]. I see that [competitor] is offering [comparable plan] for [competitor price]. I'd prefer to stay with you — is there a discount or retention offer you can apply to bring my rate closer to that?"

Key phrases I include:
- "I'd prefer to stay with you" (signals retention opportunity, not hostility)
- A specific competitor name and price (forces a real response, not a generic "let me check")
- Silence after the ask (let them fill the gap — the first person to speak loses)

### Step 3: Set the Timeline

| Days Before Renewal | Action |
|---|---|
| 30 days | Briefing packet delivered to {{TOKEN}} |
| 21 days | Reminder: "Have you had a chance to review the renewal brief for [service]?" |
| 14 days | If {{TOKEN}} is handling the call: "This is your 2-week heads-up. The cancellation window opens soon." |
| 7 days | If I'm authorized to negotiate on {{TOKEN}}'s behalf: I initiate the call/chat. |
| 48 hours | Final decision required. {{TOKEN}} chooses: renew at new rate, switch to competitor, or cancel. |

### Step 4: Document the Outcome

After the negotiation closes:
- Log the result: new rate, term length, any concessions or added features
- Update PA-11-01 (Bill Calendar) with new amount and next renewal date
- Set a calendar reminder for the NEXT renewal negotiation (11 months out for annual contracts)
- Calculate and report savings: "Your 20-minute call saved $348 this year."
