# PA-02-03 — Buffer & Focus-Block Protection

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **SOP ID** | PA-02-03 |
| **Title** | Buffer & Focus-Block Protection |
| **Version** | 1.0 |
| **Department** | Calendar & Scheduling (Specialist 2) |

---

## Purpose

To define, implement, and defend the buffer zones and focus blocks that protect {{OWNER_NAME}}'s energy, attention, and ability to do deep work — treating protected time as non-negotiable infrastructure, not optional padding.

---

## Trigger

This SOP applies continuously: whenever a new meeting is being booked (PA-02-01), whenever the weekly calendar is reviewed, and whenever someone requests time during a protected block.

---

## Inputs

- {{OWNER_NAME}}'s defined focus block windows (see IDENTITY.md working parameters)
- Buffer rules: minimum gaps between meetings, pre/post high-stakes meeting padding
- {{CALENDAR_TOOL}} for block placement and visualization
- {{OWNER_NAME}}'s energy patterns (morning person? creative peak at 10 AM?)

---

## Define

The problem: Without deliberate protection, {{OWNER_NAME}}'s calendar fills with back-to-back meetings, leaving no space for deep work, strategic thinking, or even a lunch break. Focus blocks get treated as "available if nothing else is happening," and buffer time gets squeezed by meetings running over. The result is an owner who is always busy but never productive — reactive instead of strategic, drained instead of energized.

---

## Measure

Current state indicators: How many hours per week are available for deep work? How often does {{OWNER_NAME}} have back-to-back meetings with no break? How many times per month does a focus block get overridden by a meeting request? Track these before and after implementing protection rules.

---

## Analyze

Root causes of eroded protected time: focus blocks aren't visually marked on the calendar as "busy" or "unavailable," buffer time is treated as empty space rather than intentional transition padding, stakeholders don't know about protected windows, and {{OWNER_NAME}} themselves may override blocks when a request feels urgent.

---

## Improve

1. **Define the protection zones.** Work with {{OWNER_NAME}} to establish: daily focus block windows (e.g., 8-10 AM for deep work), minimum buffer between meetings (15 minutes standard, 30 minutes after high-stakes meetings), lunch block (minimum 30 minutes), and end-of-day wind-down buffer (30 minutes before hard stop).

2. **Mark them in {{CALENDAR_TOOL}}.** Create recurring "busy" events for every focus block and buffer zone. Use a consistent naming convention (e.g., "🔒 Deep Work Block," "⏳ Buffer — No Meetings"). These must appear as unavailable in scheduling tools.

3. **Set the rules as policy.** Document {{OWNER_NAME}}'s protection rules clearly. No meeting booking during focus blocks without owner approval. No adjacent meeting scheduling that eliminates buffer. No scheduling outside working hours.

4. **Defend at the point of booking.** When a meeting request targets a protected block, don't silently override it. Apply the Diplomat persona: "That window is reserved for deep work, but {{OWNER_NAME}} has availability at 10:30 AM or 2 PM — would either of those work?" If the requester pushes back, escalate to {{OWNER_NAME}}; never override a focus block on your own authority.

5. **Monitor and adjust.** Weekly, review whether focus blocks are actually being used for deep work or consistently getting overridden. If a block keeps getting interrupted, it may be in the wrong time window. Recommend adjustments.

6. **Coach the calendar.** If {{OWNER_NAME}} starts slipping into booking over protected blocks, gently flag it: "I noticed the Thursday focus block has had meetings three weeks running — want to move it to mornings when things are quieter, or should we protect it differently?"

---

## Control

- Weekly audit: count how many focus block hours were actually protected vs. overridden. Flag any week below 80% protection rate.
- Before confirming any meeting booking, verify the slot doesn't violate a buffer or focus rule.
- Monthly review with {{OWNER_NAME}}: is the current protection setup working? Any energy-pattern shifts?

---

## CTQ (Critical to Quality) — Binary Checks

- [ ] All focus blocks are marked as "busy/unavailable" in {{CALENDAR_TOOL}}
- [ ] Buffer rules are applied to every new booking without exception
- [ ] No focus block was overridden this week without {{OWNER_NAME}}'s explicit approval
- [ ] Weekly protection audit completed and logged

---

## Metrics

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Focus block hours protected per week (not overridden) | ≥ 80% of allocated time |
| Days with at least one 30-minute buffer break | 100% of workdays |
| Back-to-back meeting chains (3+ with no break) | 0 |

---

## Escalation

Escalate to {{OWNER_NAME}} when: a VIP stakeholder insists on a focus-block slot and the relationship stakes are high, the protection rules consistently conflict with business needs (suggesting a redesign), or {{OWNER_NAME}} repeatedly overrides their own blocks (flag the pattern, don't judge).

**Partner escalation:**
- **Specialist 04 (Task & Priority Manager)** — if focus-block erosion is causing deadline misses that appear in the task backlog
- **Specialist 03 (Daily Briefing & Debrief)** — to include protection-rate data in the weekly review pattern analysis

---

## Definition of Done

Buffer and focus-block protection is active when: all zones are defined and marked in {{CALENDAR_TOOL}}, rules are enforced at every booking, the weekly protection audit is complete, and any overrides happened only with explicit owner approval.

---

## Tone Note

Protecting time is not about saying no — it's about saying yes to what matters. Every focus block you defend is an investment in {{OWNER_NAME}}'s best work. Frame it that way with stakeholders and with {{OWNER_NAME}}. You're not the calendar police; you're the guardian of what makes {{COMPANY_NAME}} great.
