# PA-02-04 — Meeting Prep Brief

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **SOP ID** | PA-02-04 |
| **Title** | Meeting Prep Brief |
| **Version** | 1.0 |
| **Department** | Calendar & Scheduling (Specialist 2) |

## Purpose
Deliver a prep brief before every meeting — {{OWNER_NAME}} knows who, why, context, and success criteria.

## Trigger & Inputs
Due 24h before meetings ({{TASK_TOOL}} task via PA-02-01). Inputs: event details, prior comms ({{INBOX_TOOL}}/CRM), notes, docs, {{OWNER_NAME}}'s priorities.

## Diagnose
**Problem:** {{OWNER_NAME}} arrives underprepared. **Root causes:** no systematic prep, scattered context, prep window too short. **Track:** prep satisfaction, meeting success rate.

## Improve

1. **Retrieve the full context.** Pull from: the calendar event details (agenda, purpose, attendees), the last 2-3 email threads or messages with these attendees, any CRM notes or relationship history, and documents referenced in the meeting invitation.

2. **Build the brief in three sections.** Keep it tight — this is a pre-read, not a research paper. Section 1: WHO — attendees, roles, relationship context (one line each). Section 2: WHY — the meeting's purpose, desired outcome, and what decision or next step should come out of it. Section 3: WHAT TO KNOW — key context from prior conversations, open action items, sensitive topics to avoid or address, and any prep {{OWNER_NAME}} should do (read a document, review a proposal).

3. **Flag what matters most.** Put the single most important thing {{OWNER_NAME}} needs to know at the very top. "Key point: They're likely to ask about the Q3 numbers. The latest report is linked below."

4. **Deliver to {{OWNER_NAME}} 24 hours before.** Drop the brief in the calendar event description, send via {{INBOX_TOOL}} or preferred delivery channel, and include a direct link to any referenced documents.

5. **For high-stakes meetings, go deeper.** Add a "Suggested Approach" section: opening framing, key questions to ask, what to listen for, and what to avoid. This turns the brief from a summary into a strategy document.

6. **Update if context changes.** If new information emerges between brief delivery and meeting time, send a quick one-line update: "Quick update — they just sent over the revised contract. Key changes flagged in the brief."

## Control
Confirm brief was useful after meeting.

## CTQ (Critical to Quality) — Binary Checks

- [ ] Brief delivered at least 24 hours before meeting
- [ ] Includes WHO section (attendees, roles, relationship context)
- [ ] Includes WHY section (purpose, desired outcome, decision needed)
- [ ] Includes WHAT TO KNOW section (prior context, action items, sensitive topics)
- [ ] Top-line "key point" highlighted at the very beginning

## Metrics

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Prep briefs delivered on time | ≥ 95% |
| {{OWNER_NAME}} satisfaction | ≥ 8/10 |
| Meeting purpose achieved | ≥ 85% |

## Escalation

Escalate to {{OWNER_NAME}} when: context is insufficient to build a useful brief (ask for a 2-minute verbal download), the meeting involves highly sensitive topics that need owner guidance on framing, or you discover information during prep research that {{OWNER_NAME}} needs to know about before the meeting.

**Partner escalation:**
- **Specialist 05 (Meeting Assistant)** — if the prep brief reveals an agenda gap needing a rebuilt agenda
- **Specialist 01 (Inbox Manager)** — if key context is missing from the communication record

## Definition of Done

A prep brief is complete when: all three sections (WHO/WHY/WHAT TO KNOW) are populated with accurate, current information, the brief is attached to the calendar event or sent to {{OWNER_NAME}}, delivery was at least 24 hours before the meeting, and any late-breaking updates have been flagged.
