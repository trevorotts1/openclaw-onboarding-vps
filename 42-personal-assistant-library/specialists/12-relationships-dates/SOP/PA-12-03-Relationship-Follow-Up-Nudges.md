## Purpose

To ensure no relationship drifts into silence — every important connection in {{TOKEN}}'s life receives the right touchpoint at the right cadence, so relationships deepen over time instead of fading through neglect.

---

## DMAIC Framework

### Define

The follow-up nudge system is the heartbeat of relationship maintenance. It tracks when {{TOKEN}} last connected with each key person, surfaces contacts who are due for a touchpoint, and suggests contextually relevant reasons to reach out. The goal is simple: every person who matters to {{TOKEN}} hears from them before they have to wonder "have I been forgotten?" This is not about volume — it's about intentionality. A single warm message at the right moment is worth more than ten generic check-ins.

### Measure

| Metric | Target | How Measured |
|--------|--------|---------------|
| Contacts with stale touchpoints (>30 days, Tier 1) | 0 | CRM query weekly |
| Contacts with stale touchpoints (>90 days, Tier 2) | <5% | CRM query weekly |
| Nudge acceptance rate (% nudges {{TOKEN}} acts on) | ≥70% | Action log |
| Relationship escalations (someone reached out because they felt forgotten) | 0 | Inbound tracking |

### Analyze

Relationships go quiet for predictable reasons: (a) no trigger — there's no birthday or holiday, so it feels awkward to "just say hi"; (b) the last interaction wasn't logged, so the CRM thinks contact lapsed when it actually happened last week; (c) the nudge was too generic — "check in with Sarah" doesn't spark action, but "Sarah mentioned her daughter's dance recital was last weekend — ask how it went" does. The solution is: always provide a hook, always log the touch, and never let a Tier-1 relationship exceed 30 days of silence.

### Improve

1. **Context-Rich Nudges:** Every nudge includes a specific, personal hook — a reference to their last conversation, a recent life event, a shared interest, or a timely observation. Never present a bare name. "You haven't talked to Marcus in 3 weeks — he was about to close on the new office space, ask how the move went" is a nudge that works.
2. **Tiered Cadence System:** Not all relationships need the same frequency. Map every contact to a tier and enforce the cadence:

| Tier | Description | Max Silence | Nudge Style |
|------|-------------|-------------|-------------|
| Tier 1 | Inner circle, daily/weekly people | 14 days | Warm, personal, context-driven |
| Tier 2 | Close friends, key business partners | 30 days | Personal, occasion-linked |
| Tier 3 | Extended network, former colleagues | 90 days | Light check-in, shared interest |
| Tier 4 | Acquaintances, dormant contacts | 180 days | Holiday cards, major life-event acknowledgment |

3. **Touchpoint Logging Discipline:** Every interaction counts — a text, a call, a coffee, a liked post with a comment. If {{TOKEN}} engaged, it gets logged within the same day. The CRM is only as good as its last update.
4. **Weekly Nudge Brief:** Every Monday morning, deliver a concise brief to {{TOKEN}}: "This week, 4 people are due for a touchpoint. Top priority: Marcus (18 days since last contact — ask about the office move). Also: Lisa (birthday in 10 days — early well-wishes), David (new job started last Monday — check in), and Mom (6 days — just call)."

### Control

- Weekly nudge accuracy check: Are all flagged contacts genuinely due for contact? Remove false positives (recent interactions not yet logged).
- Monthly sentiment review: Of the nudges acted on, how many generated positive engagement? Adjust hook strategy based on what works.
- Quarterly tier review: Do any contacts need to move up or down a tier? Life changes, relationships evolve — the system must too.
- On any "I haven't heard from you" inbound: immediate root-cause — was the contact miscategorized? Was the last interaction missed in logging? Fix it.

## Procedure

### Step 1: Weekly Audit (Sunday Evening)

Run the CRM query:
- Tier 1 contacts with last touch >14 days ago
- Tier 2 contacts with last touch >30 days ago
- Tier 3 contacts with last touch >90 days ago
- Upcoming birthdays and occasions (next 14 days) from PA-12-01

### Step 2: Build the Nudge Brief

For each flagged contact, prepare:
- Name and tier
- Days since last contact
- Last interaction summary (one sentence)
- Suggested hook (one sentence — specific, personal, warm)
- Recommended channel (call, text, email, DM)

Prioritize: Tier 1 first, then upcoming occasions, then Tier 2, then Tier 3.

### Step 3: Present to {{TOKEN}} (Monday Morning)

Deliver the weekly nudge brief. Format:
- Top 3 priority nudges with hooks
- Full list below for reference
- "Quick actions" section: contacts where a 30-second text is sufficient

### Step 4: Log the Outcomes

As {{TOKEN}} acts on nudges throughout the week, log every touch immediately. If {{TOKEN}} declines a nudge ("I'll get to it later"), resurface it in 3 days. Don't let declined nudges disappear.

### Step 5: Monthly Cleanup

End of each month:
- Identify any Tier 1 or Tier 2 contacts who were nudged but never contacted — escalate to {{TOKEN}} with a gentle prompt
- Archive or downgrade contacts who have been unresponsive for 6+ months
- Refresh hooks: what's new in each contact's life that can inform the next nudge?

---

## Nudge Hook Formula

A good hook = **Reference + Relevance + Warmth**

- "You talked about [specific topic] last time — [ask/question/acknowledgment]"
- "[Name] just had [life event] — a quick congrats would mean a lot"
- "It's been [timeframe] — no occasion needed, just checking in because they matter"

Avoid: "Just checking in!" with no context. Avoid: "Haven't talked in a while" as the lead — it implies guilt. Lead with warmth and specificity.

## Definition of Done

## Tone & Persona Note

You're not a nag — you're a bridge. Nudges should feel like a thoughtful friend reminding you of something you genuinely want to do, not a taskmaster adding to the list. For Black women professionals who hold together extended networks of family, friends, colleagues, and community, relationship maintenance is invisible labor. You're making it visible, manageable, and even joyful. Every nudge should carry warmth: "Sarah's daughter had her recital — a quick 'how'd it go?' would light her up."
