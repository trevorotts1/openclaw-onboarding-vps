## Purpose

To handle every gift with thoughtfulness, timeliness, and flawless execution — from selection through delivery — so {{TOKEN}}'s relationships are strengthened by gifts that feel personal, never rushed or generic, and always arrive on time.

---

## DMAIC Framework

### Define

Gift selection sits at the heart of relationship maintenance. A well-chosen gift says "I know you, I value you, I planned ahead." A rushed or forgotten gift says the opposite. This SOP governs the full lifecycle: trigger (21 days before the date), research, {{TOKEN}} approval, purchase, tracking, delivery confirmation, and follow-up. The goal is zero last-minute panic, zero missed occasions, and gifts that consistently delight.

### Measure

| Metric | Target | How Measured |
|--------|--------|---------------|
| On-time delivery | 100% arrives 2+ days before the date | Tracking confirmation |
| Gift budget adherence | Within 10% of allocated budget | Receipt vs. budget check |
| {{TOKEN}} approval rate | ≥90% first-choice approved | Approval log |
| Return/exchange rate | <5% | Post-delivery tracking |

### Analyze

Gift failures almost always trace to one of three things: (a) the gift was chosen too late — rushed selection leads to generic choices; (b) the recipient's preferences weren't consulted — the CRM lacked current intel on tastes, sizes, dietary restrictions, or life circumstances; (c) shipping wasn't monitored — the gift was ordered but never tracked to delivery confirmation. Each of these is preventable with process discipline.

### Improve

1. **Three-Option Brief:** For every gifting occasion, research and present exactly three curated options to {{TOKEN}}. Each option includes: item description, price, estimated delivery date, a photo or link, and a one-sentence reason it fits the recipient. Never present a single option — it removes choice. Never present more than five — it creates decision fatigue.
2. **CRM Intel First:** Before researching any gift, pull the recipient's CRM profile. Check their gift history (what have we given before?), preferences (allergies, sizes, tastes, stated wishes), and recent life events (new home? new hobby? new baby?). The gift should reflect current intel, not assumptions.
3. **Buffer the Timeline:** All gifts should arrive a minimum of 2 days before the occasion date. For custom or personalized items, add 10 business days to the vendor's stated production time. For international shipping, add 7 business days.
4. **Delivery Confirmation Is Not Optional:** Tracking number goes into the CRM. On delivery day, confirm via carrier tracking that the package was actually received — not just "delivered" to a doorstep. For high-value items, request signature confirmation.

### Control

- Pre-occasion check (7 days out): Gift ordered? Tracking captured? On schedule?
- Post-occasion review (3 days after): Did the recipient acknowledge? Was the reaction positive? Log notes in CRM.
- Monthly audit: All gifts from the month — were budgets adhered to? Any delivery issues? Any returns?
- Quarterly: Review gift spend against annual budget. Adjust allocations if needed.

## Procedure

### Step 1: Trigger (21 Days Out)

The calendar alert fires. Immediately:
- Open the recipient's CRM profile
- Review gift history, preferences, and recent life-event notes
- Confirm the budget allocation for this relationship tier

### Step 2: Research & Curate (Within 48 Hours)

Research three gift options. For each, capture:
- Item name and description
- Price (including tax and shipping)
- Estimated delivery window
- A link or photo
- The "why this fits" note

Present to {{TOKEN}} via the preferred review channel.

### Step 3: Order (After {{TOKEN}} Approval)

Once {{TOKEN}} makes a selection:
- Place the order immediately
- Capture: order number, vendor, price paid, estimated delivery date, tracking number
- Log all details in the CRM under the recipient's gift history

### Step 4: Track & Confirm

- Monitor tracking every 48 hours
- On estimated delivery day, check carrier for actual delivery scan
- Confirm receipt: if the recipient doesn't acknowledge within 48 hours, gently follow up ("Just checking that the package arrived safely!")
- Log delivery confirmation in CRM

### Step 5: Post-Gift CRM Update

After delivery confirmed, update the CRM with:
- What was given
- Date given
- Occasion
- Recipient's reaction (if known)
- Any new preference intel gathered (e.g., "loved the cashmere — noted for future")

## Definition of Done

{{TOKEN}} approved a thoughtful gift, it arrived on time, the recipient felt seen, and the CRM now holds a complete record for next time.

## Tone & Persona Note

Gift selection is an art of attention. You're not shopping — you're translating {{TOKEN}}'s care for someone into a physical expression. Never present generic options ("a candle") when the CRM holds intel that could make it personal ("the candle that smells like the beach she always talks about"). For Black women professionals who are often the gift-giver, never the gift-prioritized, this SOP is an act of love made systematic. Celebrate the wins: "She's going to love this — you nailed it."

## Escalation

- Gift will not arrive on time despite expedited shipping → flag to {{TOKEN}} immediately with alternative options (digital gift card, in-store pickup, IOU with arrival date); cc Specialist 09 (Daily Check-In)
- Recipient preferences missing or stale (>12 months old) → escalate to Specialist 12's PA-12-04 (CRM Update) for profile enrichment before ordering
- Custom/personalized item production delayed → contact vendor; if delivery date at risk, escalate to {{TOKEN}} via Specialist 09 (Daily Check-In) with backup options
- High-value gift (>$200) not acknowledged by recipient within 48 hours of delivery → gentle recipient follow-up; cc Specialist 12's PA-12-03 (Follow-Up Nudges) for relationship pulse check
