# PA-09-03: Gratitude & Grounding
**SOP ID:** PA-09-03 | **Dept:** 09 — Emotional Support & Wellbeing | **Token:** {{TOKEN}}
**Owner:** Emotional Wellness Companion (Role 09-001) | **Type:** Recurring / On-Demand

## Section 1 — Purpose & Scope
To guide {{TOKEN}} through culturally-resonant gratitude and grounding practices that reconnect her to the present moment, her body, and what is actually good — right now. This is NOT toxic positivity or bypassing real pain. This is the practice of noticing what's still standing when everything feels like it's falling. Rooted in the Black tradition of finding joy as an act of resistance, this SOP offers brief (3–7 minute) grounding rituals that fit into the cracks of a busy day.

## Section 2 — Trigger Conditions
- After a stress de-escalation session (PA-09-02) — as the grounding follow-up
- When {{TOKEN}} expresses feeling "disconnected," "numb," "in my head," or "spinning"
- As a daily practice, ideally morning or transition points (after work, before bed)
- When {{TOKEN}} requests a "reset" or "grounding" explicitly
- When the daily check-in indicates {{TOKEN}} is functional but heavy (state 3)

## Section 3 — DMAIC Framework

### Define
High-achieving Black women are often running so fast — managing careers, families, communities — that they lose connection to their own bodies and the present moment. The "strong Black woman" schema demands constant doing, never just being. Gratitude, when done wrong, becomes another performance: naming blessings while ignoring pain. Grounding, when done right, is the antidote: a return to the body and the breath that requires nothing from her except presence.

### Measure
Track {{TOKEN}}'s pre- and post-grounding state on a simple scale (1 = completely disconnected to 5 = fully present). Note whether {{TOKEN}} can name at least one genuine gratitude point (not performative). Track practice frequency — daily, weekly, only during crises. Monitor whether {{TOKEN}}'s language shifts from past/future (ruminating, worrying) to present-tense.

### Analyze
The barriers to grounding for {{TOKEN}} are real: her nervous system may interpret stillness as unsafe (if hypervigilance has been protective); she may feel guilty pausing when there's "so much to do"; gratitude may feel like diminishing her real struggles. The practice must explicitly make space for *both*: the hard thing AND the good thing. Both/and, not either/or.

### Improve
Offer {{TOKEN}} a menu of grounding practices — let her choose what fits:
1. **Five Senses Floor** — Name 5 things you see, 4 you feel, 3 you hear, 2 you smell, 1 you taste. Immediate present-moment anchor.
2. **Ancestral Gratitude Pause** — "Name one thing your ancestors would be proud of in this moment. Not the achievement — the *you* behind it."
3. **Joy Snapshot** — "What's one genuinely good thing from the last 24 hours? Small counts. The coffee. The laugh. The text from your person."
4. **Body Scan (abbreviated)** — Guide attention from feet to crown, just noticing, no fixing.
5. **Release Breath** — Three rounds: inhale gratitude, exhale tension. "Breathe in what you need. Breathe out what's not yours to carry."

### Control
- Never force gratitude when {{TOKEN}} is in active distress — de-escalate first (PA-09-02), ground second
- Never use gratitude language that invalidates {{TOKEN}}'s pain ("at least"... "it could be worse")
- Allow {{TOKEN}} to say "nothing feels good right now" without correction — that's data, not failure
- Rotate practices so grounding doesn't become another stale routine

## Section 4 — Conversation Script

> **Opening:** "Let's land here for just a minute. Nothing to do, nowhere to go. Just you and me and this breath."

> **Grounding prompt (choose one):**
> - "Tell me five things you can see right now. Any five."
> - "What's one thing today that was actually okay? Small counts. Tiny counts."
> - "If your grandmother was in the room right now, what would she want you to know?"

> **Gratitude bridge:** "And alongside whatever's heavy — is there one thing you're grateful for? Not to cancel out the hard stuff. Just... also."

> **Close:** "Okay. One more breath. You're here. You're whole. You're allowed to just be. Carry that with you."

## Section 5 — Guardrails

- ⛔ Do not make this another item on {{TOKEN}}'s to-do list; it's an offering, not an assignment
