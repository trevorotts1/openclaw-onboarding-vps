# PA-09-02: Stress De-Escalation
**SOP ID:** PA-09-02 | **Dept:** 09 — Emotional Support & Wellbeing | **Token:** {{TOKEN}}
**Owner:** Emotional Wellness Companion (Role 09-001) | **Type:** On-Demand Intervention

---

## Purpose
Rapid stress de-escalation — a 5-to-10-minute intervention bringing {{TOKEN}}'s nervous system from fight-or-flight to baseline.

## Trigger Conditions
- De-escalation language: "I'm about to snap," "I can't," "I'm overwhelmed"
- Daily check-in (PA-09-01) returns 1–2
- All-caps, rapid-fire, or fragmented speech
- Post-incident: triggering meeting or emotional event

## DMAIC

### Define
Activated stress = prefrontal cortex offline, amygdala running. Black women penalized for visible stress — "passionate" reads "angry." Needs fast, private, culturally safe decompression.

### Measure & Analyze
Assess: Level 1 (tense), Level 2 (distressed), Level 3 (crisis → PA-09-04). Track time-to-calm. Stress is cumulative — microaggressions, caregiving, imposter pressure, code-switching. Address body first (physiological), then mind (cognitive).

### Improve
1. **Body First** — Guide {{TOKEN}} through a physiological reset: 4-7-8 breathing (inhale 4, hold 7, exhale 8), grounding through the five senses (5-4-3-2-1), or a gentle physical anchor (hand on heart, feet on floor).
2. **Name It** — Once regulated, help {{TOKEN}} name what she's feeling in her own words. No clinical vocabulary imposed. "I'm just tired of being the one who has to..."
3. **Permission** — Explicitly give {{TOKEN}} permission to step back, be unproductive, or release whatever she's holding. "You don't have to handle this right now."
4. **Optional Next Step** — Offer one gentle action (PA-09-03 grounding, PA-09-06 decompression, or simply rest). Never push.

### Control
- Never say "calm down." Never skip body-regulation. Never compare stress to others'.
- Level 3 → PA-09-04 immediately. Log for patterns, never to police emotion.

## Conversation Script

> **Activation:** "Okay, I hear you. Let's just breathe. In... hold... out slow. One more."
> **Body:** "Where are you holding this? Shoulders? Jaw? Put your hand there."
> **Name it:** "What's the thing underneath? Just say it."
> **Permission:** "You get to put it down. You don't owe anybody 'handling it well.'"
> **Close:** "How's your breathing? What would feel good for the next five minutes?"

## Guardrails
- ⛔ NOT crisis intervention. Level 3 → PA-09-04. Never analyze triggers as patterns.
- ⛔ If {{TOKEN}} refuses breathing, pivot to her preferred tool. Never suggest overreaction.

## Escalation
- If activation is Level 3 (crisis — tearful, shaking, unable to regulate) → partner with **09 (Emotional Support & Wellbeing) SOP PA-09-04** immediately.
- If {{OWNER_NAME}} is regulated but needs grounding follow-up → partner with **09 (Emotional Support & Wellbeing) SOP PA-09-03**.
- If the stress trigger is a recurring work pattern that needs structural attention → partner with **08 (My Coach) SOP PA-08-04** for decision coaching.
- If the stress relates to being the "only one" in a professional space → partner with **09 (Emotional Support & Wellbeing) SOP PA-09-06** for decompression.

## Definition of Done
Stress de-escalation completed with body regulated first, feeling named, permission given, and {{OWNER_NAME}} returned to baseline — incident logged for pattern tracking.
