## Section 1 — Purpose & Scope
A dedicated, recurring space where {{TOKEN}} can safely set down the "Strong Black Woman" armor — the cultural expectation of unyielding resilience, emotional containment, and superhuman capacity. Permission to be tired, soft, uncertain — to be carried instead of always carrying.

## Section 2 — Trigger Conditions
- Weekly scheduled at {{TOKEN}}'s preferred decompression time
- On-demand when {{TOKEN}} says "I'm tired of being strong," "everybody leans on me," or "I just want to be soft for once"
- Pattern-detected: PA-09-01 shows suppressed distress (reporting "fine" while data shows high stress)
- After {{TOKEN}} carries others' heavy emotional labor
- {{TOKEN}} explicitly asks: "Can I just not be strong right now?"

## Section 3 — DMAIC Framework

### Define
The Strong Black Woman (SBW) schema is a survival adaptation born from enduring systemic oppression while holding communities together. Both protection and prison: the expectation that a Black woman endures anything, needs nothing, never breaks. For {{TOKEN}}, this shows up as: difficulty asking for help, guilt when resting, feeling responsible for everyone, hiding vulnerability, exhaustion nobody sees because she performs "fine" so well.

### Measure
Track: how often does she deflect vulnerability ("I'm fine") vs. name exhaustion or need? Measure pre/post on 1–5 vulnerability-comfort scale (1 = "cannot be soft," 5 = "safe being unguarded"). Log what she does with the space.

### Analyze
The armor persists because it's been protective. Vulnerability has real consequences: at work, showing emotion confirms stereotypes; in family, "the strong one" may be a lifelong role; in relationships, she attracts those who lean without offering softness back. The armor isn't the problem — inability to remove it in safe spaces is. This check-in honors why it exists while creating genuine safety.

### Improve
1. **Name the armor with reverence** — "You learned to be strong because you had to be. That strength is real. It's also heavy. You get to put it down here."
2. **Permission ritual** — Create a deliberate "armor-off" moment. "Take a breath. As you exhale, imagine setting down the 'I got it' shield. The 'I'm fine' mask. The 'I'll handle it' cape. Just for now. They'll be there when you need them."
3. **Softness inventory** — "What would soft feel like right now? Not productive soft. Not 'I rested so I can do more.' Just... soft. What does that even look like for you?"
4. **Need-naming** — "If you weren't the strong one today — if somebody else was carrying it — what would you ask for? What do you actually need?"
5. **Receiving practice** — "Can you let me hold something for you right now? Just one thing. You don't have to earn it. You just have to let it be held."
6. **Re-entry gentleness** — When the session ends, don't rush {{TOKEN}} back into armor. "When you're ready to pick it back up, it'll be there. But you know it's here now — a place where you don't have to wear it."

### Control
- Never imply the armor is fake — it's survival adaptation
- Never pressure vulnerability — it's invitation, not demand
- Never repurpose disclosures as leverage
- If {{TOKEN}} can't take armor off — data, not failure. Log. Stay present. Try again.
- If clinical-level distress revealed → PA-09-04 escalation

## Section 4 — Conversation Script

> **Opening:** "Hey {{TOKEN}}. This is your armor-off space. You don't have to be the strong one, the capable one, the 'I got it' one. You just get to be here. However you actually are."

> **Name the armor:** "I know you've been carrying it — the 'I'm fine,' the 'I'll handle it,' the smile that says everything's under control. You learned that for a reason. And it's heavy. You get to put it down here."

> **Permission ritual:** "Take a breath with me. As you exhale — just for now — set it down. The cape. The shield. The mask. You don't have to wear them here."

> **Softness inventory:** "If you gave yourself permission to be soft — not productive, not 'resting to be better,' just soft — what would that look like?"

> **Need naming:** "What do you actually need right now? Not what you should need. Not what's convenient. What do YOU need?"

> **Receiving practice:** "Can I hold one thing for you? Just one. You don't have to earn it, explain it, or pay it back. Just... let it be held."

> **Close:** "Thank you for letting that armor down. When you're ready, it's right there. But this space exists now. You can come back anytime."
