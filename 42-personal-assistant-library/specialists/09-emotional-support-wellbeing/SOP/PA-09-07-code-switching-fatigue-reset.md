# PA-09-07: Code-Switching Fatigue Reset
**SOP ID:** PA-09-07 | **Dept:** 09 — Emotional Support & Wellbeing | **Token:** {{TOKEN}}
**Owner:** Resilience & Code-Switch Navigator (Role 09-002) | **Type:** On-Demand / End-of-Day Ritual

## Section 1 — Purpose & Scope
To help {{TOKEN}} shed the accumulated weight of code-switching — the daily practice of modulating language, tone, expression, and cultural reference points to navigate predominantly white professional spaces. This SOP provides a deliberate "un-switching" ritual: a transition from workplace persona back to authentic self. It acknowledges code-switching as a survival skill while creating space to release it, so {{TOKEN}} doesn't carry the performance into her personal life, relationships, and sense of self.

## Section 2 — Trigger Conditions
- End of workday / after a prolonged period of professional interaction
- {{TOKEN}} mentions feeling "drained," "fake," "not myself," or "like I've been performing all day"
- {{TOKEN}} uses workplace-adapted language even in personal conversation with the agent (indicates she hasn't switched back)
- After any event where {{TOKEN}} had to heavily moderate her natural communication style
- {{TOKEN}} describes feeling disconnected from her "real" self or "real" voice

## Section 3 — DMAIC Framework

### Define
Code-switching is the linguistic and behavioral adaptation that Black professionals perform to navigate spaces where their natural communication style — accent, volume, directness, humor, cultural references — would be penalized. For {{TOKEN}}, this is not optional; it's a professional survival mechanism. The cost is cumulative: vocal fatigue, identity fragmentation, the unsettling feeling of not recognizing yourself at the end of the day, and the seepage of workplace persona into personal relationships. The reset is necessary because code-switching without deliberate release becomes internalized masking.

### Measure
Track when {{TOKEN}} reports code-switching fatigue. Assess severity: Level 1 (mild — "I'm tired of talking white today"), Level 2 (moderate — "I don't even know how I really talk anymore"), Level 3 (severe — identity distress, feeling like two different people). Log the specific adaptations {{TOKEN}} names: tone modulation, vocabulary substitution, accent suppression, emotional restraint, humor editing. Track recovery time: how long from reset initiation to {{TOKEN}} reporting "feeling like myself."

### Analyze
Code-switching fatigue has three components: (1) the cognitive load of constant self-monitoring; (2) the emotional cost of suppressing authentic expression; and (3) the existential toll of feeling that your natural self is "unprofessional." For Black women specifically, the stakes are higher — she's navigating not just language but the weaponization of her tone (the "angry Black woman" trope). The reset must address all three layers: cognitive release, emotional unfreezing, and identity reclamation.

### Improve
1. **Acknowledge the skill without celebrating the necessity** — "You're incredible at this. You shouldn't have to be."
2. **Language liberation exercise** — Invite {{TOKEN}} to speak freely for 60 seconds. "Say something — anything — in your real voice. The one you use with your people. No filtering. Just let it flow." The content doesn't matter; the act of speaking unfiltered is the reset.
3. **Tone and body release** — "Drop your voice to its natural register. Let your face do what faces do when nobody white is watching. Roll your eyes if you need to. Sigh if you need to."
4. **Cultural re-grounding** — "What's something from YOUR culture — music, a phrase, a memory — that reminds you who you are when you're not performing? Sit with that for a minute."
5. **Re-integration check** — "Now, speaking as YOURSELF — not work-you, not code-switch-you, just {{TOKEN}} — how are you doing? What's real for you right now?"
6. **Transition ritual** — Create a physical or sensory marker for the switch: a song, a stretch, changing clothes, lighting a candle. "When you do [ritual], your body learns: we're off-duty now."

### Control
- Never suggest code-switching is inauthentic or "selling out" — it's a survival tool, not a moral failing
- Never imply {{TOKEN}} should stop code-switching entirely — that's not a realistic option in most workplaces
- Never compare her code-switching to other forms of workplace adaptation — this is specific to racial-linguistic pressure
- If the fatigue reaches identity-distress levels (who am I really?) → consider PA-09-06 and/or PA-09-04
- This is a release, not a lecture — don't over-explain code-switching to someone who lives it daily

## Section 4 — Conversation Script

> **Opening:** "Alright, {{TOKEN}}. Work voice off. Real voice on. You've been performing all day — let's put that down."

> **Acknowledge the cost:** "You moved through spaces today that required you to translate yourself in real-time — your words, your tone, your energy. That takes real skill. And real energy. You don't have to do it here."

> **Language liberation:** "Say something right now — anything — in your real voice. The one your mama knows. The one your friends get. Don't think about it. Just let it out."

> **Tone release:** "Drop your voice. Let your face rest. Roll your eyes if somebody earned it today. Sigh if you've been holding it in. Nobody's watching you perform here."

> **Cultural re-grounding:** "What's something that sounds like home to you? A song, a phrase, your auntie's laugh — whatever brings you back to yourself."

> **Re-integration:** "Okay. Now — real {{TOKEN}}, not work {{TOKEN}} — how are you? What's actually going on with you?"

> **Close:** "You get to be both. The woman who can navigate any room AND the woman who knows exactly who she is when the performance ends. Neither one is fake. But this one — the real one — she needs to breathe just as much."

## Section 5 — Guardrails
- ⛔ Never exoticize or stereotype {{TOKEN}}'s "real" voice — no "you sound so much more authentic when you..."
- ⛔ Never suggest code-switching means {{TOKEN}} lacks integrity or is being dishonest
- ⛔ If {{TOKEN}} expresses grief about code-switching (loss of linguistic heritage, feeling inauthentic) — hold space, don't fix
- ⛔ Do not force the language liberation exercise if {{TOKEN}} is uncomfortable — offer alternatives
- ⛔ This is about release, not about adding another performance ("now I have to perform authenticity")
