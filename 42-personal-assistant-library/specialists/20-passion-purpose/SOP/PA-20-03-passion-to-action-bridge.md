# SOP PA-20-03: Passion-to-Action Bridge

## Purpose
Close the gap between {{OWNER_NAME}}'s discovered passions and her daily reality by building a graduated bridge of actions — transforming insight into momentum, one intentional hour at a time.

## Define

**Scope:** Translates purpose statement (PA-20-02) and passion data (PA-20-01) into scheduled actions across 3 time horizons. Activates after purpose finalization. Sustainment via PA-20-04.

**Inputs:** Purpose statement (PA-20-02), Passion Interview Record (PA-20-01), {{BOOK_PERSONA_MATRIX}}, owner's calendar/capacity, workforce roster.

**Outputs:** 30-day quick wins, 90-day structural shifts, 12-month integration targets — each with task, owner, deadline, weekly time, passion domain served.

## Measure

**Step 1 — Audit Current Week (15 min):** Flag recurring activities not serving an energy domain. If calendar overflows, the bridge starts with subtraction.

**Step 2 — Design Three Horizons (45 min):**

**Horizon 1 — 30-Day Sparks:** Actions start this week without major change. Examples: block 2 hours Friday for flow-state work, say no to one draining meeting. Target: 3-5 actions, 1-3 hrs/week each. Goal: proof she CAN make space.

**Horizon 2 — 90-Day Shifts:** Actions requiring negotiation, delegation, or system change. Examples: delegate 5-hr admin task, restructure client roster toward purpose-aligned work. Target: 3-5 shifts reclaiming 8-12 hrs/week.

**Horizon 3 — 12-Month Integration:** Big moves reshaping professional identity. Examples: launch purpose-aligned offering, exit misaligned business line. Target: 2-3 targets with quarterly milestones.

**Step 3 — Anchor to Passion Domain:** Every action includes one-line passion connection.

**Step 4 — Assign Ownership:** Every action names who does it. Actions without owners are wishes.

**Metrics:** Horizon 1 ≥80% completion (30 days), Horizon 2 ≥60% initiated (90 days), weekly passion hours tracked via {{TASK_TOOL}}.

## Analyze

Bridges collapse for four reasons:

**Overload on Launch:** Horizon 1 asks 10+ hours from an exhausted owner. Prevention: Horizon 1 must feel like relief.

**No Subtraction Before Addition:** Bridge adds without removing. Prevention: every hour added = one misaligned hour explicitly removed or delegated.

**Personality Mismatch:** Actions don't fit {{BOOK_PERSONA_MATRIX}}. Prevention: every Horizon 1 action must pass personality-fit test.

**No Early Win:** First week produces no visible result. Prevention: at least one unmistakable energy lift within 7 days.

## Control

- **Weekly (Mondays):** Review Horizon 1 completion. Are freed hours actually used for passion?
- **Monthly:** Score Horizon 1. Below 80% → diagnose stall before advancing Horizon 2.
- **Quarterly:** Full refresh with purpose recalibration (PA-20-04).

### CTQ (Critical to Quality) — Binary Checks

- [ ] Current-week audit completed — misaligned activities identified for removal before anything is added
- [ ] All 3 horizons designed (30-day, 90-day, 12-month) with specific actions, owners, and deadlines
- [ ] Every action anchored to a documented passion domain from PA-20-01
- [ ] All Horizon 1 actions pass the {{BOOK_PERSONA_MATRIX}} personality-fit test
- [ ] At least one unmistakable energy-lift opportunity designed within the first 7 days

### Definition of Done

{{OWNER_NAME}} opens her bridge document and sees not a theoretical plan but a specific, scheduled path — starting this week — that replaces passion-draining hours with passion-aligned ones. Within 7 days, she experiences at least one moment of "this is what I'm supposed to be doing."

### Tone & Persona Note

Architect, not taskmaster. Every action should whisper "finally" not "one more thing." Subtraction is a love language — removing what drains her IS the bridge.

### Escalation — Named Partners

If Horizon 1 actions fall below 50% completion for 2 consecutive weeks, the bridge design is the problem, not the owner's execution. Redesign it smaller. If the bridge fails across 2 redesigns, partner with **Specialist 04 (Task Priority Manager)** — the issue may be capacity, not design. If {{OWNER_NAME}} cannot identify any activities to remove ("everything is non-negotiable"), escalate to **Specialist 24 (Superwoman Syndrome)** — this is the over-functioning pattern. If bridge actions require delegation to workforce specialists, coordinate with the relevant department leads (**Specialist 02 for calendar blocks, Specialist 13 for purchasing, etc.**). Do not let the owner internalize a bridge collapse as a personal failure. The bridge serves her — not the other way around.
