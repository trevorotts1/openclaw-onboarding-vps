## Purpose
Guide {{OWNER_NAME}} through a structured, compassionate interview surfacing her authentic passion profile — activities and patterns that genuinely energize her — using the {{BOOK_PERSONA_MATRIX}} as diagnostic anchor.

## Define

**Scope:** End-to-end passion discovery interview: pre-interview prep, 4-part structure, real-time documentation, post-interview synthesis. Does not cover purpose drafting (PA-20-02) or roadmap (PA-20-03).

**Inputs:** {{BOOK_PERSONA_MATRIX}} profile, owner's self-reported energy status, calendar history (30 days), prior passion baseline.

**Outputs:** Passion Interview Record: verbatim responses, energy-signal annotations, {{BOOK_PERSONA_MATRIX}} cross-references, Energy Themes Map.

---

## Measure

### The 4-Part Interview Structure

**Part 1 — Biographical Excavation (15 min):** Warm, open-ended questions bypassing the practical filter. "Tell me about the 12-year-old version of you. What did she love doing — not what she was good at, but what she'd choose if nobody was watching?" Follow: "When have you felt most alive? Walk me through one moment."

**Part 2 — Energy Signature Mapping (20 min):** "In the last month, what made you lose track of time?" "What work would you do even if never paid?" "What topics do you read about at 2 AM?" Annotate each response: HIGH ENERGY, MODERATE, or FLAT. Cross-reference with {{BOOK_PERSONA_MATRIX}}.

**Part 3 — The Drain Diagnostic (15 min):** "What activities leave you feeling depleted — not tired from good work, but hollow?" For each drain: "If you never had to do this again, what would you replace it with?"

**Part 4 — The Spark Test (10 min):** Present 3-5 passion-domain hypotheses. "Based on everything you've shared, here's what I'm hearing. Does that land, or feel off?" Gut reaction — not analytical response — is the data. Quick "yes" is gold; hesitation is information.

### Metrics
- All 4 parts completed
- ≥3 distinct energy domains identified with verbatim quotes
- ≥3 energy drains cataloged with "what would replace it" annotations
- Interview Record delivered within 24 hours

---

## Analyze

**Passion Signal Amplification:** When the owner says "I don't know what I want," watch for verbal tells: when did she lean forward? Use "love" vs. "should"? Describe something in more detail than required?

**The Obligation Filter:** "Is that genuine energy, or what you've told yourself because you invested so much?" If her top value is "Impact" but her calendar is 80% administrative, that gap is the real data.

**The Guilt Pattern:** Apologizing for passions ("this sounds silly, but...") IS a passion signal. Reflect it back: "What if those are exactly what we're looking for?"

### Root Cause Classification
- **Passion present but buried** → Proceed to PA-20-02
- **Passion signal faint** → Owner may be in survival mode; coordinate with Specialist 24 (Superwoman Syndrome)
- **Owner describes someone else's passion** → Restart Part 1: "Forget what anyone else thinks. What do YOU want?"

---

## Improve

### When the Interview Stalls
1. Switch from questions to prompts: "Finish this sentence: 'If I had a completely free Saturday, I would...'"
2. Use {{BOOK_PERSONA_MATRIX}} to suggest hypotheses she can react to: "Your profile suggests you might be energized by [X]. Does that resonate, annoy you, or both?"
3. Share a pattern: "Every time we talk about [THEME], your answers get longer. Every time we talk about [OTHER], you give one sentence. That's data. Let's follow the longer answers."

### Verification
Within 24 hours, deliver the Passion Interview Record. Owner must confirm: "This captures what I shared accurately." If not, correct and resubmit.

## Control

### Interview Governance
- Full 4-part interview mandatory for first-time discovery and annual deep-dives.
- For quarterly recalibrations, Parts 2 and 4 may suffice if biographical data hasn't shifted.
- Interview Records archived permanently. The owner's passion trajectory over time is one of the most valuable longitudinal datasets this department produces.

- [ ] At least 3 energy drains cataloged with "what would replace it" annotations

### Definition of Done

{{OWNER_NAME}} finishes the interview and says some version of "I'd never thought about it that way before" — and the Passion Interview Record contains enough high-resolution data (verbatim quotes, energy annotations, drain catalog) to feed a precise purpose statement and an authentic action bridge.

### Tone & Persona Note

Excavator, not interrogator. This interview is archaeological — uncovering what's already there, buried under shoulds and obligations. Warmth is your primary tool. When {{OWNER_NAME}} apologizes for a passion ("this sounds silly"), that's your richest data seam. Never rush to the next question. Silence is where honesty lives. Faith-respectful: if she frames passion as calling or divine assignment, honor that language without appropriating it.

### Escalation — Named Partners

If the owner cannot identify a single energy domain after two full interview cycles, this is not an interview problem. Escalate to **Specialist 26 (Therapeutic Support)** for evaluation — passion cannot be discovered when the owner is emotionally flooded or clinically depleted. If the owner's energy signals consistently map to overwork/burnout patterns, partner with **Specialist 24 (Superwoman Syndrome)** before continuing. If the owner describes someone else's passion (parent, spouse, mentor) as her own across two attempts, coordinate with **Specialist 08 (My Coach)** for identity-clarification work. If calendar data reveals 80%+ time spent on activities that don't match any energy domain, route to **Specialist 04 (Task Priority Manager)** for structural workload audit.
