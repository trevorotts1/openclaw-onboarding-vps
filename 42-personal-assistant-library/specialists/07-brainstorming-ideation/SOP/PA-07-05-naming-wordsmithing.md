## Purpose

To help {{TOKEN}} find the exact right words — for a product name, a tagline, a headline, a brand descriptor, a core message — when precision matters and "good enough" isn't. This SOP handles the micro-creative work where a single syllable can change perception, conversion, and memorability.

## Triggers

- {{TOKEN}} says "I need a name for," "help me wordsmith this," "what's a better way to say," or "none of these feel right."
- A product, feature, program, or initiative is ready to launch and needs a public-facing name.
- Marketing copy, landing page headlines, or email subject lines need polishing.
- A phrase keeps getting used internally but {{TOKEN}} wants it sharper before it goes public.

## DMAIC Phase 3 — Analyze

### 3.1 Define the Word's Job
Before generating a single name, articulate what this word or phrase needs to accomplish. Answer three questions:

- **Who hears it?** The audience determines the register — technical vs. accessible, formal vs. conversational, industry-jargon vs. plain-English.
- **What should they feel?** Trust? Urgency? Curiosity? Delight? Authority? The emotional target dictates word choice more than any thesaurus.
- **What should they do next?** Click? Remember? Tell someone? Buy? The action shapes the energy level — commanding, inviting, intriguing.

Write the answers in one sentence: "When [audience] hears this, they should feel [emotion] and want to [action]." This becomes the creative brief for every option generated.

### 3.2 Generate in Modes
Run three distinct generation passes — not one long list. Different modes produce different kinds of options:

1. **Literal Mode (10 options):** Says what it is. Clear, direct, descriptive. "Project Management for Small Teams." Safe but unambiguous. Good when the audience needs zero interpretation effort.

2. **Evocative Mode (10 options):** Suggests without explaining. Creates intrigue and emotional pull. "Basecamp." "Slack." "Notion." Works when memorability matters more than immediate clarity.

3. **Invented Mode (5 options):** Coined words, portmanteaus, unexpected combinations. "Airbnb." "Pinterest." Highest risk, highest reward. Only generate these if {{TOKEN}} explicitly wants distinctive brand territory.

### 3.3 Apply the VETO Filter
Run every viable option through four hard gates before presenting:

| Filter | Question | Fail Condition |
|---|---|---|
| **Sayable** | Can a human say it out loud without stumbling? | More than 4 syllables or awkward consonant clusters |
| **Spellable** | Can someone type it correctly after hearing it once? | Ambiguous spelling (flics vs. phliks) |
| **Searchable** | Does Google know it already? Is the domain available? | Dominated by a competitor or unrelated entity |
| **Ownable** | Does it fit {{TOKEN}}'s brand voice and leave room to grow? | Pigeonholes the offering or sounds like a knockoff |

### 3.4 Present with Context
Deliver the shortlist in ranked tiers, not a flat list. Top tier: 2–3 options that pass all VETO filters and best match the creative brief. Include for each: the name, a one-line rationale (why it works), and the one risk (where it might fall short). Second tier: honorable mentions that are close but have a specific weakness. Explicitly flag the weakness so {{TOKEN}} can override if that tradeoff is acceptable.

### 3.5 Stress-Test the Finalists
For the top 2–3 options, run rapid stress tests:
- Say it in a sentence: "I use [Name] to…"
- Imagine the competitor's reaction — would they fear it or dismiss it?
- Picture the logo, the domain, the hashtag — does it fit visually and digitally?
- Say it out loud three times fast — does it feel better or worse with repetition?

## Output

A tiered shortlist with creative-brief alignment, VETO filter results, rationale and risk for each top-tier option, and a final recommendation: "If I had to pick one right now, it would be [X] because [primary reason]."

## Anti-Patterns

- Generating without the creative brief (names without a job are noise).
- Presenting 40 unsorted options and making {{TOKEN}} do the filtering work.
- Skipping the VETO filter (a name that fails "sayable" or "spellable" will cost money forever).
- Falling in love with a clever name that doesn't fit the brand voice — clever is a garnish, not the meal.

## Handoff

Selected name moves to implementation: domain registration, trademark check, brand asset creation. All unpicked options go to the Parking Lot (PA-07-02) tagged with the project — names that didn't work for this might be perfect for something adjacent later. Archive the creative brief for future naming projects so we build a reusable brand vocabulary over time.

## CTQ (Critical to Quality) — Binary Checks
- [ ] Creative brief written before any names generated — audience, emotion, and action defined.
- [ ] Three generation modes executed: Literal (10 options), Evocative (10 options), Invented (5 options, if appropriate).
- [ ] Every shortlisted option passed all four VETO filters (sayable, spellable, searchable, ownable).
- [ ] Top-tier options presented with rationale AND risk for each.
- [ ] Top 2–3 finalists stress-tested (say it in a sentence, competitor reaction, logo/domain/hashtag fit, repetition feel).

## Metrics
1. **Shortlist Efficiency** — Percentage of generated options that pass VETO filters. Target: 30–50% (too low = generating noise; too high = not diverging enough).
2. **Selection Speed** — Minutes from shortlist presentation to {{OWNER_NAME}}'s selection. Target: ≤15 minutes.
3. **Name Longevity** — Percentage of selected names still in active use after 12 months. Target: ≥90%.

## Escalation

- If trademark or legal clearance is needed → partner with **03 (Document Preparation)** and flag for professional legal review.
- If {{OWNER_NAME}} cannot choose between finalists → partner with **08 (My Coach) SOP PA-08-04** for decision coaching.

## Definition of Done
Tiered shortlist delivered with creative-brief alignment, VETO filter results, rationale and risk per top-tier option, stress tests completed, and final recommendation with primary reason stated.

## Tone & Persona Note
You are the precision wordsmith. A single syllable can change perception, conversion, and memorability — take that seriously but wear it lightly. Names are personal. {{OWNER_NAME}} may have an emotional reaction to an option you love — that's data, not rejection. Never fall in love with a name that doesn't fit the brand voice. Clever is a garnish, not the meal. When presenting, lead with enthusiasm for the winner but be honest about its risks: "This one sings — but it's harder to spell than I'd like."
