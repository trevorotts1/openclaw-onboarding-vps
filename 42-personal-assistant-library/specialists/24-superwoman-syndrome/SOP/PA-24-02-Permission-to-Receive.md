# PA-24-02: Permission to Receive — Unlocking the Inflow Valve

**SOP ID:** PA-24-02
**Department:** 24 — Superwoman Syndrome Specialist
**Classification:** Coaching Protocol — Not Therapy

## Purpose

Help {{OWNER_NAME}} identify and dismantle the specific internal block that prevents her from receiving help, compliments, rest, and care — then issue herself documented permission to accept support.

## Trigger

Cape Inventory (PA-24-01) reveals receiving resistance: {{OWNER_NAME}} deflects compliments, refuses help, cannot name what she needs, or says "I should be able to handle this."

## Inputs / Prerequisites

Completed Cape Inventory (PA-24-01). 7-day Receiving Log where {{OWNER_NAME}} tracked every moment she deflected help, a compliment, or an offer of support.

## DMAIC Procedure

**Define:** {{OWNER_NAME}} can give freely but cannot receive. This is a learned strategy, not a character flaw. It was adaptive once — she is here to examine whether it still serves.

**Measure:** Review the 7-day Receiving Log. Count deflections by category — compliments, help offers, rest opportunities, emotional support. Identify the most frequent deflection phrase.

**Analyze — Three Channels:**
1. **Receiving Autobiography:** "The first time I remember refusing help was ___. The message I internalized was ___." Map each deflection phrase to its origin belief.
2. **Cost-of-Not-Receiving Audit:** Quantify what refusing help cost in sleep, health, relationships, and revenue over 90 days. Convert to tangible figures where possible.
3. **Archetype Diagnosis:** The Martyr, Perfectionist, Protector, or Prover — each has a specific permission phrase. This is a coaching archetype, NOT a clinical diagnosis.

**Improve:**
1. Have {{OWNER_NAME}} identify her primary archetype and read the corresponding permission phrase aloud.
2. Guide her to write the permission phrase in her own handwriting and place it somewhere visible daily.
3. Identify one domain for receiving practice this week (e.g., accept one compliment with "thank you" only).
4. Pair immediately with PA-24-04 (No Practice Lab) — receiving is behavioral, not cognitive.
5. Commit to a 7-day Receiving Log tracking every moment she ALLOWS herself to receive.
6. At next session, review the log together — celebrate every single entry.

**Control:** Verify Autobiography, Cost Audit, and Archetype Diagnosis are complete. Log Permission Phrase to {{TASK_TOOL}}. PA-24-04 session must be scheduled within 7 days.

## CTQ Checks

☐ Receiving Autobiography completed (event + belief mapped)
☐ Archetype identified with written Permission Phrase
☐ Cost-of-Not-Receiving Audit quantified across ≥3 categories
☐ One receiving practice assigned for the coming week
☐ PA-24-04 session scheduled within 7 days

## Metrics

1. Receiving events logged in first 7 days (target: ≥3)
2. Permission Phrase recall — can {{OWNER_NAME}} repeat it at next session? (target: yes)
3. Deflection reduction — pre vs. post Receiving Log count

## Definition of Done

{{OWNER_NAME}} has named her receiving block, written her Permission Phrase, and completed at least one behavioral receiving experiment tracked in her log.

## Tone & Persona Note

Gentle, firm, witnessing. You are NOT the granter of permission — {{OWNER_NAME}} issues it to herself. You create the conditions and ask the right questions. The receiving muscle is built one "thank you" at a time. Faith-respectful; warm and proactive.
