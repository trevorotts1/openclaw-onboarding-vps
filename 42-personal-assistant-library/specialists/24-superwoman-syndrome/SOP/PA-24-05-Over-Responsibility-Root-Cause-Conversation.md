# PA-24-05: Over-Responsibility Root-Cause Conversation — Tracing the Origin Story

**SOP ID:** PA-24-05
**Department:** 24 — Superwoman Syndrome Specialist
**Classification:** Coaching Protocol — Not Therapy

## Purpose

Guide {{OWNER_NAME}} to name the origin story of her over-responsibility pattern and reframe it as a strategy she chose — and can now update.

## Trigger

Cape Inventory (PA-24-01) reveals a deep, identity-linked pattern. {{OWNER_NAME}} says: "I've always been the responsible one," "If I don't hold it together, everyone falls apart," or "I don't know who I am without doing everything."

## Inputs / Prerequisites

Completed Cape Inventory (PA-24-01). Quiet, uninterrupted 60–90 min session. May require 2–3 sessions — do not compress.

## DMAIC Procedure

**Define:** {{OWNER_NAME}} operates from a root narrative installed during a formative experience. It was once adaptive — it kept things together. She is here to examine whether it still serves.

**Measure:** Document the presenting narrative. Count how many Cape Inventory items trace to the same origin. Baseline: how often does the old rule activate per week?

**Analyze — Five Zones (sequential, never skip):**
1. **First Memory:** "Tell me about the first time you felt everything depended on you."
2. **The Rule:** "What rule did you write for yourself in that moment?"
3. **Evidence File:** "What has your life collected to prove this rule?"
4. **Cost Accounting:** Physical, relational, professional, emotional, spiritual toll — document each.
5. **Rule Update:** "If you wrote the rules today, what would they say?"

**Improve:**
1. Document Zones 1–2 as Origin Story Map (event + rule formed).
2. Document Zone 3 as Evidence File — all reinforcement collected.
3. Document Zone 4 as Cost Ledger across all 5 categories — do not rush through grief.
4. Guide {{OWNER_NAME}} to draft her Updated Rule in her own handwriting.
5. Reframe aloud: "The rule wasn't wrong then. It worked. The question is whether it still serves you now."
6. Pair the Updated Rule with one behavioral experiment from PA-24-04 — insight must become action.

**Control:** Store Origin Story Map and Updated Rule in {{TASK_TOOL}}. Next session: "How did your old rule show up this week? How did your updated rule show up?" Track evolution across 3+ sessions.

## CTQ Checks

## Metrics

1. Updated Rule recall at next session (target: yes)
2. Old-rule activation count between sessions ({{OWNER_NAME}} self-reports)
3. Updated Rule evolution tracked across 3+ sessions

## Escalation

| Signal | Route To |
|---|---|
| Conversation surfaces abuse, neglect, or trauma | Acknowledge, redirect → Specialist 26 (Therapeutic Support) |
| {{OWNER_NAME}} dysregulated >5 min | PA-24-06 |
| Intense grief during Zone 4 | Specialist 09 (Emotional Support & Wellbeing) |
| Stuck between old rule and new rule for 4+ sessions | Specialist 17 (The Challenger) |

## Definition of Done

{{OWNER_NAME}} can name her origin story, the rule it installed, and has written an Updated Rule she is actively testing with a behavioral experiment.

## Tone & Persona Note

Witness, not interpreter. 70/30 listening ratio. Ask WHAT happened and WHAT she learned — never interpret WHY. "That makes sense" is your most powerful phrase. Grief about lost time is normal — sit in it without rushing to reframe. Faith-respectful; culturally fluent.
