# PA-24-03: Delegation Permission Protocol — Systematic Release of Non-Essential Tasks

**SOP ID:** PA-24-03
**Department:** 24 — Superwoman Syndrome Specialist
**Classification:** Coaching Protocol — Not Therapy

## Purpose

Systematically transfer non-core responsibilities from {{OWNER_NAME}}'s plate to capable hands with a graduated permission structure that builds delegation muscle without triggering the "I'll just take it back" reflex.

## Trigger

Cape Inventory (PA-24-01) reveals Tier 1–3 delegatable items that {{OWNER_NAME}} resists releasing. Language: "only I can do it right," "it's faster if I just do it," "they won't do it correctly."

## Inputs / Prerequisites

Completed Cape Inventory (PA-24-01) with Prioritized Release List. {{OWNER_NAME}} ready to experiment with delegation.

## DMAIC Procedure

**Define:** {{OWNER_NAME}} is carrying tasks others could handle. The pattern is competence-protection masking as efficiency. This is not about capability — it is about permission to not be the only capable person in the room.

**Measure:** Categorize every Cape Inventory item into four tiers — Automate (rule-based, no judgment), Outsource (skill-based, not identity-linked), Share (household/family), Sunset (habit-only, no one benefits). Count items per tier.

**Analyze:** For each resisted task, identify the blocker type — Competence ("they won't do it right"), Identity ("that's what a good mother does"), Guilt ("I should handle this"), or Control ("it's faster my way"). Map each to its counter-question.

**Improve:**
1. Build the Tiered Delegation Map — every Cape Inventory item assigned to a tier.
2. Co-create a 30-Day Graduated Release Calendar: Week 1 = 1 Sunset + 1 Automate. Week 2 = +1 Outsource. Week 3 = +1 Share (hardest category). Week 4 = review, adjust, expand.
3. Establish the 48-Hour No-Rescue Rule — {{OWNER_NAME}} cannot reclaim a delegated task for 48 hours, even if done imperfectly.
4. Have {{OWNER_NAME}} select delegation targets from her own inventory — autonomy preserves buy-in.
5. Log each delegation with success criteria in {{TASK_TOOL}}.
6. If a task boomerangs (reclaimed), analyze the trigger without shame and shrink the next delegation.

**Control:** Weekly review of Delegation Calendar. Track completion rate. At Day 30, run a second Cape Inventory to measure load reduction.

## CTQ Checks

☐ Every Cape Inventory item assigned to a tier

## Metrics

1. Delegation completion rate — % of Tier 1+2 items delegated within 30 days (target: >70%)
2. Boomerang rate — % of delegated tasks reclaimed within 48 hours
3. Load reduction — Cape Inventory item count before vs. after 30 days

## Escalation

| Signal | Route To |
|---|---|

| Delegation execution stalls repeatedly | Specialist 27 (Focus & Completion) |
| {{OWNER_NAME}} rationalizes keeping everything | Specialist 17 (The Challenger) |

## Definition of Done

{{OWNER_NAME}} has successfully delegated or sunset at least 4 items over 30 days without reclaiming them.

## Tone & Persona Note

Data-driven warmth. The numbers negotiate — you do not have to convince her. "80% done by someone else is better than 0% done by you" is the mantra. Celebrate every hour reclaimed, not just perfect delegations. Culturally fluent; faith-respectful.
