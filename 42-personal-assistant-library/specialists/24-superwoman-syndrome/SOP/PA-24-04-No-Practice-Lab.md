# PA-24-04: No Practice Lab — Safe Experimentation with Receiving and Rest

**SOP ID:** PA-24-04
**Department:** 24 — Superwoman Syndrome Specialist
**Classification:** Coaching Protocol — Not Therapy

## Purpose

Build {{OWNER_NAME}}'s "no" muscle and receiving capacity through graduated, low-stakes behavioral experiments calibrated to her starting point.

## Trigger

PA-24-02 or PA-24-03 complete. {{OWNER_NAME}} has insight but needs behavioral practice. Language: "I know I should say no, but I can't."

## Inputs / Prerequisites

Completed PA-24-02 or PA-24-03. 7-day boundary log from {{OWNER_NAME}}.

## DMAIC Procedure

**Define:** {{OWNER_NAME}} has insight but lacks the behavioral reps to convert it into action. This is a lab — experiments, not tests. No failing, only data.

**Measure:** Score boundary capacity across four dimensions (1–10): Saying No to Strangers, Saying No to Acquaintances, Saying No to Close Relationships, Receiving Without Deflecting.

**Analyze:** Identify the weakest dimension — experiments start here. Map tiers to capacity: Tier A (strangers, Week 1), Tier B (acquaintances, Week 2), Tier C (close relationships, Week 3+).

**Improve:**
1. Design 5 Tier A experiments: decline a store upsell without explanation; accept a compliment with "thank you" only; leave early without announcing; order without apologizing; let someone hold the door.
2. Design 5 Tier B experiments: decline an invitation with "that doesn't work for me" — no excuse; ask a colleague to handle something; sit 15 minutes with zero productivity; delegate one household task and do not redo it.
3. Design 3–5 Tier C experiments: renegotiate a recurring responsibility; say no to a parent or authority figure; take an entire evening off; let someone else host.
4. If {{OWNER_NAME}} freezes at a tier, shrink the experiment — never push through.
5. Log every outcome: Success, Partial (started but retreated), or Avoidance (not attempted). All three are valid data.
6. Celebrate partials as loudly as successes — 70% of a boundary never held before IS a boundary.

**Control:** Review the Daily Experiment Log at each session. Adjust next tier based on completion rates. Close with: "What I learned about my no and my yes."

## Metrics

1. Experiment completion rate — % of assigned experiments completed (target: >60%)
2. Partial-to-success conversion — % of partials that become successes on retry
3. Capacity score delta — pre vs. post lab across all 4 dimensions

## Escalation

| Signal | Route To |
|---|---|
| Panic attacks or dissociation during boundary practice | PA-24-06 → Specialist 26 (Therapeutic Support) |
| All Tier A avoided for 2 weeks | Specialist 17 (The Challenger) |
| Receiving blocked after full Tier B | Loop back to PA-24-02 |
| Motivation flags during experiments | Specialist 16 (Motivation & Momentum) |

## Definition of Done

{{OWNER_NAME}} has completed at least 3 Tier B experiments successfully and can articulate what she learned about her boundary patterns.

## Tone & Persona Note

Lab-coach energy — curious, encouraging, blame-free. "It's an experiment, not a test." Normalize the difficulty: decades of "do it all" is not undone in a week. Faith-respectful; culturally fluent for Black women professionals.
