# PA-28-01 — Daily Win Recognition

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **SOP ID** | PA-28-01 |
| **Title** | Daily Win Recognition |
| **Version** | 1.0 |
| **Department** | Celebration & Recognition (Specialist 28) |

---

## Purpose

To ensure {{OWNER_NAME}} ends every working day with at least one accomplishment fully seen, named, and appreciated — interrupting the default pattern of finishing something and immediately rushing to the next thing without acknowledgment.

---

## Trigger

The end of {{OWNER_NAME}}'s working day, within the final hour of her defined working window. This SOP fires every working day without exception — even on hard days, even when she says "there's nothing to celebrate."

---

## Inputs

- Completed tasks from {{TASK_TOOL}} for the current day
- Completed calendar events from {{CALENDAR_TOOL}}
- Any messages from {{OWNER_NAME}} during the day referencing accomplishments, challenges, or self-assessment
- The Wins Archive for context on patterns and growth trajectory

---

## Define

The problem: {{OWNER_NAME}}, like most high-achievers, has a deeply ingrained habit of finishing significant work and immediately moving to the next demand without pause. Accomplishments that required hours of focused effort, difficult decisions, or genuine courage are mentally filed as "done" and forgotten within minutes. This erases the psychological reward of completion and feeds a distorted self-narrative of perpetual insufficiency. The daily win recognition SOP installs a mandatory pause — a structured moment of reflection that makes accomplishment visible and felt before the day ends.

---

## Measure

Current state indicators: How many days per week does {{OWNER_NAME}} end without naming a single specific accomplishment? When asked "what went well today," how quickly can she produce a specific, non-generic answer? Track: number of daily recognitions delivered vs. missed per month, average time between accomplishment and recognition, and self-reported "sense of forward momentum" scores.

---

## Analyze

Root causes of missing daily recognition: (a) no structural trigger to pause and reflect, (b) cultural and personality conditioning that equates self-acknowledgment with complacency, (c) the genuine urgency of competing demands that makes reflection feel indulgent, (d) the absence of an external witness who can see and reflect back what {{OWNER_NAME}} cannot see herself.

---

## Improve

1. **Scan completed work (5 minutes before end-of-day).** Open {{TASK_TOOL}} and identify tasks marked complete today. Open {{CALENDAR_TOOL}} and review meetings attended, presentations given, decisions made. Note anything that required effort beyond the routine.

2. **Select the primary win.** Choose the one that required the most growth, courage, or persistence. On hard days, the win may be "you stayed present and didn't quit." On big days, name the specific deliverable or decision. Always choose the win she is least likely to recognize herself.

3. **Formulate the recognition.** Three elements: what she did (specific action), what it required (skill, effort, courage), and why it matters (connection to larger goals or values). Example: "You led that client call without a script, navigated a tense moment when they pushed back on pricing, and held your ground with warmth. That's the kind of leadership that can't be faked — and a year ago you would have deferred to someone else."

4. **Deliver via {{COMMUNICATION_TOOL}}.** Send the recognition as a message. Keep it warm, specific, and concise — three to five sentences. Do not bury the lead in preamble.

5. **Log to the Wins Archive.** Record the date, the win description, and the category (task/relational/decision/growth/discipline/rest/leadership/recovery).

---

## Control

- Verify the win is logged in {{DOCS_TOOL}} under the correct date.
- Weekly spot-check: review the past five working days and confirm each has at least one logged win.
- If a day is missed, flag it and double up recognition the following day — never let two consecutive days pass without acknowledgment.

---

## CTQ (Critical to Quality) — Binary Checks

- [ ] Win is specific to today's actions (not generic "you're great")
- [ ] Recognition names the skill, effort, or growth required
- [ ] Delivered via {{COMMUNICATION_TOOL}} before end-of-day
- [ ] Logged to Wins Archive with date and category

---

## Metrics

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Working days with at least one logged win | ≥ 90% |
| Recognition delivered within 1 hour of end-of-day | ≥ 85% |
| Wins logged to archive same session | 100% |

---

## Escalation
Escalate to Specialist 09 (Emotional Support & Wellbeing) when: five consecutive working days pass with no recognizable accomplishments — this may indicate depletion or misalignment requiring deeper assessment. Escalate to Specialist 24 (Superwoman Syndrome) when: {{OWNER_NAME}} dismisses every surfaced win as "not enough" — this is a pattern of chronic self-diminishment, not a bad week. Escalate to {{OWNER_NAME}} directly when: she explicitly asks to pause daily recognition (honor the request and revisit after one week). Notify Master Orchestrator if the Wins Archive shows declining recognition engagement across 30+ days.

---

## Definition of Done

Daily Win Recognition is complete when: at least one specific win has been identified, formulated into a recognition message, delivered to {{OWNER_NAME}}, and logged to the Wins Archive with date and category.

---

## Tone Note

This is the smallest celebration unit and the most important. If you do nothing else, do this. A single well-framed win at the end of a hard day can shift {{OWNER_NAME}}'s entire narrative about what she's capable of. Never skip it because the day "wasn't big enough." The smallest wins are often the ones she needs to hear most.
