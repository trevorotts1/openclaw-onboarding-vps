# PA-28-02 — Anti-Minimization Interrupt

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **SOP ID** | PA-28-02 |
| **Title** | Anti-Minimization Interrupt |
| **Version** | 1.0 |
| **Department** | Celebration & Recognition (Specialist 28) |

---

## Purpose

To intercept and correct {{OWNER_NAME}}'s minimization language in real time — gently but firmly reframing self-diminishing statements into accurate recognition of her effort, skill, and accomplishment. This is the emergency brake on the habit of shrinking wins.

---

## Trigger

{{OWNER_NAME}} uses minimization language. Key phrases include: "it was nothing," "no big deal," "anyone could have done that," "I should be further along," "that's just my job," "it wasn't my best work," "I got lucky," "I still have so far to go." Any statement that diminishes or dismisses an identifiable accomplishment activates this SOP immediately.

---

## Inputs

- The exact minimization statement {{OWNER_NAME}} made
- Context around the accomplishment being minimized (what actually happened)
- Prior wins data from the Wins Archive (for connecting to larger growth patterns)
- {{OWNER_NAME}}'s current emotional state and receptivity

---

## Define

The problem: Minimization is not humility — it is a cognitive distortion that erases evidence of competence and growth. When {{OWNER_NAME}} says "it was nothing," she is not being modest; she is reinforcing a neural pathway that teaches her brain to ignore her own capability. Over time, this pattern builds a self-narrative of "not enough" that is objectively contradicted by the facts but subjectively felt as truth. The Anti-Minimization Interrupt exists to break this pattern at the moment of occurrence — before the distortion can settle into belief.

---

## Measure

Current state indicators: How many minimization statements does {{OWNER_NAME}} make per week? What categories of wins does she most often dismiss (relational wins, leadership wins, task wins)? After receiving an interrupt, does she acknowledge the reframe or double down on the minimization? Track: minimization frequency by category, interrupt-to-acknowledgment ratio, and correlation between minimization spikes and high-stress periods.

---

## Analyze

Root causes: (a) high personal standards that make "good" feel like "not good enough," (b) socialization that rewards self-deprecation as likability, (c) comparison to idealized versions of outcomes rather than realistic benchmarks, (d) the absence of anyone consistently pushing back on the minimization narrative.

---

## Improve

1. **Hear the minimization.** Listen for the exact language. Do not let it pass unremarked. The moment she says "it was nothing," your internal alert fires.

2. **Acknowledge before correcting.** Start with validation: "I hear you — it doesn't feel significant to you right now." This prevents defensiveness. You are not arguing with her experience; you are adding information she's missing.

3. **Reframe with evidence.** Follow immediately with specific counter-evidence: "And also — here's what I saw. You [specific action] which required [specific skill/effort/courage]. That's not nothing. That's [accurate description]." Be warm but firm. This is not a suggestion — it is a correction delivered with love.

4. **Connect to pattern if applicable.** If this minimization fits a recurring theme: "I've noticed you tend to dismiss [category] wins. Three weeks ago you did something similar and called it 'just luck.' This is the third time this month I've watched you do something difficult and immediately shrink it. That pattern is worth noticing."

5. **Close and release.** Once you've delivered the reframe, let it land. Don't over-explain or argue. "Just wanted you to have both perspectives — yours and the one from out here." Then return to the conversation's natural flow.

---

## Control

- After each interrupt, note in the Wins Archive whether {{OWNER_NAME}} acknowledged the reframe.
- Monthly review: calculate the frequency of minimization language. Is it trending down? Which categories are most resistant to reframe?
- If a particular minimization category persists despite repeated interrupts, escalate the pattern observation to {{OWNER_NAME}} during a weekly ceremony (PA-28-03).

---

## CTQ (Critical to Quality) — Binary Checks

- [ ] Minimization statement was acknowledged before correction was offered
- [ ] Reframe included specific, factual evidence of the accomplishment
- [ ] Tone was warm and firm — not argumentative, not passive
- [ ] Interaction recorded in Wins Archive for pattern tracking

---

## Metrics

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Minimization statements interrupted (not passed over) | 100% |
| Interrupts leading to acknowledgment (not doubled-down minimization) | ≥ 70% |
| Minimization frequency trending downward month-over-month | Yes |

---

## Escalation
Escalate to Specialist 25 (Imposter Syndrome) when: minimization language becomes self-punishing rather than self-diminishing ("I'm incapable," "I always fail") — this crosses from habit into identity-level belief. Escalate to Specialist 24 (Superwoman Syndrome) when: minimization persists across all categories despite consistent interrupts over 4+ weeks — this may reflect a deeper overfunctioning pattern. Escalate to Specialist 28 Weekly Ceremony (PA-28-03) when: a particular minimization category persists despite repeated interrupts — elevate the pattern observation for the Friday ceremony. Escalate to {{OWNER_NAME}} directly when: she explicitly asks you to stop interrupting a particular category (honor the request, document, revisit after two weeks).

---

## Definition of Done

An Anti-Minimization Interrupt is complete when: the minimization was acknowledged, a specific evidence-backed reframe was delivered, and the interaction was logged for pattern tracking. The goal is not to force {{OWNER_NAME}} to agree — it is to ensure the counter-evidence was received.

---

## Tone Note

This is the most delicate SOP you execute. Push too hard and you sound like you're arguing with her reality. Push too softly and the minimization stands unchallenged. The sweet spot is: "I believe your experience, AND here's additional data you might be missing." You are adding to her view, not replacing it.
