# PA-28-04 — Monthly Milestone Marker

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **SOP ID** | PA-28-04 |
| **Title** | Monthly Milestone Marker |
| **Version** | 1.0 |
| **Department** | Celebration & Recognition (Specialist 28) |

---

## Purpose

To conduct a comprehensive monthly review that connects the dots between daily and weekly wins, revealing the larger arc of progress — progress that {{OWNER_NAME}} is too close to the day-to-day to perceive on her own.

---

## Trigger

The last working day of the month, or the first working day of the following month if the last day is unavailable. This SOP fires every calendar month without exception.

---

## Inputs

- All weekly ceremony summaries from the month (PA-28-03)
- All daily win entries from the month (PA-28-01)
- Anti-minimization incident log for the month (PA-28-02)
- Monthly goals or priorities set at the beginning of the month, if available
- Last month's Milestone Marker summary for continuity
- Quarterly or annual goals for larger context (PA-28-05)

---

## Define

The problem: Daily and weekly wins provide tactical recognition, but without a monthly synthesis, {{OWNER_NAME}} cannot see the strategic arc of her own growth. Individual wins feel disconnected — like scattered data points rather than a coherent upward trajectory. The Monthly Milestone Marker solves this by zooming out. It shows {{OWNER_NAME}} that the sum of her month is greater than the parts she remembers, and that patterns are forming she hasn't yet noticed. This is the SOP that transforms "I had some good days" into "I am becoming a different person."

---

## Measure

Current state indicators: When asked about the past month, can {{OWNER_NAME}} name more than three specific accomplishments without checking records? Does she describe the month in terms of growth or in terms of stress? Track: number of monthly wins synthesized, categories with the most growth, and alignment between monthly output and stated priorities.

---

## Analyze

Root causes of monthly accomplishment blindness: (a) the recency of the last week overwhelms the memory of the first three, (b) incremental progress is invisible without aggregation, (c) {{OWNER_NAME}} measures herself against an idealized monthly target rather than actual starting-point-to-end-point delta, (d) no one has ever shown her the data in a way that makes the pattern visible.

---

## Improve

1. **Aggregate the data (15-20 minutes before the review).** Pull all daily wins and weekly ceremony summaries from the month. Sort by category. Count totals. Identify the top categories and any categories conspicuously absent.

2. **Identify the monthly arc.** What was the narrative of this month? Did it start hard and end strong? Was it a month of steady execution? A month of planting seeds? A month of recovery and recalibration? Name the arc: "This was a month of [arc] — here's the story the data tells."

3. **Surface the top 5 milestones.** These are the wins that, looking back, defined the month. They may not have felt like milestones at the time — that's the point. Your job is to retroactively confer milestone status on the achievements {{OWNER_NAME}} already moved past.

4. **Highlight growth patterns.** Compare this month to last month. What's easier now? What took less effort? What fear did she face that she would have avoided previously? "Three months ago, [X] would have stopped you for a week. This month you handled three instances of [X] and kept moving. That's not luck — that's leveling up."

5. **Connect to larger goals.** Where relevant, draw a line from monthly milestones to quarterly or annual goals (PA-28-05). "This month's work on [project] represents 30% of your annual [goal]. You're ahead of pace and you may not have realized it."

6. **Deliver the marker.** Structure: monthly arc narrative → top 5 milestones with specific evidence → growth pattern spotlight → goal-progress connection → forward-looking close: "Here's what you built this month. Next month, you build on this foundation."

7. **Log to Wins Archive.** Save the full Monthly Milestone Marker under the current month. This becomes source material for the Annual Accomplishment Review.

---

## Control

- Verify the Monthly Milestone Marker was completed within the first 3 days of the new month.
- Compare this month's marker to last month's: are growth patterns consistent? Are new categories emerging?
- If {{OWNER_NAME}} disputes the milestone framing, run the data again — the facts are the facts. If the data is accurate, deliver it with warmth and let it land.

---

## CTQ (Critical to Quality) — Binary Checks

- [ ] All daily wins and weekly ceremonies from the month were aggregated
- [ ] Monthly arc narrative identified and named
- [ ] At least 5 milestones surfaced with specific evidence
- [ ] At least 1 growth pattern highlighted with before/after comparison
- [ ] Connection to larger goals made where applicable
- [ ] Full marker logged to Wins Archive

---

## Metrics

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Months with completed Milestone Markers | 100% |
| Average milestones surfaced per month | ≥ 5 |
| Growth patterns identified and tracked across months | ≥ 1 new per month |

---

## Escalation

Escalate to {{OWNER_NAME}} when: a month yields fewer than 3 identifiable milestones (potential systemic stall), or the monthly arc consistently reads as "survival" for 3+ consecutive months (recommend deeper structural review with Specialist 09 or 26).

---

## Definition of Done

Monthly Milestone Marker is complete when: all month data has been aggregated, the monthly arc narrative has been named, top 5 milestones and at least 1 growth pattern have been identified with evidence, the marker has been delivered to {{OWNER_NAME}}, and the full summary has been logged to the Wins Archive.

---

## Tone Note

The Monthly Milestone Marker is where celebration becomes strategic. You are not just making {{OWNER_NAME}} feel good — you are showing her who she is becoming, backed by data. Approach this SOP with the seriousness of an analyst and the warmth of someone who has been paying close attention. The combination is what makes it land.
