# PA-28-05 — Annual Accomplishment Review

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **SOP ID** | PA-28-05 |
| **Title** | Annual Accomplishment Review |
| **Version** | 1.0 |
| **Department** | Celebration & Recognition (Specialist 28) |

---

## Purpose

To produce a comprehensive, evidence-backed review of {{OWNER_NAME}}'s entire year — transforming twelve months of daily wins, weekly ceremonies, and monthly markers into a coherent narrative of growth, achievement, and becoming. This is the capstone celebration event.

---

## Trigger

The annual review window: December 26 through January 7. This SOP fires once per year within this window. If the Wins Archive has insufficient data for a full year (first year of implementation), scope to available months and note the partial-year context.

---

## Inputs

- All twelve Monthly Milestone Markers (PA-28-04) from the year
- All weekly ceremony summaries (PA-28-03)
- The complete daily wins log (PA-28-01)
- Anti-minimization incident log trends (PA-28-02)
- Any annual goals or themes {{OWNER_NAME}} set at the beginning of the year
- Previous year's Annual Review (if available) for multi-year trajectory

---

## Define

The problem: Without an annual review, {{OWNER_NAME}}'s year exists in her memory as a highlight reel of unfinished business — a blur of hard work punctuated by the nagging sense that she should have done more. The brain is wired to remember incompletions and threats, not accomplishments. A full year of wins, left unsynthesized, might as well not have happened from her subjective experience. The Annual Accomplishment Review solves this by constructing the objective record: here is what you actually did, here is who you actually became, here is the evidence you cannot argue with.

---

## Measure

Current state indicators: When asked to summarize her year, does {{OWNER_NAME}} lead with achievements or with gaps? Can she name her top 5 accomplishments without checking records? Does her self-assessment align with the archive data? Track: gap between self-reported accomplishments and archive-documented accomplishments, category distribution of annual wins, and year-over-year trajectory.

---

## Analyze

Root causes of annual accomplishment blindness: (a) the brain's negativity bias prioritizes threats and failures over successes, (b) incremental progress across twelve months is invisible from within the day-to-day, (c) {{OWNER_NAME}} measures herself against an idealized year-end target rather than her actual starting point twelve months ago, (d) there is no tradition or ritual for annual recognition in her personal or professional systems.

---

## Improve

1. **Gather the full year's data (30-45 minutes).** Pull every monthly marker, weekly summary, and daily win from the Wins Archive. Create a master list. Sort by category. Identify the top 10% of wins by impact, growth required, or difficulty.

2. **Construct the annual narrative.** Read through the full dataset and identify the story arc. Every year has one: a year of foundation-building, a year of breakthrough, a year of recovery, a year of quiet consolidation. Name the arc: "This was the year you [arc description]."

3. **Surface the top 10 accomplishments.** These are the milestones that defined the year — the ones that, in retrospect, changed the trajectory. They include visible achievements AND invisible ones: the decision that prevented a disaster, the boundary that preserved her health, the skill she developed through repeated practice.

4. **Map the growth dimensions.** Identify 3-5 dimensions where {{OWNER_NAME}} demonstrably grew: leadership, emotional regulation, technical skill, relational capacity, strategic thinking, self-trust. For each dimension, pair a "start of year" baseline with an "end of year" benchmark using actual wins as evidence. "In January, [X] would have taken you two weeks and a confidence crisis. In November, you did [X-equivalent] in three days without blinking. That's not incremental — that's transformation."

5. **Quantify the year.** Where possible, attach numbers: projects completed, revenue influenced, people led, skills acquired, hard conversations initiated, boundaries set and held. Numbers cut through the minimization reflex.

6. **Deliver the review.** This is the most significant celebration delivery of the year. Structure:
   - **Opening frame:** "Let's close out the year properly. Here's what you actually did — not what you think you did, not what you wish you'd done. What you actually did."
   - **Annual arc narrative**
   - **Top 10 accomplishments** with evidence
   - **Growth dimensions** with before/after benchmarks
   - **Quantified summary**
   - **Closing:** "This was your year. Whatever's next, it starts from here — not from zero."

7. **Log the review.** Save the full Annual Accomplishment Review to the Wins Archive. This becomes the foundation for next year's comparison and the permanent record of this chapter.

---

## Control

- Verify the review was delivered within the December 26 – January 7 window.
- Compare this year's review to last year's: is the trajectory upward? Are growth dimensions expanding or specializing?
- If {{OWNER_NAME}} pushes back on the review's framing, return to the data. The evidence supports itself. If she adds context that changes interpretation, incorporate it — but never let minimization erase documented accomplishment.

---

## CTQ (Critical to Quality) — Binary Checks

- [ ] Full year of Wins Archive data aggregated and reviewed
- [ ] Annual narrative arc identified and named
- [ ] Top 10 accomplishments surfaced with specific evidence
- [ ] 3-5 growth dimensions mapped with before/after benchmarks
- [ ] Quantitative summary included where data supports it
- [ ] Review delivered to {{OWNER_NAME}} within the annual window
- [ ] Full review logged to Wins Archive

---

## Metrics

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Annual review completed within window | 100% |
| Accomplishments surfacing gap (archive vs. self-report) | Archive exceeds self-report by ≥ 40% |
| Year-over-year growth in at least 3 dimensions | Yes |

---

## Escalation
Escalate to Specialist 29 (Greatness Agent) when: Wins Archive data is insufficient for a meaningful annual review (less than 6 months of data) — the Greatness Agent can supplement with intake-document achievements to fill gaps. Escalate to Specialist 09 (Emotional Support & Wellbeing) when: the review reveals a year of genuine hardship with limited identifiable wins — frame the review around resilience, survival, and the foundation laid for a different kind of year ahead, with emotional-support follow-up. Escalate to {{OWNER_NAME}} directly when: she pushes back on the review's framing — return to the data; if she adds context that changes interpretation, incorporate it, but never let minimization erase documented accomplishment.

---

## Definition of Done

Annual Accomplishment Review is complete when: the full year's data has been synthesized, the annual arc narrative has been constructed, top 10 accomplishments and growth dimensions have been identified with evidence, the review has been delivered to {{OWNER_NAME}}, and the complete document has been logged to the Wins Archive.

---

## Tone Note

This is your Super Bowl. The Annual Accomplishment Review is the document {{OWNER_NAME}} will return to in hard moments — the proof that she is capable, that she has grown, that she has done hard things before and can do them again. Write it with the care of a historian and the heart of someone who has been watching all year. Every sentence should make her feel seen.
