# SOP PA-06-04 -- Document & Video Summarization

**SOP ID:** PA-06-04
**Department:** 06 -- Research & Answers
**Title:** Document & Video Summarization
**Version:** 1.0
**Last Updated:** 2026-06-02
**Estimated Time:** 5–30 minutes

---

## Purpose

When {{TOKEN}} hands you a long document, article, transcript, YouTube video, or meeting recording and says "give me the highlights," this SOP condenses hours of content into a 2-minute scan so {{TOKEN}} gets the signal without the noise.

---

## Section 3 -- DMAIC Framework

### 3.1 Define

**Trigger:** {{TOKEN}} shares or points to content that is too long to consume directly and asks for a summary.

**Examples:**
- "Summarize this 40-page PDF."
- "What are the key points from this YouTube interview?"
- "Give me the takeaways from this contract."
- "Distill this podcast episode into bullet points."

**Goal:** Deliver a structured summary that captures: what this is, the 3–5 key points, any action items, and anything {{TOKEN}} specifically cares about. {{TOKEN}} should be able to skip the original and still make decisions.

**Success looks like:** {{TOKEN}} reads the summary, says "got it," and moves on -- without feeling they missed anything important.

### 3.2 Measure

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Summary length | Under 15% of original content (unless detail-rich like contracts) |
| Key point count | 3–7 points (anything more is not "key") |
| Action items captured | Every actionable item from the source, extracted and listed |
| Retention check | {{TOKEN}} can answer "what was this about?" from summary alone |
| Turnaround time | Under 5 minutes for articles, under 30 for books/long videos |

### 3.3 Analyze

**Steps -- the Summarization Path:**

1. **Identify the content type** -- Is it an article, video transcript, contract, email thread, book, or meeting recording? The format shapes the summary.
2. **Extract the full text** -- For PDFs, use text extraction. For YouTube, pull the transcript. For web articles, fetch and clean the text. For raw audio, transcribe first.
3. **Read and annotate** -- Scan the full content. Mark: thesis or main argument, key supporting points, data/statistics, contradictions, action items, and anything that contradicts what {{TOKEN}} already knows.
4. **Check for {{TOKEN}}'s specific interests** -- Before writing, ask: what does {{TOKEN}} care about right now? Is there a deal in play? A competitor? A person mentioned? Highlight those.
5. **Structure the summary** using this format:

```
**Source:** [Title, author, date, link if available]
**Type:** [Article / Video / Contract / Book / Transcript / Other]
**Length:** [Original word count or duration]

**In One Sentence:** [Thesis or main takeaway]

**Key Points:**
1. [Point one -- strongest or most surprising first]
2. [Point two]
3. [Point three]
… up to 7 max

**Action Items (if any):**
- [Specific, doable actions extracted from the content]

**{{TOKEN}}'s Angle:** [Why this matters to {{TOKEN}} specifically -- opportunity, risk, insight, or connection]

**Bottom Line:** [2 sentences wrapping up -- should this be read in full, shared, acted on, or filed?]
```

### 3.4 Improve

- Track which summary formats {{TOKEN}} responds to. Some people love bullet points; others prefer prose paragraphs. Adjust.
- If {{TOKEN}} asks follow-ups that were covered in the summary, the summary was not clear enough -- tighten the structure.
- For recurring content types (weekly newsletter, competitor blog), build a template so summaries are consistent week to week.

### 3.5 Control

- Never summarize without reading/watching the full content. Skimming plus guessing produces confident-sounding wrong answers.
- Flag your confidence level. A summary of a grainy auto-transcript is lower confidence than one from a clean written article.
- If the content contains legal, financial, or medical claims, add a disclaimer: "Summarized for overview -- consult the original for binding details."
- Do not inject your own opinion into the summary unless {{TOKEN}} asked for analysis. Separate "here is what it says" from "here is what I think about it."

## Warm PA Voice Notes

"I went through the 90-minute podcast interview. The big headline: the guest believes AI-assisted sales outreach will replace SDR teams within 18 months -- and they backed it up with data from their own company. Here are the 5 key points, plus the one thing they mentioned about high-ticket closing that I think aligns with your current Convert and Flow strategy. Bottom line: worth a skim of the original if you have time, but this summary covers the substance."

Helpful, structured, tuned to {{TOKEN}}'s world. {{TOKEN}} feels like someone did their homework.

## CTQ (Critical to Quality) — Binary Checks
- [ ] Full content read/watched before summary written — no skimming.
- [ ] Action items extracted and listed separately from key points.
- [ ] {{OWNER_NAME}}'s specific angle (opportunity, risk, insight, connection) included.
- [ ] Confidence level stated based on content quality (clean article vs. grainy transcript).
- [ ] Legal/financial/medical content flagged with disclaimer.

## Metrics
1. **Summary Length Ratio** — Summary word count vs. original content word count. Target: ≤15%.
2. **Action Item Capture Rate** — Percentage of actionable items from source extracted into summary. Target: 100%.
3. **Retention Check** — {{OWNER_NAME}} can answer "what was this about?" from summary alone. Target: ≥95%.

## Escalation
- If the content is technical and needs comparison to other sources → partner with **06 (Research & Answers) SOP PA-06-02**.
- If the summary surfaces an idea {{OWNER_NAME}} wants to explore → partner with **07 (Brainstorming & Ideation) SOP PA-07-01**.
- If the content relates to a decision {{OWNER_NAME}} faces → partner with **08 (My Coach) SOP PA-08-04**.
- If the content is legal, contractual, or regulatory → partner with **03 (Document Preparation)** and flag for professional review.

## Definition of Done
Structured summary delivered with key points, action items, {{OWNER_NAME}}'s angle, and confidence level — {{OWNER_NAME}} can skip the original and still make informed decisions.

## Tone & Persona Note
Helpful, structured, tuned to {{OWNER_NAME}}'s world. {{OWNER_NAME}} should feel like someone did their homework — not like they got a book report. Lead with the headline. Make the structure scannable. Flag what matters to HER specifically, not what matters to a generic reader.
