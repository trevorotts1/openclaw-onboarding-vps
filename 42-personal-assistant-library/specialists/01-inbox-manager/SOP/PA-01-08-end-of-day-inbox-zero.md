## When to Run

The last 20 minutes of every workday.

**Frequency:** Daily — no exceptions.

**Inputs:** {{EMAIL_TOOL}} inbox — every email that has arrived since the mid-day processing sweep.

---

## Define

- **Problem:** An inbox left with unprocessed emails overnight creates three problems: (a) Decision debt — emails that were read but not actioned sit in the inbox and require re-reading tomorrow, effectively doubling processing time, (b) Morning overwhelm — starting the day with 40+ unread emails creates immediate stress before any real work begins, (c) Lost opportunities — an email that arrived at 4:45pm with a time-sensitive request sits unread until 9:00am the next morning. Inbox zero is the daily discipline that prevents all three.
- **Goal:** The inbox is at zero — every email has been touched, assigned a destination, and moved out of the inbox — before you sign off for the day. Zero means: the inbox view is empty. No unread badges. No "I will deal with this tomorrow" emails. Every message has been deleted, delegated, deferred, or done. {{OWNER_NAME}} receives an end-of-day report: inbox status, emails processed, items flagged for tomorrow.
- **Scope:** All business email accounts managed by this role. The entire inbox — primary, social, promotions, updates tabs if using Gmail. Every folder and label where inbound email lands.

---

## Measure

- **CTQ 1 — Inbox count at sign-off:** The number displayed as unread/in-inbox at the end of the end-of-day sweep. Target: 0. Every single day.
- **CTQ 2 — Sweep duration:** Time to complete the end-of-day sweep. Target: 20 minutes or less.
- **CTQ 3 — Carry-over rate:** Percentage of emails from the end-of-day sweep that are still flagged but unresolved at the start of the next day. Target: 0% — nothing is left mid-process.
- **Metrics:** Emails processed in end-of-day sweep (count), sweep completion time (minutes), zero streak (consecutive days of inbox zero), items flagged for {{OWNER_NAME}}'s morning review (count).

---

## Analyze

1. **End-of-day email patterns.** The last 2 hours of the workday typically bring: (a) end-of-day replies from people clearing their own inboxes, (b) "before you leave" or "by EOD" requests, (c) automated end-of-day reports and digests, (d) last-minute meeting follow-ups. Category (b) is the most important — these are the emails that, if missed, create a crisis by tomorrow morning. The end-of-day sweep catches these before they become tomorrow's fires.
2. **The "one more thing" trap.** The hardest part of inbox zero is resisting the urge to keep processing after the sweep is done. A new email arrives at 5:55pm — do you process it or leave it? Rule: if you are still at your desk and the sweep is not formally closed, process it. If you have already sent the end-of-day report and signed off, it waits for the morning sweep. The boundary is the sign-off message — once sent, the day is closed.

---

## Improve

1. **The final sweep routine.** (a) Open the inbox — scan all unread messages since the mid-day sweep, (b) Apply the 4-D triage to every message (Delete, Delegate, Defer, Do), (c) For any Do items that need {{OWNER_NAME}}'s input, draft the reply or summary now — do not leave "draft tomorrow" items, (d) Verify: is the inbox view empty? Are there any flagged or starred items still sitting there? (e) Check all tabs/folders where inbound lands — the zero must be universal.
2. **The end-of-day report.** Send {{OWNER_NAME}} a closing message: "Inbox is at zero. [X] emails processed this sweep. [Y] total today. [Z] items flagged for your review tomorrow morning: [brief list]. Follow-ups: [status]. Good night." This message serves two purposes: it informs {{OWNER_NAME}} and it forces you to verify that nothing is left hanging.
3. **The "handle it now" rule for quick actions.** During the end-of-day sweep, if an email requires less than 2 minutes to fully resolve (quick reply, quick file, quick forward), do it immediately. Do not flag it for tomorrow. The end-of-day sweep is for CLOSING the day, not for creating tomorrow's to-do list.

---

## Control

- **The zero-streak tracker.** Maintain a simple count: consecutive days of inbox zero. If the streak breaks, investigate: what happened? Was the sweep interrupted? Too many emails? A personal emergency? Learn from the break and restart the streak.
- **Morning verification.** The first thing you check every morning: did the end-of-day sweep actually hold? If any emails arrived after your sign-off, they are waiting and will be processed in the morning sweep. That is expected. But if the inbox shows emails that were there BEFORE the sweep, the sweep failed. Investigate and fix the process.
- **Monthly streak report.** Report the inbox zero streak to {{OWNER_NAME}} monthly. A 30-day streak is the target. A 90-day streak means the system is working perfectly. Celebrate the streaks — they represent hours of protected attention for {{OWNER_NAME}}.

## Definition of Done

The inbox is at zero across all connected accounts and tabs, the end-of-day report has been sent to {{OWNER_NAME}}, and you have signed off — the day is closed, and tomorrow will start clean.

## Escalation

- **Specialist 03 (Daily Briefing & Debrief)** — to hand off tomorrow's flagged items and priority signals for the morning briefing
- **Specialist 04 (Task & Priority Manager)** — if flagged items include tasks or deadlines that need to appear in tomorrow's Top-3
- Escalate to {{OWNER_NAME}} — if the end-of-day sweep cannot reach zero due to an extraordinary volume of urgent inbound (this should be rare; it signals a systemic issue to discuss)

## Tone & Persona Note

The end-of-day sweep is a sacred ritual. It is the boundary between work and rest — for {{OWNER_NAME}} and for you. Every email you close out is permission for {{OWNER_NAME}} to truly disconnect. Your end-of-day report should feel like a trusted colleague locking the office door and handing over the keys: "Everything is handled. You're free. Good night." Protect this boundary fiercely — and celebrate every zero-streak milestone.
