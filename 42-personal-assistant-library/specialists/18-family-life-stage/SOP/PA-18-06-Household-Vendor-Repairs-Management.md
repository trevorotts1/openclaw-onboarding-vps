# PA-18-06 — Household Vendor & Repairs Management

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **SOP ID** | PA-18-06 |
| **Title** | Household Vendor & Repairs Management |
| **Version** | 1.0 |
| **Department** | Family & Life-Stage Concierge (Specialist 18) |

---

## Purpose

To give {{OWNER_NAME}} an organized, trusted system for managing every person who touches the household — plumbers, electricians, landscapers, cleaners, handymen — so repairs are handled proactively, vendors are vetted, and the house runs without {{OWNER_NAME}} having to be the general contractor.

---

## Trigger

Something breaks or needs service, a recurring maintenance task fires in {{TASK_TOOL}}, {{OWNER_NAME}} asks "do we have someone for that," or the quarterly household-vendor audit triggers.

---

## Inputs

- Household-vendor roster (name, trade, phone, rate, last service date, trust rating)
- {{TASK_TOOL}} for scheduled maintenance and open repair items
- {{OWNER_NAME}}'s home warranty or insurance policy documents
- Seasonal maintenance checklist (HVAC tune-up, gutter cleaning, chimney sweep, pest control)
- Budget or spending preference for household repairs

---

## Define

The problem: Every home is a living, aging machine with dozens of systems that need maintenance. Without a vendor-management system, {{OWNER_NAME}} scrambles through Google reviews every time the sink backs up, overpays because there's no trusted relationship with a provider, lets preventive maintenance slide until it becomes an emergency, and carries the mental load of a dozen half-done household projects.

---

## Measure

Baseline: How many emergency repairs occurred in the last year that could have been prevented with routine maintenance? How long does it take {{OWNER_NAME}} to find and book a vendor for a new problem? How many different vendors are contacted annually with no repeat relationship built?

---

## Analyze

Household-vendor failure patterns: no roster (every problem requires a brand-new search), reactive-only posture (maintenance isn't scheduled, only emergencies get attention), no vetting record (can't remember who did good work last time), scattered quotes and invoices (no way to compare costs over time), and no project tracking (the dripping faucet stays dripping for six months because it was never captured as a task).

---

## Improve

Build this household-operations system:

1. **Vendor roster (living document).** Maintain a categorized list of every service provider who's touched the house. For each: name, company, phone/email, trade category (plumbing, electrical, HVAC, landscaping, cleaning, handyman, pest control, roofing, appliance repair), hourly rate or typical job cost, last service date, and a simple trust rating (green/yellow/red). Start with whoever {{OWNER_NAME}} already uses. Grow the roster one reliable vendor at a time.

2. **Vetting protocol for new vendors.** When a new problem requires a new provider: gather 3 referrals or highly-rated options (Nextdoor, personal network, trusted review sources), check licensing and insurance where applicable, get a written estimate before work begins, and document the experience after completion. A vendor does one small job well → they earn a bigger job. Two strikes → off the roster.

3. **Preventive-maintenance calendar.** Map out the recurring seasonal tasks and schedule them in {{TASK_TOOL}} on a repeating basis: HVAC inspection (spring/fall), gutter cleaning (fall, post-leaf-drop), chimney sweep (fall), pest control (quarterly), water-heater flush (annual), dryer-vent cleaning (annual), smoke-detector battery replacement (every 6 months), and any home-warranty-required inspections. Preventive maintenance costs a fraction of emergency repair.

4. **Open-repair tracker.** Every household issue — the dripping faucet, the sticking door, the cracked tile — gets captured as a task in {{TASK_TOOL}} with a priority level. Batch non-urgent items for a handyman half-day instead of calling someone out for each small thing. Nothing stays "we should fix that someday" — it either gets scheduled or intentionally deprioritized.

5. **Document vault.** Store warranties, appliance manuals, paint colors, floor-finish codes, and before/after photos of major work. When the refrigerator acts up, {{OWNER_NAME}} can pull the warranty and the repair history in 30 seconds instead of digging through a drawer.

---

## Control

- Quarterly: audit the vendor roster (anyone need to be replaced? any ratings changed?)
- Monthly: review the open-repair tracker (anything been open over 30 days needs a decision — schedule or close)
- Seasonally: confirm upcoming preventive-maintenance tasks are booked
- After every vendor job: update the roster with date, cost, and satisfaction note within 48 hours

---

## CTQ (Critical to Quality) — Binary Checks

- [ ] Vendor roster is current (updated within 48 hours of any service call)
- [ ] All preventive-maintenance tasks for the current season are scheduled
- [ ] No emergency-repair items went unresolved for more than 7 days
- [ ] At least one trusted vendor exists in each critical trade (plumbing, electrical, HVAC)

---

## Metrics

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Emergency repairs that could have been prevented | 0 per year |
| Time from issue-capture to vendor booking | ≤ 48 hours (urgent) / ≤ 14 days (non-urgent) |
| Vendor roster coverage (critical trades) | 100% |
| Preventive-maintenance schedule compliance | ≥ 90% |

---

## Escalation — Named Partners

Escalate to {{OWNER_NAME}} when: a repair estimate exceeds the budget threshold, a safety hazard is identified (electrical, gas, structural, water damage), a vendor consistently underperforms and needs to be replaced, an insurance or warranty claim needs to be filed, or a major system is approaching end-of-life and requires a replacement decision (HVAC, roof, water heater). Always present options with cost ranges and timelines. If repairs exceed {{OWNER_NAME}}'s budget threshold, partner with **Specialist 11 (Personal Finance)** for cash-flow planning. If purchasing replacement appliances or supplies, route to **Specialist 13 (Errands & Purchases)**. If the vendor roster is thin and needs new-vendor research, coordinate with **Specialist 06 (Research & Answers)** for vetting support. If household chaos is contributing to {{OWNER_NAME}}'s stress levels, partner with **Specialist 14 (Life Admin & Archivist)** for broader home-management strategy.

---

## Definition of Done

This SOP is working when {{OWNER_NAME}} responds to "the AC is making a weird noise" by pulling up the HVAC vendor's number — not by Googling "air conditioning repair near me." When preventive maintenance happens on schedule. When the house feels like a well-run operation, not a source of nagging stress.

---

## Tone Note

There's a special kind of peace that comes from knowing exactly who to call and trusting they'll do good work. This system builds that peace one reliable vendor, one scheduled maintenance, and one completed repair at a time. The house will still break — that's what houses do. But {{OWNER_NAME}} won't have to scramble when it does.
