# PA-18-02 — Kids & School Logistics

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **SOP ID** | PA-18-02 |
| **Title** | Kids & School Logistics |
| **Version** | 1.0 |
| **Department** | Family & Life-Stage Concierge (Specialist 18) |

---

## Purpose

To give {{OWNER_NAME}} a single reliable system for managing the endless stream of school communications, permission slips, event dates, supply needs, and teacher touchpoints — so nothing falls through the cracks and {{OWNER_NAME}} stays present for the moments that matter.

---

## Trigger

New school communication arrives (email, app notification, paper flyer in backpack), {{OWNER_NAME}} asks "what's happening at school this week," or the weekly school-logistics review fires in {{TASK_TOOL}} (recommended: Sunday afternoon).

---

## Inputs

- School communication channels (email, parent portal, classroom apps like ClassDojo/Remind/Seesaw)
- {{CALENDAR_TOOL}} for family calendar
- School district calendar (holidays, early-dismissal days, conference windows)
- {{TASK_TOOL}} for action items (forms to sign, supplies to buy, volunteer slots to fill)

---

## Define

The problem: School communication comes from a dozen directions — teacher emails, the principal's newsletter, the PTA blast, the classroom app, the crumpled flyer in the bottom of the backpack. Without a central triage system, {{OWNER_NAME}} discovers the bake sale 12 hours before it happens and the permission slip the morning of the field trip. This SOP creates one trusted inbox-to-action pipeline for everything school-related.

---

## Measure

Baseline: How many school-related surprises happened last month? How often did {{OWNER_NAME}} have to scramble for something that should have been on the calendar? Track missed deadlines, late-notice events, and frequency of "I didn't know about that" moments.

---

## Analyze

School-logistics failure modes: communication arrives in channels {{OWNER_NAME}} doesn't regularly check, paper items get lost before they reach the kitchen counter, dates are shared once and never transferred to the family calendar, and the sheer volume of non-urgent school chatter buries the one thing that actually requires action.

---

## Improve

Follow this weekly rhythm:

1. **Centralize the intake.** Route all school email to a dedicated folder or label. Enable notifications from classroom apps for direct messages only — mute the general feed. Designate one physical landing spot for paper communications (a tray, a folder, a magnet clip on the fridge).

2. **Sunday school sweep.** Every Sunday, run a 15-minute school-logistics sweep: open every channel, extract every date and action item, and do three things immediately — add events to {{CALENDAR_TOOL}}, create tasks in {{TASK_TOOL}} with due dates 48 hours before the deadline, and discard or file the source communication.

3. **Morning backpack check.** A 90-second ritual: check the designated paper landing spot, glance at the classroom app for direct messages, confirm today's specials (PE shoes? library books? instrument?).

4. **Teacher-touchpoint cadence.** Maintain a light calendar reminder for parent-teacher conferences, open-house nights, and periodic check-ins. Not every teacher needs weekly contact, but {{OWNER_NAME}} should never miss a scheduled conference.

5. **Supply-chain buffer.** Keep a running "school supplies" list in {{TASK_TOOL}}. When a teacher note mentions "we're running low on tissues" or the science fair needs tri-fold boards, capture it immediately. Batch-buy once a month.

---

## Control

- Every Sunday: verify all known school events for the upcoming 2 weeks are on {{CALENDAR_TOOL}}
- Every evening: confirm tomorrow's backpack needs are handled
- Monthly: audit the school-email folder — if anything slipped past triage, tighten the net

---

## CTQ (Critical to Quality) — Binary Checks

- [ ] This week's school events are on {{CALENDAR_TOOL}} by Sunday evening
- [ ] All permission slips and forms due this week are submitted on time
- [ ] Tomorrow's backpack/supply needs are confirmed tonight
- [ ] No school event surprised {{OWNER_NAME}} with less than 48 hours' notice

---

## Metrics

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| School events on calendar by Sunday night | 100% |
| Permission-slip/form deadlines met | ≥ 98% |
| Late-notice school surprises | 0 per month |
| Teacher conferences attended | 100% |

---

## Escalation — Named Partners

Escalate to {{OWNER_NAME}} when: a serious behavioral or academic concern arrives from a teacher, a deadline was missed that has consequences (field-trip exclusion, late fee, registration close), or a pattern of "I didn't know" moments suggests a broken intake channel. Always present the escalation with the original communication attached. If school logistics overwhelm is chronic, partner with **Specialist 04 (Task Priority Manager)** for triage redesign. If supply-chain management requires purchasing coordination, route to **Specialist 13 (Errands & Purchases)**. If behavioral or academic concerns require deeper interventions, coordinate with **Specialist 09 (Emotional Support & Wellbeing)** for parental support strategy.

---

## Definition of Done

This SOP is healthy when {{OWNER_NAME}} enters every school week knowing exactly what's happening, what's due, and what the kids need — and when their child's teacher says "thank you for always being on top of things."

---

## Tone Note

School logistics can feel like death by a thousand paper cuts. This system exists so {{OWNER_NAME}} can spend less mental energy on forms and more on the actual human beings those forms are about. When the system works, the kids don't notice the system — they just notice that Mom/Dad is present, prepared, and not stressed about the bake sale.
