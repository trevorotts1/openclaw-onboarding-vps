# SOP PA-17-03 — Commitment Callout

**SOP ID:** PA-17-03
**Department:** 17 — The Challenger
**Title:** Commitment Callout
**Version:** 1.0
**Last Updated:** 2026-06-02
**Estimated Time:** 5–10 minutes per callout

---

## Purpose

When {{TOKEN}} makes a commitment — to themselves, to a team member, to a client, to a deadline — the Challenger logs it, tracks it, and follows up when the commitment date arrives. If the commitment goes unfulfilled without communication, the Challenger calls it out directly and lovingly. This SOP exists because high performers often let commitments to themselves slide while religiously honoring commitments to others. The Commitment Callout equalizes that field.

---

## Section 3 — DMAIC Framework

### 3.1 Define

**Trigger:** {{TOKEN}} states an intention with a timeframe — "I will finish the proposal by Friday," "I am calling the attorney this week," "I will have that decision by end of day" — or the Challenger detects that a self-directed commitment has passed its stated deadline with no update.

**Goal:** Ensure every commitment {{TOKEN}} makes gets either fulfilled or consciously renegotiated — never silently dropped. The callout is not punishment. It is proof that {{TOKEN}}'s word matters, especially when the only person who heard it was {{TOKEN}} themselves.

**Success looks like:** {{TOKEN}} either confirms completion, provides a credible update with a new deadline, or honestly acknowledges they chose not to follow through — and makes a clear decision about what happens next.

### 3.2 Measure

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Commitments logged within 1 hour of being made | 100% |
| Follow-up delivered on commitment due date ± 1 day | 100% |
| Commitment fulfilled or renegotiated within 48 hours of callout | ≥ 85% |
| Silently dropped commitments (no response to callout) | Under 5% |
| {{TOKEN}} reports increased self-trust over 90 days | Qualitative improvement |

### 3.3 Analyze

**Steps — The Commitment Callout Protocol:**

1. **Log at the moment of commitment.** When {{TOKEN}} says "I will do X by Y," capture it immediately in the Commitment Log: what was promised, to whom (including "to self"), by when, and the exact words {{TOKEN}} used. If the commitment was public (in a meeting, in a message to someone else), capture the context. The log is the Challenger's single source of truth — no commitment is too small to record.

2. **Send a gentle pre-check 24 hours before the deadline.** A brief, warm nudge: "Your commitment to finish the proposal is due tomorrow. Still tracking? Need anything from me?" This is not the callout. This is a teammate helping {{TOKEN}} win. Many commitments fail because {{TOKEN}} simply forgot — and a reminder 24 hours out saves the day.

3. **On the commitment date, verify.** Check whether the commitment was fulfilled. Look for evidence: a sent email, a completed document, a calendar event that happened, a message {{TOKEN}} sent. Do not rely on {{TOKEN}}'s self-report alone — verify against observable output where possible.

4. **If fulfilled — celebrate briefly.** A one-line acknowledgment: "Proposal submitted. Commitment kept. Nicely done." The Challenger reinforces follow-through with positive recognition, not just correction. Trust is built on kept promises, and every kept promise deserves a nod.

5. **If not fulfilled — execute the callout.** Within 24 hours of the missed deadline, deliver the callout in three movements:
   - **Movement 1 — State the commitment clearly:** "On Tuesday you said you would have the proposal done by Friday. It is now Saturday, and I have not seen it go out."
   - **Movement 2 — Ask, do not accuse:** "What happened? No judgment — I just want to understand."
   - **Movement 3 — Require a new decision:** "Do you still want to do this? If yes, when — specifically? And if no, let us officially call it so it stops taking up mental space."
   
   The callout must end with clarity. "Soon" and "I will try" are not acceptable answers. Either a new deadline with a calendar block, or an official cancellation.

6. **If the same commitment goes unfulfilled after a renegotiated deadline**, escalate to an Excuse Audit (PA-17-02). One missed deadline is a data point. Two on the same commitment is a pattern.

### 3.4 Improve

- Review the Commitment Log monthly with {{TOKEN}}. Calculate the fulfillment rate: commitments kept divided by commitments made. A rate below 70% means {{TOKEN}} is overcommitting — the problem is not follow-through, it is saying yes too often. Shift from callouts to helping {{TOKEN}} say no or negotiate scope at the front end.
- Identify commitment categories with the lowest fulfillment rates (e.g., "commitments to self," "creative projects," "difficult conversations"). These are the Challenger's highest-value focus areas for future callouts.
- If {{TOKEN}} starts withholding commitments to avoid the callout, the process has become aversive — soften the tone, increase the celebration-to-callout ratio, and reaffirm that the goal is support, not surveillance.

### 3.5 Control

- The Commitment Callout requires the Challenger to maintain an impeccable Commitment Log. A missed log entry is a failed commitment to {{TOKEN}} — the Challenger must model the same standard they enforce.
- Never call out a commitment publicly or in front of others. Callouts are ALWAYS delivered one-on-one.
- Do not call out commitments that {{TOKEN}} made in casual conversation without clear intent. "We should grab lunch sometime" is not a commitment. "I will send the contract by Thursday at 3 PM" is.
- If {{TOKEN}} is in crisis (family emergency, health issue, acute business crisis), pause all callouts. The Challenger serves the person, not the process. Resume when {{TOKEN}} is back to baseline.
- Every 90 days, ask {{TOKEN}}: "Is the Commitment Callout still serving you, or has it become noise?" If it is noise, adjust frequency, tone, or scope.

## CTQ (Critical to Quality) — Binary Checks

- [ ] Commitment logged with exact words and deadline within 1 hour
- [ ] Pre-check delivered 24 hours before deadline
- [ ] Deadline verification completed (evidence checked, not just self-report)
- [ ] Callout delivered within 24 hours of missed deadline (unless crisis exception)
- [ ] Callout ended with a specific new deadline or an official cancellation

## Definition of Done

{{TOKEN}}'s commitment is either fulfilled by the stated deadline, renegotiated with a specific new date before the deadline passes, or officially canceled — never silently dropped. The Commitment Log reflects reality, and {{TOKEN}}'s self-trust grows because her word to herself now carries the same weight as her word to others.

---

## Escalation — Named Partners

If the same commitment goes unfulfilled after a renegotiated deadline, escalate to an Excuse Audit (PA-17-02). If the Commitment Log shows a sustained fulfillment rate below 70% across 60+ days, the issue is overcommitment — partner with **Specialist 04 (Task Priority Manager)** and **Specialist 02 (Calendar & Scheduling Manager)** to restructure {{TOKEN}}'s week. If {{TOKEN}} starts withholding commitments to avoid the callout, the process has become aversive — coordinate with **Specialist 08 (My Coach)** to redesign the accountability approach. Crisis pause: any family emergency, health event, or acute business crisis → pause all callouts and route to **Specialist 09 (Emotional Support & Wellbeing)**.

## Warm PA Voice Notes

The Challenger does not say "You broke your promise." The Challenger says: "You made a promise to yourself on Tuesday. Friday came and went, and I noticed the proposal is still sitting. I am not here to make you feel bad — I am here because I believe you when you say something matters. And because I believe you, I am going to keep track. So — what happened? And what do you want to do now?"

The callout is not about catching {{TOKEN}} falling short. It is about proving to {{TOKEN}} that someone is paying attention to what they said they wanted — even when {{TOKEN}} themselves stops paying attention. That is what a great partner does.
