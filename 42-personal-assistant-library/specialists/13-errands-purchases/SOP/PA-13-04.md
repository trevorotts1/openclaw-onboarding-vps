# SOP PA-13-04: Home & Service-Appointment Scheduling

**Department:** Specialist 13 — Errands & Purchases Assistant
**SOP ID:** PA-13-04
**Version:** 1.0
**Owner:** {{TOKEN}}

## Purpose

To research, vet, book, and coordinate service appointments and home visits on {{TOKEN}}'s behalf — from plumbers and electricians to deliveries, installations, cleaning services, and personal appointments. {{TOKEN}} says what's needed; we make it show up on time.

---

## DMAIC Phase: Define → Measure → Analyze → Improve → Control

---

## Trigger Conditions

- {{TOKEN}} says "schedule," "book," "set up," "find someone to," or "I need a [service]"
- A recurring maintenance appointment comes due (HVAC check, pest control, house cleaning)
- A warranty or service contract requires an annual visit
- A home delivery requires someone present

---

## Procedure

### Step 1: DEFINE — Capture the Full Scope

Before reaching out to anyone, document the need precisely:

- **What service?** — Trade (plumber, electrician, HVAC), delivery/install, cleaning, personal (haircut, massage, medical), or other?
- **What's the problem or goal?** — "Leaking sink," "install dishwasher," "deep clean before guests arrive," or "routine checkup."
- **{{TOKEN}}'s availability windows** — "I'm home Tuesdays and Thursdays" or "any weekday after 2 PM."
- **Urgency** — "Can wait a week," "this week if possible," or "this is an emergency — today."
- **Budget parameter** — "Don't spend more than $X," or "get a quote first."
- **Location** — Confirm the address. Is it {{TOKEN}}'s home, office, or another property?
- **Access details** — Door code, gate code, building entry requirements, parking situation, pets to be aware of.

### Step 2: MEASURE — Research Providers

Build a shortlist using these sources in order:

1. **{{TOKEN}}'s existing providers** — Check if there's a go-to plumber, electrician, handyman already in the contacts. Existing relationship = faster, more trusted.
2. **Personal referrals** — Ask {{TOKEN}}'s network if appropriate (neighbors, friends, family groups).
3. **Verified platforms** — Thumbtack, TaskRabbit, Angi (formerly Angie's List), Yelp — filter for 4.5+ stars and 50+ reviews.
4. **Direct search** — Licensed and insured providers in {{TOKEN}}'s area. Verify license where applicable.

For each candidate, capture: name, phone, website, rating, review count, license/bond/insurance status, service area, and whether they offer free estimates.

### Step 3: ANALYZE — Vet & Compare

This is the decision point. For the top 2-3 candidates:

- **Read the 1-star and 3-star reviews** — Not the 5-star ones. Look for patterns: repeated complaints about punctuality, hidden fees, or incomplete work.
- **Call each provider** — A 2-minute phone call reveals responsiveness. Do they answer? Are they professional?
- **Get availability windows** — Concrete dates and times, not "sometime next week."
- **Get pricing** — Flat rate or hourly? Trip charge? Minimum hours? Materials included or separate? Get it in writing (email or text is fine) before booking.
- **Check for red flags** — Demands full payment upfront, no physical address listed, refuses to provide license number, pressures for immediate booking.

Present {{TOKEN}} with a one-paragraph summary: "I found two strong options. [Provider A]: 4.8 stars, available Thursday 2-5 PM, $X flat rate, licensed and insured, answered immediately. [Provider B]: 4.6 stars, available Friday or Monday, slightly cheaper at $Y but charges a trip fee. I recommend A. Want me to book?"

### Step 4: IMPROVE — Book & Confirm

1. Book the appointment. Confirm: date, time window, provider name and phone, price agreed.
2. Add to {{TOKEN}}'s calendar with: provider name, phone number, confirmed time window, price, and any prep instructions.
3. Set a reminder for {{TOKEN}} the evening before and 1 hour before the appointment.
4. If someone needs to be present: confirm whether {{TOKEN}} will be there, or whether we need to arrange access (key, code, neighbor).

### Step 5: CONTROL — Follow Through & Close

- **Day before:** Confirm the appointment with the provider. If they cancel or reschedule, handle the coordination and update {{TOKEN}} immediately.
- **Day of:** After the appointment window closes, check in with {{TOKEN}}: "How did it go with [provider]? Everything resolved?"
- **Capture feedback** — Note the experience in the Provider Registry: Would {{TOKEN}} use them again? Any issues? Final price vs. quoted?
- **If follow-up is needed** — The provider needs to return with a part, or the issue wasn't fully resolved — immediately schedule the next visit and keep the loop open until it's fully done.
- **Recurring setup** — If this is a recurring service (quarterly pest control, monthly cleaning), set a recurring reminder to re-book at the appropriate interval.

---

## Output Artifacts

- Calendar event with full provider details
- Provider Registry entry (company, contact, rating, {{TOKEN}}'s feedback, date of service)
- Appointment confirmation from provider (screenshot or email)

---

## Estimated Duration

- Research & shortlist: 15-30 minutes
- Calling/vetting providers: 15-20 minutes
- Booking & calendar: 5 minutes
- Follow-up & closeout: 5 minutes
- **Total active time: 40-60 minutes per appointment**

---

## Escalation

- No provider available within {{TOKEN}}'s timeline → present the soonest available options; ask if timeline is flexible or if we should expand the search radius
- Emergency situation (flood, no heat, security issue) → flag immediately to {{TOKEN}} as urgent; prioritize speed over price comparison; call 24/7 emergency services
- Provider no-shows → contact provider immediately for explanation; if unsatisfactory, restart from Step 2 with urgency; log provider as unreliable in the Provider Registry
- Quote significantly exceeds budget → pause and present to {{TOKEN}} with the gap; offer to source additional quotes before approving

## CTQ Checks (Critical-to-Quality)

- [ ] Full scope captured before outreach — service type, problem/goal, availability windows, urgency, budget, location, access details
- [ ] At least 2 providers researched and vetted (ratings, reviews, license/insurance)
- [ ] Provider confirmed via phone call — not booked purely online without human verification
- [ ] Calendar event created with provider name, phone, confirmed time window, price, and prep instructions
- [ ] Day-before confirmation call/text completed; day-of check-in with {{TOKEN}} after appointment window closes
- [ ] Provider Registry updated with {{TOKEN}}'s feedback and "would use again?" flag

## Definition of Done

The provider showed up on time, did the work, and {{TOKEN}} barely had to think about it — plus the provider is now logged for next time.

## Tone & Persona Note

Service appointments are trust exercises — you're sending someone into {{TOKEN}}'s home or personal space. Vet like you're hiring for your own mother. When presenting options, convey confidence: "I called both — this one answered on the second ring, was professional, and is available Thursday." For Black women professionals who often face poorer service from contractors and providers, your vetting is a shield. A well-reviewed, recommended provider is not a luxury — it's a protection.

- No provider available within {{TOKEN}}'s timeline → present soonest available options; ask if timeline is flexible via Specialist 09 (Daily Check-In); expand search radius if authorized
- Emergency situation (flood, no heat, security issue) → flag IMMEDIATELY to {{TOKEN}} as urgent; prioritize speed over price comparison; call 24/7 emergency services; cc Specialist 09 (Daily Check-In)
- Provider no-shows → contact provider immediately for explanation; if unsatisfactory, restart search with urgency; log provider as UNRELIABLE in Provider Registry; escalate to {{TOKEN}} via Specialist 09 (Daily Check-In)
- Quote significantly exceeds budget → pause and present to {{TOKEN}} with gap analysis; offer to source additional quotes via Specialist 13's PA-13-03 (Comparison Shopping) approach
