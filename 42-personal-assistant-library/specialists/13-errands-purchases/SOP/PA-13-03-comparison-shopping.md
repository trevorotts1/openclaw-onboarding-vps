# SOP PA-13-03: Comparison Shopping

**Department:** Specialist 13 — Errands & Purchases Assistant
**SOP ID:** PA-13-03
**Version:** 1.0
**Owner:** {{TOKEN}}

---

## Purpose

To research, compare, and recommend the best purchase option when {{TOKEN}} knows what they want but hasn't decided where or which one to buy. We do the legwork so {{TOKEN}} makes one confident decision, not fifteen browser tabs of indecision.

---

## DMAIC Phase: Define → Measure → Analyze → Improve → Control

---

## Trigger Conditions

- {{TOKEN}} says "find me the best," "what's a good," "compare prices on," or "is there a better deal on"
- {{TOKEN}} sends a product category or description: "I need a good [X] — something reliable, around $Y"
- A purchase request from PA-13-01 is paused because {{TOKEN}} wants price validation before ordering

---

## Procedure

### Step 1: DEFINE — Know Exactly What We're Shopping For

Clarify with {{TOKEN}} before opening a single tab:

- **What is it?** — Specific model, or a category ("a carry-on suitcase")? If a category, narrow it: "Hard shell or soft? Spinner wheels? Any must-have features?"
- **Budget range** — "What's your sweet spot, and is there a hard ceiling?"
- **Must-haves vs. dealbreakers** — "Anything it absolutely must have? Anything that would make you say no immediately?"
- **Timeline** — "When do you need it in hand?"
- **Preferences** — "Any retailers you prefer or avoid? Open to refurbished or open-box?"

### Step 2: MEASURE — Cast a Wide Net

Research across multiple channels. Minimum sources:

- **Major retailers** — Amazon, Walmart, Target, Best Buy, Costco, and category-specific leaders
- **Direct from brand** — Manufacturer's own site (often best warranty, sometimes best price)
- **Price comparison engines** — Google Shopping, CamelCamelCamel (price history)
- **Refurbished/Open-box** — If {{TOKEN}} is open to it, check manufacturer refurbished programs, Back Market, Amazon Warehouse
- **Cashback/rewards portals** — Rakuten, Honey, credit card shopping portals

For each option, capture: seller, price, shipping cost, delivery ETA, warranty, return policy, and any active coupon codes.

### Step 3: ANALYZE — Compare Across Dimensions

This is the core of the SOP. Build a comparison matrix weighing:

| Factor | Weight |
|--------|--------|
| Price (total, including shipping + tax) | 35% |
| Delivery speed vs. {{TOKEN}}'s timeline | 20% |
| Return policy & warranty | 20% |
| Seller reputation & authenticity guarantee | 15% |
| Extras (rewards points, cashback, bundle deals) | 10% |

Key analysis questions:
- Is the lowest price from an authorized retailer? (Unauthorized = warranty may be void.)
- Is the price suspiciously low? (See PA-13-01 Step 3 red flag scan.)
- Does the price history show a pattern? Is this a good time to buy, or does it regularly drop lower?

### Step 4: IMPROVE — Present the Recommendation

Deliver a clean, one-glance summary to {{TOKEN}}:

"Here's what I found for [item]:

- **Top pick:** [Retailer] — $X total, delivers by [date], [return policy]. [One sentence why.]
- **Best value:** [Retailer] — $Y, slower delivery but saves $Z. [Trade-off note.]
- **Budget pick:** [Retailer] — $W, same item, [condition if not new].

I recommend the top pick because [key reason]. Want me to order, or would you prefer one of the others?"

Never present more than three options unless {{TOKEN}} asks for more.

### Step 5: CONTROL — Execute or Archive

- If {{TOKEN}} chooses: immediately execute via **PA-13-01**.
- If {{TOKEN}} says "not now": save the comparison in the Comparison Archive with date and notes, so it's ready when the need resurfaces.
- If the price drops after purchase and the retailer offers price matching: proactively file a price adjustment claim.

---

## Output Artifacts

- Three-option comparison summary (delivered to {{TOKEN}} as a message)
- Full research grid (saved to Comparison Archive for future reference)
- Price history graph if relevant (CamelCamelCamel screenshot)

---

## Estimated Duration

- Clarification: 5 minutes
- Research & price gathering: 15-30 minutes
- Analysis & recommendation write-up: 10 minutes
- **Total active time: 30-45 minutes per comparison**

---

## Escalation

- No options meet the budget → present the closest options and ask if budget is flexible
- Only one retailer carries the item → note limited availability; recommend acting quickly if intent is firm
- Conflicting reviews make quality uncertain → flag to {{TOKEN}} with review summary; suggest ordering from a retailer with easy returns as insurance

---

## CTQ Checks (Critical-to-Quality)

- [ ] {{TOKEN}}'s requirements clarified (what, budget, must-haves, timeline, preferences) before any research begins
- [ ] Minimum 4 sources researched: major retailers, direct from brand, price comparison engines, and at least one alternative channel (refurbished/cashback)
- [ ] Exactly 3 options presented — never 1, never more than 5
- [ ] Every option includes: seller, total price (with shipping + tax), delivery ETA, return policy, and one-sentence rationale
- [ ] Comparison delivered within the timeline {{TOKEN}} specified — or sooner

## Definition of Done

{{TOKEN}} sees three clear, well-researched options for exactly what they need, makes one confident choice, and never wonders "did I get the best deal?"

## Tone & Persona Note

Comparison shopping is liberation from the infinite scroll. You're saving {{TOKEN}} from the 15-tab purgatory where every option looks the same and decision fatigue takes over. Present options like a trusted friend who did the homework: "Here's what I'd pick and why — but you've got two other solid choices." For Black women professionals whose time is perpetually stretched, 30 minutes of your research saves her 2 hours of browser-tab paralysis. That's the value proposition.

## Escalation

- No options meet {{TOKEN}}'s budget → present the closest 3 options with specific gap noted ("closest is $X over"); ask if budget is flexible via Specialist 09 (Daily Check-In)
- Only one retailer carries the item → note limited availability; recommend acting quickly; escalate to Specialist 13's PA-13-01 (Order Execution) if {{TOKEN}} confirms
- Conflicting reviews make quality uncertain → flag to {{TOKEN}} with review summary; recommend ordering from retailer with easy returns — cc Specialist 13's PA-13-02 (Returns & Refunds) awareness
- Price dropped after purchase within retailer's price-match window → proactively file price adjustment claim; cc Specialist 11's PA-11-04 (Renewal Negotiation) for claim documentation

*Comparison shopping isn't about finding the cheapest option. It's about finding the smartest option — the one {{TOKEN}} won't second-guess later.*
