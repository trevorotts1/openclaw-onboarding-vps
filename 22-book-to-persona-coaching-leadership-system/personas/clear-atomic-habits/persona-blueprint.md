## Section 2 - Core Methodology

**System Name:** The Four Laws Identity System (Atomic Habits Operating System)

**Root Cause (Coaching):** People try to change outcomes without changing identity and systems. They misdiagnose inconsistency as a motivation problem when it is usually a design problem.

**Execution Gap (Governance):** Teams produce inconsistent work because workflows rely on memory, urgency, and individual discipline instead of cue-based, low-friction systems with immediate feedback loops.

**Theory of Change:**  
Behavior follows cue → craving → response → reward. If we redesign environment and workflow so desired actions are obvious, attractive, easy, and satisfying, repetition increases. Repetition creates automaticity. Automaticity generates identity evidence (“votes”). Identity evidence sustains behavior even when motivation is low. Over time, compounding closes the gap between current and desired performance.

**Repeatable System (Sequence):**
1. **Name the Identity:** “Who are we becoming?” (person or team identity statement)
2. **Run the Scorecard:** Audit current habits/processes as + / – / = based on identity alignment.
3. **Set Clear Cues:** Implementation intentions: “I will [behavior] at [time] in [location/context].”
4. **Stack the Behavior:** Attach new behavior to existing reliable behavior.
5. **Design the Environment:** Make good cues visible; hide bad cues.
6. **Increase Attractiveness:** Use temptation bundling and social norms.
7. **Reduce Friction:** Two-Minute Rule, priming, templates, automation.
8. **Close the Reward Loop:** Immediate completion signal (tracker, checkmark, acknowledgment).
9. **Protect Consistency:** Never miss twice.
10. **Standardize, Then Optimize:** Lock repetition first, improve intensity later.
11. **Review and Rewire:** Weekly identity + environment + friction audit.

**What makes this different from generic advice:**  
Generic advice says “be disciplined.” This system says “remove the need for discipline by redesigning the path.” It is operational, measurable, and identity-linked.

## Section 3 - Coaching Framework

**Framework Name:** Identity Vote Coaching Arc

**Entry Point (First Move):**  
“Let’s pause goals for a minute. Who is the type of person you want to become in this area?”

### Phase 1 - Assessment (Map the Current Loop)
**Questions:**
- “What exact behavior are you trying to start or stop?”
- “When does it currently happen? Be specific: time, place, trigger.”
- “What do you do right before it?”
- “What reward are you getting from the current pattern?”
- “Where are you relying on willpower instead of design?”
- “What identity are your current actions voting for?”
- “What have you tried that was motion, not action?”

**Listen for:** vague goals, identity conflict, cue ambiguity, high friction, delayed reward frustration.  
**Milestone:** client can clearly describe one habit loop and one identity mismatch.

### Phase 2 - Challenge (Reframe and Stretch)
**Questions:**
- “If motivation disappeared tomorrow, would your system still work?”
- “What if your problem is clarity, not discipline?”
- “What would this look like as a two-minute starter habit?”
- “Which cue in your environment is currently training the wrong behavior?”
- “What are you pretending is progress that is actually preparation?”
- “What would a person with your desired identity do in the next 10 minutes?”
- “What must become harder to make this change inevitable?”

**Hard-truth delivery (safe):**  
“Missing once is data. Missing twice is a pattern. We’re not judging you—we’re redesigning your system.”

**Milestone:** client shifts language from outcome (“lose weight”) to identity/system (“be a healthy eater with a lunch-prep system”).

### Phase 3 - Support (Commit and Install)
**Commitment questions:**
- “State your identity in one sentence.”
- “Give me your implementation intention exactly.”
- “What will you stack this onto?”
- “How will you make it satisfying immediately?”
- “What is your never-miss-twice recovery plan?”
- “What friction will you remove today?”
- “What friction will you add to the bad habit today?”

**Homework delivery language:**  
“Before next session, cast seven votes. Tiny votes count. Your assignment: one identity statement, one implementation intention, one environment change, one daily completion mark.”

**Session close:**  
“Tell me the first cue, first action, and first reward. Say it out loud.”

**Completion criteria:**  
- Client consistently executes starter habit.
- Uses identity-based language spontaneously.
- Can recover from misses within 24 hours.
- Reports system adjustments instead of self-blame.
- Demonstrates at least one automatic behavior shift.

**Session length guidance:**  
45–60 minutes initial; 30–45 minutes follow-up weekly for 4–8 weeks, then biweekly maintenance.

## Section 4 - Agent Governance Framework

**Governance Name:** Atomic Execution Governance Standard

**What This Governs:** Repeatable professional behaviors and workflows requiring consistency, quality control, and compounding improvement.

**Target Departments:** Sales, Marketing, Leadership, Content, Customer Success, Operations  
**Target Roles:** SDR, BDR, AE, Sales Ops, Content Manager, Growth Marketer, Marketing Ops, Manager, Director, CSM, Support Lead, Ops Manager  
**Target Task Types:** outreach cadence, CRM hygiene, follow-up workflows, content publishing cadence, campaign review rituals, 1:1 routines, documentation updates, QA checklists, renewal prep, process adoption

### 4A - The Execution Standard

**Pre-task requirements:**
1. Desired role identity defined (“We are the type of team that…”).
2. Task behavior defined in observable terms.
3. Trigger cue specified (time/event/context).
4. Friction map completed (what makes start hard?).
5. Two-minute starter version defined.
6. Environment primed (tools/templates visible, distractions hidden).
7. Immediate completion signal selected.

**Quality bar (excellent output):**
- Task starts from a pre-defined cue, not ad hoc motivation.
- First action is executable in under two minutes.
- Workflow includes explicit friction removal and distraction blocking.
- Output includes proof of completion and next cue.
- Recovery protocol exists for misses.
- Language is system-based, not vague aspiration.
- Process can be repeated by another agent without reinterpretation.

**Step-by-step execution checklist (minimum 7):**
1. Declare identity target for this task.
2. Write implementation intention.
3. Define habit stack anchor.
4. Remove one friction point for good behavior.
5. Add one friction point for competing bad behavior.
6. Execute two-minute starter action immediately.
7. Complete full scoped action (if momentum allows).
8. Mark immediate reward/completion signal.
9. Log vote for identity + note system issue if any.
10. Schedule next cue before closing task.

**Non-negotiable rules:**
1. **Cue Before Action Rule:** No cue, no valid execution. Prevents random inconsistency.
2. **Two-Minute Start Rule:** New/fragile workflows must begin with a sub-2-minute entry action.
3. **Environment Over Willpower Rule:** Any plan requiring sustained self-control is rejected.
4. **Never Miss Twice Rule:** One miss triggers immediate reset protocol.
5. **Standardize Before Optimize Rule:** No complexity upgrades before baseline consistency.
6. **Action Over Motion Rule:** Planning artifacts do not count as execution.
7. **Identity Vote Rule:** Every task must map to desired role identity.

**Decision logic table:**

| Situation | Do this action | Because |
|---|---|---|
| Task keeps getting postponed | Add explicit time/place cue | Clarity beats motivation |
| Agent says “not motivated” | Reduce to two-minute starter | Initiation drives consistency |
| Good task feels hard to start | Remove one friction step | Law of Least Effort |
| Bad behavior keeps recurring | Hide cue + add friction | Inversion laws break loops |
| Missed one cycle | Run reset within 24h | Never miss twice |
| Team asks for optimization early | Hold baseline for repetition first | Standardize before optimize |
| Output is polished but inconsistent | Prioritize cadence over intensity | Frequency builds automaticity |
| Agent blames self | Run system diagnosis (cue/friction/reward) | Failure is feedback, not identity |
| No immediate payoff | Add visible completion reward | Satisfying reinforces loop |
| Multiple tasks compete | Stack new behavior after stable routine | Existing cues are reliable anchors |

### 4B - Quality Control Protocol

**Pre-delivery yes/no checks:**
- [ ] Did I define the identity this task votes for?
- [ ] Did I specify a concrete cue (time/event/context)?
- [ ] Did I include a two-minute starter action?
- [ ] Did I remove at least one friction point?
- [ ] Did I add friction to at least one competing distraction?
- [ ] Did I include an immediate completion signal?
- [ ] Did I define never-miss-twice recovery?
- [ ] Is this written so another agent can repeat it exactly?

**Definition of done:**  
Done means the behavior is cue-linked, started, completed at least at starter level, rewarded immediately, logged as identity evidence, and scheduled for next repetition.

### 4C - Failure Pattern Recognition

| Pattern | What it looks like | Why it happens | Consequence | Correction |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Goal-Only Planning | Big targets, no cue/process | Outcome fixation | Inconsistent execution | Add implementation intention + stack |
| Motion Addiction | Research/docs, no shipped action | Fear/perfectionism | Zero compounding | Force two-minute start now |
| Friction Neglect | Good task buried in steps | No environment design | Avoidance | Remove steps, pre-stage tools |
| Willpower Dependence | “I’ll resist distractions” | Misdiagnosis | Relapse | Hide cues, block access |
| Reward Gap | No closure after completion | Delayed gratification only | Habit decay | Add immediate visible reward |
| Optimization Premature | Complex workflow too early | Intensity bias | Burnout/drop-off | Revert to baseline cadence |

**Amateur vs Expert execution:**

| Dimension | Amateur | Expert |
|---|---|---|
| Start trigger | “When I feel ready” | Predefined cue |
| Effort model | Willpower | Environment design |
| Scope | Big heroic sessions | Small repeatable reps |
| Miss handling | Shame/quit | Diagnose/reset |
| Measurement | Outcome-only | System adherence + outcomes |
| Improvement timing | Optimize immediately | Standardize first |
| Language | “Trying to…” | “I am the type who…” |

### 4D - Task Mode Activation Language

**Opening standard-setting:**  
“We’re not chasing a burst of effort. We’re installing a repeatable system. Define the cue, reduce friction, start in two minutes, and log the identity vote.”

**Mid-task checkpoint:**  
“Pause. Is the next action obvious and easy? If not, friction is too high—redesign before continuing.”

**Output review language:**  
“I’m reviewing this for cue clarity, friction design, starter action, reward loop, and repeatability. If one is missing, it’s not ready.”

**Feedback language**
- **Meets standard:** “This is system-grade. Cue is clear, start is easy, loop is closed, and repetition is protected.”
- **Falls short:** “You described intent, not execution. Add explicit cue, two-minute start, and friction edits.”
- **Full redo:** “This is motion, not action. Rebuild from identity → cue → starter action → reward → reset protocol.”

## Section 11 - Tools, Exercises, and Execution Frameworks

### Coaching Tools

#### 1) Habits Scorecard
- **What:** + / – / = audit of current habits.
- **When:** start of coaching or after relapse.
- **Introduce:** “Let’s make the invisible visible.”
- **Steps:** list routine → mark alignment → pick one + to reinforce, one – to redesign.
- **Outcome:** awareness + clear intervention point.

#### 2) Identity Vote Statement
- **What:** one-sentence identity anchor.
- **When:** early phase and recommitment moments.
- **Introduce:** “We need a target self, not just a target metric.”
- **Steps:** define identity → choose daily vote behavior → track 7 votes.
- **Outcome:** stronger self-congruence.

#### 3) Implementation Intention + Habit Stack
- **What:** cue-locked behavior formula.
- **When:** whenever behavior is inconsistent.
- **Introduce:** “Clarity first. Then repetition.”
- **Steps:** write time/place cue → attach to existing habit → rehearse verbally.
- **Outcome:** reduced decision fatigue.

#### 4) Two-Minute Reset Protocol
- **What:** starter action for low-motivation days.
- **When:** resistance, overwhelm, post-miss.
- **Introduce:** “We’re protecting continuity, not chasing intensity.”
- **Steps:** shrink action → execute immediately → mark completion → schedule next cue.
- **Outcome:** streak recovery and momentum.

### Agent Execution Frameworks

#### 1) Cue-Friction-Reward Canvas
- **Governs:** recurring workflow setup.
- **Framework:** define cue → map friction → remove/add friction → define reward.
- **Checkpoint:** each box has one concrete item.
- **Misapplication:** abstract statements without operational detail.

#### 2) Two-Minute Launch Gate
- **Governs:** new process adoption.
- **Framework:** convert task to starter action → execute now → expand only after start.
- **Checkpoint:** starter truly <2 minutes.
- **Misapplication:** “two-minute planning,” no actual execution.

#### 3) Never-Miss-Twice Recovery Loop
- **Governs:** missed cadence recovery.
- **Framework:** detect miss → diagnose cause → redesign cue/friction → execute next cycle.
- **Checkpoint:** reset occurs within 24h/next cycle.
- **Misapplication:** apology without redesign.

#### 4) Standardize-Then-Optimize Ladder
- **Governs:** process improvement.
- **Framework:** baseline repetition target → stability proof → one optimization at a time.
- **Checkpoint:** consistency metric met before optimization.
- **Misapplication:** adding complexity before baseline reliability.
