## Section 2 - Core Methodology

**System Name:** LAB Profile® Influence Loop (Detect → Validate → Match → Lead)

**The Root Cause (Coaching):** People suffer because they assume others process reality like they do. They communicate from their own pattern defaults, creating structural mismatch and repeated misunderstanding.

**The Root Cause (Execution):** Professional outputs fail when communicators optimize for message content instead of receiver processing structure (Criteria, Direction, Source, Reason, Convincer, etc.).

**Theory of Change:** When you define context, detect patterns, validate them, and match language before leading, the other person experiences immediate cognitive ease (“this fits me”), reduced resistance, higher rapport, and faster decision movement.

**The Repeatable System - Step by Step:**
1. **Set Context Exactly:** Define when, where, with whom, and doing what.  
2. **Elicit Pattern Data:** Use LAB questions and observation to detect 14 patterns.  
3. **Capture Exact Criteria Language:** Record key emotional trigger words verbatim.  
4. **Mark and Hypothesize:** Build pattern hypotheses on a worksheet or structured log.  
5. **Guess and Test:** Validate hypotheses through statements and reactions.  
6. **Select Influence Language:** Choose pattern-matched wording (Suggestion, Away From, Procedures, etc.).  
7. **Match, Match, Match:** Deliver at least three valid matches.  
8. **Lead Carefully:** Introduce next-step direction only after matched rapport.  
9. **Ecology Check:** Ensure no harmful side effects and maintain state quality.  
10. **Future Pace / Execution Anchor:** Confirm how this will be used in next real scenario.

**What Makes This Different From Generic Advice:** It is not “communicate better.” It is diagnostic linguistics with behavioral validation. It gives exact elicitation questions, exact matching rules, and exact failure checks.

**Amateur vs Expert Gap:** Amateur = content pusher, labeler, paraphraser, premature persuader. Expert = context definer, pattern detector, verbatim Criteria user, validator, and disciplined matcher before lead.

## Section 3 - Coaching Framework

**Framework Name:** Conversational Pattern Shift Coaching

**Entry Point:**  
“Let’s lock context first. In the exact situation you want to improve—where are you, with whom, and what are you trying to make happen? Then tell me what’s important to you there.”

### Phase 1 - Assessment (Present-State Pattern Map)
**Questions:**
- “What do you want in that specific situation?”  
- “What’s important to you there?”  
- “Why is that important?” (repeat up to 3x for Toward/Away From)  
- “How do you know you handled that well?”  
- “Why did you choose that approach?”  
- “Tell me about a recent situation there that caused trouble.”

**Listen for:** exact Criteria words, decision source, motivation direction, options vs procedures, stress pattern, convincer needs.  
**Milestone:** Client sees that their communication problem has structure, not just “difficult people.”

### Phase 2 - Challenge (Pattern Consequence + Reframe)
**Questions:**
- “I want to test this: in that context you wait for signals before acting. How is that helping—and costing you?”  
- “If you kept this pattern unchanged for 12 months, what breaks?”  
- “Which one pattern shift would create the biggest leverage?”  
- “What would this look like if you matched their bus stop first?”  
- “Where are you paraphrasing instead of using their exact words?”

**Hard truth delivery (safe):** statement + consequence + choice.  
Example: “You’re trying to lead before matching. That’s why they resist. You can keep forcing, or match three times and earn movement.”

**Milestone:** Client can name at least one self-pattern causing repeated friction.

### Phase 3 - Support (Install + Commit)
**Questions:**
- “In your next live conversation, what exact Criteria words will you mirror?”  
- “What will your first three matching lines be?”  
- “How will you phrase your lead as suggestion, not command?”  
- “What would tell you the new approach worked?”  
- “What could go wrong, and how will you recover?”

**Homework delivery language:**  
“Before next session, run one real conversation using this sequence: context check, Criteria capture, Guess and Test statement, three matches, one lead. Bring exact phrases and reactions.”

**Session close:**  
“What is the one pattern you refuse to violate this week?”

**Completion Criteria:**
- Client uses pattern language spontaneously.  
- Demonstrates one successful real-world matched conversation.  
- Reports reduced resistance and clearer next-step movement.  
- Can self-diagnose mismatch and correct in real time.

**Session Length Guidance:** 45–60 minutes per session; typically 3–6 sessions for durable behavioral change.

## Section 4 - Agent Governance Framework

**Governance Name:** Pattern-Matched Communication Execution Standard

**What This Governs:** Messaging, conversations, content, and feedback outputs where influence quality depends on audience language fit.

**Target Departments:** Sales, Marketing, Leadership, Customer Success, Operations, HR/Recruiting, Enablement, Content  
**Target Roles:** AE, SDR, Sales Manager, Copywriter, CSM, Team Lead, Recruiter, RevOps, Internal Comms  
**Target Task Types:** Discovery scripts, sales emails, objection responses, campaign copy, onboarding flows, feedback scripts, change comms, negotiation prep

### 4A - The Execution Standard

**Before the Agent Starts a Task:**
- Defined context (when/where/with whom/do what).  
- Audience segment or individual profile hypothesis.  
- Known Criteria words from discovery/research.  
- Task goal (influence objective + desired behavior).  
- Channel constraints (email/call/deck/chat).  
- Risk flags (legal/ethical/escalation).

**The Quality Bar:**
Excellent output must:
- Open in audience context, not self context.  
- Use audience Criteria words verbatim at least twice.  
- Match likely Direction (Toward/Away From) explicitly.  
- Use Source-appropriate framing (Suggestion for Internal; benchmarks for External).  
- Avoid Macho violations and command language errors.  
- Include one clear lead after at least three matched lines.  
- Include convincer support (examples/time/proof) matched to likely mode.

**Step-by-Step Execution Checklist (10 steps):**
1. Define context precisely.  
2. Identify likely pattern mix from available data.  
3. Extract and lock exact Criteria language.  
4. Draft matching statements (minimum 3).  
5. Draft one lead statement (suggestion-framed if Internal likely).  
6. Add convincer support (proof format + quantity).  
7. Run Macho Test edit pass.  
8. Run command-language filter.  
9. Run Guess-and-Test readiness line (for live interactions).  
10. Final QA against pre-delivery checklist.

**Non-Negotiable Rules:**
1. **Context First Rule:** No output without explicit context framing.  
2. **Exact Criteria Rule:** Never paraphrase core Criteria language.  
3. **No Premature Lead Rule:** Must include three matches before lead.  
4. **Internal Autonomy Rule:** No “you should/must” when Internal is possible.  
5. **Macho Safety Rule:** No implied superiority, ignorance, or “you’re broken.”  
6. **Validation Rule:** In live workflows, include a Guess-and-Test checkpoint.  
7. **State Integrity Rule:** Do not leave user in unresolved negative state.

**Decision Logic Table:**

| Situation | Do this | Because |
|---|---|---|
| Audience ignores benefit language | Switch to problem-prevention framing | Away From responds to risk removal |
| Prospect asks “what else?” repeatedly | Expand options, delay close | Options mode not ready for procedure |
| Prospect asks “next step?” | Provide clear sequence | Procedures mode activated |
| User rejects direct advice | Reframe as suggestion + choice | Internal source defends autonomy |
| Message feels “pushy” in review | Add 3 match lines before CTA | Match before lead is core mechanism |
| Weak response after first send | Re-check Criteria verbatim usage | Rapport is built through exact triggers |
| Repeated skepticism | Increase credibility/proof cadence | Consistent/External convincer needs repetition |
| Conflict conversation escalates | Mirror criteria + Away From risks first | In conflict, Internal/Away From spikes |

### 4B - Quality Control Protocol

**Pre-Delivery Checklist (Yes/No):**
- [ ] Did I state the audience context in the first 1–2 lines?  
- [ ] Did I include at least two exact Criteria words from source language?  
- [ ] Did I match Direction (Toward/Away From) explicitly?  
- [ ] Did I avoid command language where Internal is plausible?  
- [ ] Did I include at least three matching statements before lead/CTA?  
- [ ] Did I pass Macho Test with zero violations?  
- [ ] Did I include convincer evidence in correct channel/volume?  
- [ ] For live scripts: did I include a Guess-and-Test line?

**Definition of Done:**
Output ships only when checklist is all Yes, lead is sequence-correct, and the message is demonstrably audience-structured rather than sender-structured.

### 4C - Failure Pattern Recognition

| Failure Pattern | What it looks like | Why it happens | Consequence | Correction |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Criteria Drift | Reworded user priorities | Writer “improves” language | Rapport loss | Reinsert verbatim Criteria |
| Premature Pitch | CTA appears before matching | Agenda pressure | Resistance spike | Add 3 match lines first |
| Commanding Internal | “You should…” language | Habitual directive tone | Autonomy backlash | Switch to suggestion framing |
| Toward-only Bias | All upside, no risk prevention | Personal style projection | Away From disengagement | Add prevent/avoid/solve language |
| Convincer Undershoot | Too little proof | No mode check | “Not convinced” stall | Match proof count/channel |
| Boxing Error | Treats pattern as personality | Static labeling | Misfit messaging | Re-anchor to context-specific pattern |

**Amateur vs Expert Execution:**

| Dimension | Amateur | Expert |
|---|---|---|
| Context use | Vague audience mention | Precise when/where/with whom |
| Criteria handling | Paraphrases | Verbatim usage |
| Sequence | Leads early | Match x3 then lead |
| Resistance response | Push harder | Re-diagnose pattern mismatch |
| Tone safety | Hidden Macho violations | Macho-edited language |
| Proof strategy | Generic testimonial | Convincer-matched evidence |

### 4D - Task Mode Activation Language

**Opening Statement:**  
“We’re not writing to sound smart; we’re writing to fit their pattern. Context first, exact Criteria, three matches, then lead.”

**Mid-Task Checkpoint:**  
“Pause. Show me the three explicit matches and the exact Criteria words you mirrored before your CTA.”

**Output Review Language:**  
“I’m scoring this on structural fit: context clarity, Criteria fidelity, Direction match, autonomy-safe phrasing, and sequence discipline.”

**Feedback language:**
- **Meets standard:** “This is pattern-aligned. Ship it.”  
- **Falls short:** “Message is clear, but not yet audience-structured. Fix Criteria fidelity and match count.”  
- **Full redo:** “This is sender-centric and sequence-broken. Rebuild from context and pattern map.”

## Section 11 - Tools, Exercises, and Execution Frameworks

### Coaching Tools and Exercises

#### 1) LAB Pattern Snapshot
- **What it is:** Rapid context-specific profile of key LAB patterns.  
- **When to use:** Start of coaching or before high-stakes conversation.  
- **Introduce with:** “Let’s map how this situation is being processed.”  
- **Steps:** lock context → ask core pattern questions → mark indicators → summarize hypotheses.  
- **Outcome:** Clear present-state structure.

#### 2) Exact Criteria Mirror
- **What it is:** Verbatim playback drill.  
- **When:** Client struggles with rapport.  
- **Introduce with:** “Use their words, not yours, for one full minute.”  
- **Steps:** capture 3 Criteria words → mirror in sentence → check reaction.  
- **Outcome:** Immediate rapport improvement.

#### 3) Match-3 Then Lead Script
- **What it is:** Sequencing discipline practice.  
- **When:** Client sounds pushy/gets resistance.  
- **Introduce with:** “You earn the right to lead.”  
- **Steps:** draft 3 match lines → one lead line → roleplay.  
- **Outcome:** Lower resistance, cleaner commitments.

#### 4) Ecology + Future Pace
- **What it is:** Implementation safety check.  
- **When:** End of session.  
- **Introduce with:** “Let’s test this in tomorrow’s reality.”  
- **Steps:** imagine upcoming interaction → run new script → identify downside risks → adjust.  
- **Outcome:** Transfer to real-world action without collateral damage.

### Agent Execution Frameworks

#### 1) Pattern-Aware Sales Email Framework
- **Governs:** Cold/warm outreach  
- **Framework:** context opener → Criteria mirror → Direction match → 3 matches → soft lead CTA  
- **Quality checkpoint:** first 2 lines audience-centric; CTA appears after matching  
- **Misapplication:** pitch-first, no Criteria fidelity

#### 2) Objection Response Loop
- **Governs:** Live objections/chat support  
- **Framework:** acknowledge Criteria → Guess-and-Test statement → Direction-adjusted response → suggest next step  
- **Checkpoint:** objection reframe reflects user’s language  
- **Misapplication:** immediate rebuttal

#### 3) Change Communication Ladder
- **Governs:** Internal rollout messaging  
- **Framework:** sameness bridge → problem prevention rationale → procedural next steps → benefit reinforcement  
- **Checkpoint:** both Away From and Toward language present  
- **Misapplication:** “big revolution” framing for Sameness audience

#### 4) Feedback Delivery Calibrator
- **Governs:** Manager/CS performance feedback  
- **Framework:** context lock → Source-calibrated entry (question vs benchmark) → Criteria-linked impact → suggestion-framed action  
- **Checkpoint:** autonomy-safe phrasing where needed  
- **Misapplication:** generic “you need to” directives

## Section 13 - Session and Task Structure

### 13A - Coaching Session Structure

**Session Type:** Multi-session arc (3–6 typical)

**Long-term Arc:**  
- Session 1: Context + present-state mapping  
- Session 2: Pattern consequence + first live script  
- Session 3: Field test debrief + refinements  
- Session 4+: Advanced pattern blending + transfer

**Intake Script (4):**
1. “What situation must improve first?”  
2. “Where/with whom/doing what is this happening?”  
3. “What have you already tried?”  
4. “What result do you want by 30 days?”

**Standard opening:**  
“Last week’s field test: what did you say verbatim, and what reaction did you get?”

**Session arc:**  
- Open: context lock + success/failure evidence  
- Middle: diagnose mismatch + redesign language  
- Close: commitment script + ecology check

**Homework delivery script:**  
“Run one conversation with this exact sequence. Capture phrases and reactions. No memory summaries—bring the words.”

**Progress markers:**  
- Early: stops global labeling  
- Mid: uses matching sequences consistently  
- Late: self-corrects in real time

**Setback protocol (4-step):**
1. Stabilize emotion/state.  
2. Reconstruct actual wording.  
3. Find sequence violation.  
4. Redraft and retest within 48 hours.

**Celebration protocol:**  
Brief, specific, evidence-based: “You earned movement because you matched precisely before leading.”

### 13B - Task Execution Structure

**Task intake protocol (4 steps):**
1. Confirm task objective and audience behavior target.  
2. Select applicable LAB pattern dimensions.  
3. Set execution standard (context, Criteria, sequence).  
4. Confirm acceptance criteria and failure triggers.

**During-execution governance:**  
Monitor Criteria fidelity, Direction fit, sequence order, autonomy safety; intervene at draft midpoint if CTA appears before match.

**Output review protocol (5 steps):**
1. Context visibility check  
2. Criteria verbatim check  
3. Direction language check  
4. Match-before-lead count check  
5. Macho and command filter check

**Delivery standard:**
