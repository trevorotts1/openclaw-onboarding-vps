### 2. The Central Problem
The central problem is poor communication (or a lack of it altogether), which creates huge problems at work, at home, and in the world at large. Discord among people is frequent, ranging from small annoyances to lifelong resentments to complex conflicts between nations. The specific issue is that most people, particularly successful people, use the same strategy with others that they used to convince themselves, failing to recognize that "other people are different." People communicate through filters shaped by history, sense of identity, beliefs about what is true, and values about what is right, creating misunderstanding. Many psychometric assessments stick people in boxes with labels, failing to recognize people's inherent flexibility to shift thinking and behavior as situations change. The person who needs this book suffers from failed communication attempts, inability to predict or influence others' behavior, and frustration when others don't respond as expected.

### 3. The Root Cause of Human Struggle
The root cause is the failure to recognize that people inhabit different "Models of the World" created through the mental filtering processes of Deletion, Distortion, and Generalization. As Noam Chomsky identified, these three processes create individual filters that determine what information people let in and what they keep out. The author states: "From Noam Chomsky and many others, we know that people do not actually live in Reality. By deleting, distorting and generalizing, we inhabit our perceptions and interpretations of Reality." Additionally, Rodger Bailey's work revealed that people use specific Meta Programs (or LAB Profile® Patterns) that function as doors through which they interact with the world. The real underlying reason people struggle is that they unconsciously assume others share their same patterns, leading to mismatched communication: "Most people, particularly successful people, use the same strategy with others that they used to convince themselves to believe or do something, but.... other people are different!"

### 4. The Complete Methodology - Every Step
The LAB Profile® methodology consists of the following repeatable steps:

1. **Establish Context**: Identify the specific frame of reference (When, Where, With whom, and a verb) before assessing patterns, as patterns shift based on Context.

2. **Detect the 14 Patterns**: Elicit information using specific questions or conversational observation to detect patterns across two categories:
   - **Motivation Patterns (6)**:
     * Level (Proactive/Reactive): Listen for sentence structure and body language
     * Criteria (Hot Buttons): Ask "What do you want in...?" or "What's important to you?"
     * Direction (Toward/Away From): Ask "Why is having that important?" up to 3 times
     * Source (Internal/External): Ask "How do you know you've done a good job?"
     * Reason (Options/Procedures): Ask "Why did you choose your present work?"
     * Decision Factors (Sameness/Sameness with Exception/Difference): Ask "What is the relationship between your work this year and last year?"
   - **Productivity Patterns (8)**:
     * Scope (Specific/General): No question; observe sentence structure (details vs. summaries)
     * Attention Direction (Self/Other): No verbal question; test with "pencil drop" or observe non-verbal responses
     * Stress Response (Feeling/Choice/Thinking): Ask "Tell me about a work situation that caused you trouble"
     * Style (Independent/Proximity/Cooperative): Ask "Tell me about a work experience that was [Criteria]. What did you like about it?"
     * Organization (Person/Thing): Ask same as Style question, listening to whether they mention people or tasks
     * Rule Structure (My/My, My/., No/My, My/Your): Ask "What is a good way for you to increase your success?" and "What is a good way for someone else to increase their success?"
     * Convincer Channel (See/Hear/Read/Do): Ask "How do you know someone else is good at their work?"
     * Convincer Mode (Number of Examples/Automatic/Consistent/Period of Time): Ask "How many times do they have to demonstrate this before you are convinced?"

3. **Mark the Worksheet**: Record indicators on the LAB Profile® Worksheet, marking frequency of observed patterns.

4. **Guess and Test**: Validate the diagnosis by giving feedback using statements (not questions) describing the behaviors of each pattern, and observing the person's reaction (nodding, confusion, negation).

5. **Select Influencing Language**: Choose specific words and phrases that match the detected patterns.

6. **Apply Match, Match, Match, Lead**: Match the person's patterns at least three times before attempting to lead them to a new perspective.

7. **Execute Context-Specific Application**: Use the profile for recruitment, sales, coaching, change management, or negotiation by adapting your language and process to the specific patterns detected.

### 5. Non-Negotiable Principles
- **The Bus Stop Principle**: "To get people to go somewhere with you, you need to meet them where they are... and not just pretend that they are already where you want them to be. Instead, go to their bus stop and from their bus stop, invite them on the bus."
- **Context is King**: Patterns are context-dependent, not permanent personality traits. "The LAB Profile® simply describes the form of our door, what specifically we let in and out in a given situation, or Context."
- **How Over What**: You must pay attention to HOW people answer (structure of language, non-verbal cues) rather than WHAT they talk about.
- **No Good or Bad Patterns**: "There are no good or bad ones to have. You can judge the appropriateness of each Pattern only in the Context of the activity that needs to be done."
- **Exact Criteria**: You must use the person's exact Criteria words, never paraphrase. "To solve this problem, and, to show someone you have understood them, play back their key words, their Criteria."
- **Match Before Lead**: You must match someone's way of being/language at least three times before attempting to lead them.
- **The Macho Test**: Communication must not imply: 1) There is something they don't already know, 2) I am telling them what to do, 3) They have a problem and I have the solution, 4) They are not perfect, or 5) I am better than they are.
- **Validation Required**: Always use Guess and Test to verify your diagnosis; never assume.

### 7. Coaching Questions
The author uses specific questions to elicit each pattern:
- **Criteria**: "What do you want in [context]?" "What's important to you?" "What has to be there?"
- **Direction**: "Why is having that [Criteria] important?" (Ask up to 3 times to determine Toward/Away From)
- **Source**: "How do you know that you have done a good job [at context]?" "Where do you know that?"
- **Reason**: "Why did you choose your present work [or house, vacation, etc.]?"
- **Decision Factors**: "What is the relationship between your work this year and last year?" or "What is the relationship between this job and your last one?"
- **Stress Response**: "Tell me about a [work] situation that caused you trouble."
- **Style & Organization**: "Tell me about a [work] experience that was [their Criteria]. What did you like about it?"
- **Rule Structure**: "What is a good way for you to increase your success [at work]?" followed by "What is a good way for someone else to increase their success [at work]?"
- **Convincer Channel**: "How do you know that someone else is good at their work?" or "How do you know that [a car] is worth [buying]?"
- **Convincer Mode**: "How many times do they have to demonstrate this [see, hear, read, do] for you to be convinced?"

Additionally, for shifting thinking:
- "Do you feel bad enough now to make some changes or would you rather wait until you feel even worse?"
- "What business am I in?" (for Away From people to re-center)
- "How does this activity fit in with what I want to accomplish?"
- "Can you imagine what we'll think about this situation two years from now?" (to help dissociate from feelings)

### 8. Tools, Exercises, and Practices
- **The LAB Profile® Worksheet**: A form for marking checkmarks next to pattern indicators during interviews.
- **Hierarchy of Criteria**: An exercise where you hold out hands representing two criteria and ask "Which one attracts you?" to determine priority.

- **The 4-Step Motivation Method©**: 1) Why (Toward or Away From), 2) What (for Internals or Externals), 3) How (choices/Options or steps/Procedures), 4) Appreciation.
- **The Bad News Formula©**: A method for delivering bad news that maintains rapport (specific formula not fully detailed but implied to start with good news or frame appropriately).

- **Conversational Coaching©**: A 7-step process involving getting in state, establishing framework, getting permission, agreeing on problem/outcome, analyzing Present State-Desired State in LAB Profile® terms, intervention through Pattern overlapping, and future pacing.
- **Guess and Test**: The validation methodology where you make statements about detected patterns and observe reactions.

### 9. Objection Handling and Resistance
- **For Internal People**: Avoid Command Language ("you should"). Instead use Language of Suggestion: "You might want to consider," "Only you can decide," "Here's some information so you can decide."
- **For Macho Patterns**: Use phrases like "As you probably know..." (then state what they don't know), "How have you solved this problem?" (implies they already solved it), "With your experience and knowledge in this area..." Use the Macho Test to edit out implications of superiority.
- **For Away From People**: Don't focus solely on goals/benefits. Acknowledge problems to avoid: "This will prevent..." "You won't have to worry about..."
- **For Consistent People**: "They are never completely convinced. Every day is a new day." You must re-establish rapport and credibility at each contact. Use language like "try it; each time you use it; daily."
- **For Skeptics (Internal/Away From/Consistent)**: Pre-empt objections by stating them first: "It may seem that because the problem has gone on so long, it may be hard to solve." Establish credibility rigorously before making suggestions.
- **When People Can't Choose (Criteria Conflict)**: Ask permission to do the activity, check if one criterion is a component of another, or check if they have two labels for the same experience.
- **When People Resist Change**: Match their Decision Factors pattern first (Sameness language for Sameness people rather than "revolution" language).

### 12. The Repeatable Execution System
For professional application in communication and influence:
1. **Diagnose**: Identify the Context clearly using When, Where, With whom, and a verb. Ask the LAB Profile® questions or detect patterns through conversational observation.
2. **Record**: Use the Profiling Worksheet to mark indicators for each of the 14 categories.
3. **Validate**: Use the Guess and Test methodology—describe behaviors using statements and observe reactions to confirm accuracy.
4. **Strategize**: Select the appropriate Influencing Language for the detected patterns (e.g., Language of Suggestion for Internals, Away From language for problem-solvers, Procedures language for step-by-step processors).
5. **Execute**: Apply the Match, Match, Match, Lead sequence—match the person's patterns at least three times before attempting to lead them to a new perspective or action.
6. **Specific Applications**:
   - **Sales**: Map the customer's buying process through Convincer Channel/Mode sequences; use External language initially then shift as they become Internal.
   - **Recruitment**: Profile the position requirements; write advertisements using language that attracts the right patterns; screen candidates for fit.
   - **Coaching**: Use Conversational Coaching©—establish framework, get permission, analyze Present State vs. Desired State patterns, intervene by overlapping patterns, check ecology, and future pace.
   - **Change Management**: Diagnose present culture patterns; design change messages using language that matches current patterns initially, then leads to desired patterns.
   - **Negotiation**: Determine both parties' patterns (typically Internal/Away From in conflict); use language that acknowledges criteria and suggests rather than commands.

### 14. Non-Negotiable Execution Rules
- **Context Specification**: You must specify the Context (When, Where, With whom) before profiling and ensure the person stays within that context throughout.
- **Exact Criteria**: Never paraphrase Criteria; you must use the person's exact words/phrases.
- **No Command Language for Internals**: Never use "you should" or direct commands with someone who has an Internal pattern; use Language of Suggestion only.
- **Macho Test Compliance**: All communication must pass the Macho Test—must not imply the other person doesn't know, that you're telling them what to do, that they have a problem you can solve, that they're imperfect, or that you're better.
- **Validation Before Action**: Never act on a diagnosis without using Guess and Test to validate.
- **Match Three Times**: You must match the person's patterns at least three times before attempting to lead them.
- **Ecology Check**: Every intervention must be checked for possible negative consequences before completion (NLP requirement adapted by the author).
- **State Management**: Never leave a person in a negative emotional state after asking the Stress Response question; always shift them to positive Criteria before ending.

### 15. Failure Patterns
- **Paraphrasing Criteria**: Using your own words instead of the person's exact Criteria words, causing loss of rapport.
- **Commanding Internals**: Telling an Internal person what to do using Command Language ("you should"), causing immediate resistance.
- **Macho Violations**: Implying you know something they don't, causing Macho individuals to reject your input entirely.
- **Pattern Assumption**: Assuming a pattern is a permanent personality trait rather than context-dependent, leading to inaccurate predictions.
- **Options Overload**: Giving a Procedures person too many options or choices, causing paralysis; or forcing a Procedures person to break rules.
- **Procedure Rigidity**: Forcing an Options person to follow rigid procedures without explaining why or allowing flexibility, causing rebellion.
- **Toward Language with Away From**: Focusing only on goals/benefits with highly Away From people, failing to trigger their motivation (which requires problems to solve).
- **Inconsistent Credibility**: Failing to re-establish credibility with Consistent people at each contact, causing them to reject previously accepted ideas.
- **Skipping Validation**: Failing to Guess and Test, resulting in acting on incorrect pattern diagnoses.
- **Wrong Convincer**: Providing insufficient examples for Number of Examples people, or trying to convince Automatic people after they've decided no.

### 16. Decision Logic
- **When uncertain between patterns**: Ask alternate questions or use "Where do you know that?" to test.
- **When choosing criteria**: Use the Hierarchy of Criteria forced-choice method (holding out hands) to determine priority.
- **When influencing**: Apply Match, Match, Match, Lead—match at least three times before leading.
- **When facing Macho resistance**: Apply the Macho Test editing rules (rephrase to "As you probably know," etc.).
- **When in conflict**: Recognize both parties go into Internal/Away From; meet them at their bus stop using their Criteria and Language of Suggestion.
- **When selling**: Determine if customer is in Options (exploring) vs. Procedures (ready to buy) by asking "Are you ready to look at the next step?" If they say "What else do you have?" they are still in Options.
- **When changing habits**: Use External measures (reminders, contracts) initially, then shift to Internal with Procedures for maintenance.
- **When coaching**: Determine if client needs more Options (if stuck in rigid thinking) or more Procedures (if overwhelmed by choices).

### 17. Self-Review Protocol
- **Check Context**: Did I clearly specify and maintain the Context throughout?
- **Check Criteria**: Did I use the exact words or did I paraphrase?
- **Check Macho**: Does my communication pass the Macho Test (5 criteria)?
- **Check Language Match**: Did I use the appropriate Influencing Language for the detected patterns (e.g., Suggestion for Internal, Away From for problem-focused, etc.)?
- **Check Validation**: Did I Guess and Test before proceeding with influence?
- **Check Matching**: Did I match at least three times before leading?
- **Check State**: Did I leave the person in a positive or neutral state (ecology check)?
- **Check Convincer**: Did I provide the right number of examples or duration for their Convincer Mode?
- **Check Direction**: Did I balance Toward/Away From language appropriately for their pattern?

### 18. Definition of Done
The work is complete when:
- All 14 categories have been profiled with marks on the worksheet.
- Patterns have been validated through Guess and Test with explicit or implicit confirmation from the subject.
- The specific Context has been clearly identified and documented.
- Appropriate Influencing Language has been selected and prepared for each relevant pattern.
- For coaching: The client has shifted from Present State to Desired State patterns, passed the ecology check, and successfully future-paced the solution.
- For recruitment: The job profile is complete, the advertisement contains the correct Influencing Language to attract the right patterns, and candidates have been screened against the profile.
- For sales: The customer's Convincer Channel and Mode have been satisfied, and they have shifted into Procedures mode (ready for next steps).
- For change: The gap between present and desired culture patterns has been bridged with appropriate communication strategies.
- Communication passes the Macho Test and uses no Command Language with Internal subjects.

### 19. The Amateur-to-Expert Gap
**Amateurs:**
- Paraphrase Criteria instead of using exact words.
- Ask "why" directly rather than "Why did you choose..." to detect Options/Procedures.
- Treat patterns as permanent personality traits rather than context-dependent states.
- Use one-size-fits-all communication regardless of detected patterns.
- Miss non-verbal cues (Self/Other indicators, body language for Level).
- Push for decisions with Options people who are still exploring.
- Tell Internal people what to do using Command Language.
- Skip the Guess and Test validation phase.
- Use Active Listening (paraphrasing content) instead of playing back exact Criteria.
- Assume everyone is motivated by goals (Toward).

**Experts:**
- Extract and use exact Criteria words exclusively.
- Use specific elicitation questions that reveal patterns indirectly.
- Recognize that patterns shift based on Context and significant life events.
- Tailor Influencing Language precisely to the detected pattern combination.
- Observe and interpret non-verbal cues (sentence structure, body language, voice tone).
- Recognize when someone shifts from Options to Procedures (ready to decide) and act accordingly.
- Use Language of Suggestion ("you might consider") with Internal people.
- Rigorously validate every diagnosis through Guess and Test before proceeding.
- Playback exact Criteria words to demonstrate understanding and maintain rapport.
- Recognize that 40% of people are Away From motivated (by problems) and adjust language accordingly.
- Understand and work with Combination Patterns rather than isolated single patterns.
