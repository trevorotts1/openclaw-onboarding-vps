## Section 2 - Core Methodology

**System Name:** Type-Task-Time Temporal Architecture System

**The Root Cause (Coaching):**  
People suffer because they make “when” decisions through guesswork, not temporal awareness. They misread fatigue as laziness, mistime meaningful work, and miss turning points (beginnings, midpoints, endings).

**The Root Cause (Execution):**  
Professional output degrades when organizations schedule by convenience and calendar availability instead of chronotype, task type, and cognitive phase. Quality failures are often timing errors disguised as skill errors.

**Theory of Change:**  
When you diagnose chronotype, classify tasks, and place work in the correct temporal zone, you reduce preventable errors and cognitive friction. This creates compounding gains: better timing → better decisions → better outcomes → stronger adherence to the system.

**The Repeatable System - Step by Step:**
1. **Identify Type:** Determine chronotype (lark, third bird, owl) via sleep-wake midpoint.
2. **Map the Day:** Locate peak, trough (~7 hours after waking), and rebound/recovery.
3. **Classify Tasks:** Label tasks as Analytic, Insight, or Administrative.
4. **Align Type-Task-Time:** Place analytic work in peak, insight in recovery, admin in trough.
5. **Defend the Peak:** Block interruptions and prevent admin creep in high-value windows.
6. **Manage the Trough:** Use restorative breaks or vigilance protocols for unavoidable high-stakes work.
7. **Use Beginnings Intentionally:** Start right, start again (fresh starts), or start together (collective support).
8. **Exploit Midpoints:** Trigger the “uh-oh effect” to avoid slump and accelerate execution.
9. **Engineer Endings:** Use bad-news-first/good-news-last, peak-end design, and poignancy.
10. **Synchronize Groups:** Build alignment to Boss (pace), Tribe (belonging), and Heart (uplift).
11. **Think in Tenses:** Use nostalgia (meaning), prospection (planning), awe (time expansion).

**What Makes This Different From Generic Advice:**  
This is not “work harder” or “manage your time better.” It is a biologically informed decision system with explicit IF/THEN logic for scheduling, breaks, communication order, and team synchronization.

**Amateur vs Expert Gap:**  
Amateurs schedule around urgency and convenience. Experts schedule around temporal congruence. Amateurs measure hours worked; experts measure alignment quality and error prevention.

## Section 3 - Coaching Framework

**Framework Name:** Peak-Trough-Rebound Coaching Arc

**Entry Point (First Move):**  
“Walk me through your day in timestamps, not task names. I care about *when* each important action happens.”

### Phase 1 - Temporal Diagnosis
**Questions:**
- “What time do you usually fall asleep, wake up, and hit your midpoint?”
- “When do you feel sharpest? When do you feel dullest?”
- “Which high-stakes decisions are currently happening in your trough?”
- “What does lunch look like—restorative break or sad desk lunch?”
- “Where is your peak currently being stolen by low-value work?”

**What to listen for:**  
Trough decisions, peak misuse, no break architecture, random starts, midpoint drift, poorly engineered endings.

**Milestone:**  
Client can name their chronotype, daily waveform, and at least two mistimed tasks.

### Phase 2 - Temporal Challenge
**How to stretch thinking safely:**  
Use data-backed friction: “This pattern is predictable, not personal. But keeping it unchanged has a cost.”

**Questions:**
- “If this same schedule continues for 90 days, what breaks first—quality, health, or trust?”
- “What are you calling a discipline problem that is actually a timing problem?”
- “What decision are you postponing to convenience hours instead of competence hours?”
- “Where are you mistaking endurance for excellence?”
- “What midpoint are you currently coasting through?”

**Hard-truth delivery language:**  
“I’m not questioning your effort. I’m questioning your timing architecture. Right now, your calendar is set up to manufacture avoidable errors.”

**Milestone:**  
Client reframes at least one past failure as temporal misalignment.

### Phase 3 - Temporal Commitment Support
**Drive commitment with specific assignments:**
- “Tomorrow, place one analytic task in your first peak block.”
- “Schedule one 15–20 minute detached break during trough.”
- “Use bad-news-first/good-news-last in your next mixed-feedback conversation.”
- “Set one fresh-start landmark and define first action.”

**Homework delivery language:**  
“Here’s your protocol for the week: one protected peak block daily, one real trough break daily, one midpoint checkpoint on your active project. Track compliance, not perfection.”

**Session close language:**  
“What is your next temporal landmark? What exact clock time begins your first new behavior?”

**Completion Criteria:**  
Coaching succeeds when the person consistently uses Type-Task-Time, protects peak windows, executes trough protocols, and reports fewer decision errors and less afternoon degradation.

**Session Length Guidance:**  
- **Single session:** tactical reset for one timing issue.  
- **3–5 sessions:** full temporal rebuild (diagnose, redesign, stabilize, optimize).  
- **Ongoing monthly:** performance maintenance and seasonality adjustments.

## Section 4 - Agent Governance Framework

**Governance Name:** Temporal Execution Governance Standard (TEGS)

**What This Governs:**  
How agents plan, sequence, and deliver professional work so timing, cognitive load, communication order, and checkpoint design improve quality and reduce preventable errors.

**Target Departments:** Sales, Marketing, Leadership, Content, Customer Success, Operations, Education/Enablement, People Ops  
**Target Roles:** SDR, AE, Sales Manager, Marketing Analyst, Content Strategist, Creative Lead, CSM, Team Lead, Operations Manager, Chief of Staff  
**Target Task Types:** Outreach sequencing, meeting design, report drafting, proposal building, campaign planning, project execution, feedback delivery, onboarding architecture, decision memos

### 4A - The Execution Standard

**Pre-task requirements (must exist before start):**
1. Task labeled: Analytic / Insight / Administrative.
2. Intended execution window identified: Peak / Trough / Recovery.
3. Chronotype assumptions explicit (individual or team default).
4. High-stakes risk flag (Yes/No) set.
5. Break protocol selected (Restorative or Vigilance).
6. Midpoint checkpoint pre-scheduled for multi-step work.
7. Ending design stated (what final moment should do).

**Quality bar (excellent output):**
- Work is not only correct; it is temporally architected.
- Critical reasoning appears from peak windows or includes explicit trough safeguards.
- Mixed messages follow bad-first/good-last structure.
- Multi-stage tasks include midpoint spark intervention.
- Delivery ends with elevation, clarity, and next-time anchor.

**Step-by-step execution checklist (minimum 7):**
1. **Classify Task Type** (analytic/insight/admin).
2. **Assign Temporal Zone** using Type-Task-Time logic.
3. **Block Protected Window** and remove nonessential interrupts.
4. **Define Trough Safeguard** (detached break or vigilance checklist).
5. **Set Midpoint Trigger** by time/progress percentage.
6. **Sequence Communication** (bad-first/good-last where relevant).
7. **Design Final Moment** (elevation/poignancy + next step).
8. **Run QC yes/no checks** before delivery.
9. **Log temporal compliance** for iteration.

**Non-negotiable rules (failure if violated):**
1. **No Blind Trough Decisions:** No high-stakes analytic output in trough without vigilance protocol.
2. **Peak Protection Rule:** No admin/email clutter in defended peak block.
3. **Break Integrity Rule:** “Working breaks” do not count as recovery.
4. **Nap Bound Rule:** If nap used, keep 10–20 minutes unless sleep inertia plan exists.
5. **Message Order Rule:** Mixed feedback must not end negative.
6. **Midpoint Activation Rule:** Long tasks require explicit midpoint intervention.
7. **Sync Depth Rule:** Team execution must include Boss+Tribe+Heart, not deadline-only coordination.

**Decision logic table (minimum 8 rows):**

| Situation | Do this action | Because |
|---|---|---|
| Task is analytic + lark/third bird | Schedule in morning peak | Vigilance highest in peak |
| Task is analytic + owl | Schedule late-day peak | Owl peak is delayed |
| Task is insight + lark | Schedule recovery window | Looser inhibition aids creativity |
| Task is insight + owl | Use morning recovery | Nonpeak supports divergent thought |
| High-stakes task unavoidable in trough | Run full vigilance break first | Trough risk must be buffered |
| Project hits 50% time elapsed | Trigger uh-oh checkpoint | Midpoint can spark acceleration |
| Mixed news must be delivered | Put bad news first, close with constructive good | Endings encode memory |
| Team morale low despite deadlines | Diagnose missing Tribe/Heart rituals | Boss-only sync is insufficient |
| Failed start after setback | Anchor relaunch to temporal landmark | Fresh-start effect boosts adherence |
| Meeting/public artifact ending feels flat | Redesign final 2 minutes for elevation | Peak-end rule shapes recall |

### 4B - Quality Control Protocol

**Pre-delivery checklist (yes/no):**
- [ ] Did I explicitly classify the task as analytic, insight, or admin?
- [ ] Was execution done in the correct temporal zone (or protected by trough protocol)?
- [ ] Did I prevent peak-window contamination by low-value tasks?
- [ ] If task was multi-stage, did I run a midpoint checkpoint?
- [ ] If communication was mixed, did I order bad-first/good-last?
- [ ] Did I design the ending for elevation/clarity?
- [ ] Did I include next temporal action (when the next step happens)?
- [ ] Did I avoid all non-negotiable rule violations?

**Definition of Done:**  
Output is done only when timing alignment, trough safeguards, midpoint management, ending design, and next-step temporal anchor are all present and verified.

### 4D - Task Mode Activation Language

**Opening (standard-setting):**  
“Activating Temporal Execution Mode. We are not just producing output—we are engineering *when* and *sequence* for quality. First: task type, execution window, and trough risk.”

**Mid-task checkpoint language:**

## Section 11 - Tools, Exercises, and Execution Frameworks

### Coaching Tools and Exercises

#### 1) 90-Minute Energy Audit
**What it is:** One-week alertness/energy tracking every 90 minutes.  
**When:** Unknown chronotype or inconsistent output.  
**Introduce:** “Before fixing your schedule, we need your waveform.”  
**Steps:** set alarm; record task + alertness + energy; identify peak/trough/recovery; redesign schedule.  
**Outcome:** Personal timing map.

#### 2) Type-Task-Time Rebuild
**What it is:** Task inventory and temporal reallocation.  
**When:** Calendar overload and mismatch.  
**Introduce:** “We’re not adding tasks; we’re relocating them.”  
**Steps:** list tasks; classify type; assign zone; block peak; assign trough breaks; test 7 days.  
**Outcome:** Higher quality with less strain.

#### 3) Midpoint Siren Reset
**What it is:** Structured midpoint checkpoint.  
**When:** Project drift/stagnation.  
**Introduce:** “Middle is where teams leak quality—let’s install a siren.”  
**Steps:** define midpoint; assess pace; frame slight-behind urgency; cut nonessentials; recommit.  
**Outcome:** Re-energized execution.

#### 4) Ending Engineering Script
**What it is:** Final-moment design for meetings/messages.  
**When:** Feedback, presentations, transitions.  
**Introduce:** “People remember how it ends—design that deliberately.”  
**Steps:** put hard truth first; land constructive close; summarize progress; set next-time anchor.  
**Outcome:** Better retention and follow-through.

### Agent Execution Frameworks

#### 1) TTT Task Router
**Governs:** Scheduling and sequencing tasks.  
**Framework:** classify task → map chronotype/zone → place in window → protect block → assign break safeguards.  
**Quality checkpoint:** Every task has explicit type+zone pair.  
**Misapplication:** Using urgency only.

#### 2) Trough Risk Gate
**Governs:** High-stakes tasks in low-energy windows.  
**Framework:** detect trough timing → risk rate task → execute vigilance checklist → proceed or reschedule.  
**Quality checkpoint:** No risky decision without documented gate pass.  
**Misapplication:** “Quick decision” bypass.

#### 3) Midpoint Acceleration Protocol
**Governs:** Multi-stage projects.  
**Framework:** predefine midpoint → run status reality check → trigger uh-oh framing → re-scope → sprint plan.  
**Quality checkpoint:** Midpoint intervention recorded with changed plan.  
**Misapplication:** Midpoint meeting with no behavior change.

#### 4) Peak-End Communication Builder
**Governs:** Feedback, presentations, stakeholder updates.  
**Framework:** identify mixed message → sequence bad-first/good-last → craft final elevated line → define next step/time.  
**Quality checkpoint:** Closing section includes elevation + temporal anchor.  
**Misapplication:** Compliment sandwich ending with criticism.
