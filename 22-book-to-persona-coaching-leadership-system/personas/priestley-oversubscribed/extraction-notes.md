### 2. The Central Problem
The central problem is that most businesses are "undersubscribed"—they have excess supply above demand, resulting in losses or mere wages rather than profits. Business owners suffer from chasing clients rather than selecting whom they want to work with. They focus on the entire marketplace rather than finding the small group of people who truly value what they offer, forcing them to compete on price and remain average. They engage in "busy-ness" rather than running campaign-driven enterprises, going after clients one at a time in a linear fashion that is unsustainable and leads to burnout. They forget the basic economic truth that profit is only tolerated when demand exceeds supply. They make the mistake of showing desperation to sell—answering calls late at night, discounting, and screaming "I will do anything for a sale"—which turns potential buyers away. They struggle because they cater to what the "market" thinks rather than creating their own market, and they rush to sell without warming people up or creating the right buying conditions.

### 3. The Root Cause of Human Struggle
"The market abhors a profit; a profit is only tolerated if demand is higher than supply." The root cause is the imbalance between demand and supply. When supply is too great and everyone who wants something can get it, prices fall and margins disappear. The real underlying issue is attempting to exist in an open market rather than creating a market of your own. As Priestley states: "Being part of an open market is a problem; you'll forever be battling the forces of demand and supply outside of your control. Having a market of your own is the key to becoming oversubscribed." Additionally, people struggle because they try to please everyone rather than accepting that "being oversubscribed requires nothing more than a situation whereby some people who really want something have to miss out on having it." They also fail because they don't understand that "people don't buy what they need, they buy what they want"—emotional desire outweighs logical need every time.

### 4. The Complete Methodology - Every Step
The methodology is the **Campaign-Driven Enterprise Method**, structured as follows:

**Three Campaign Types:**
1. **Weekly Micro-Campaigns:** Baseline activity generating consistent sales (e.g., weekly "lunch and learn" sessions).
2. **Quarterly Spotlight Campaigns:** Special promotions aligning with bigger brands to create buzz.
3. **Annual Big Message Campaigns:** Sharing high-level ideas and themes beyond specific products.

**Six Phases of Campaigns:**

**Phase 1: Campaign Planning**
- Know your capacity (how many you can truly delight).
- Identify who it's for (market with capacity to pay, who values you, who you want to serve).
- Create a campaign theme (something bigger than your product—philosophy, lifestyle, transformation).
- Create a campaign timeline (start/finish dates, communications schedule, milestones, events).

**Phase 2: Build-Up**
- Signal intentions to the market; collect signals back (don't ask for the sale, ask for the signal).
- Use the "Big Five" for lead generation: Hustle, Joint Ventures, Advertising, Referrals, Own Data.
- Think mobile and media assets first.
- Educate and entertain (80-20 rule).
- Apply the "7-11-4" principle (7 hours, 11 interactions, 4 locations to build trust).
- Market products-for-prospects (low-risk first steps).
- Name your terms (set standards and expectations).

**Phase 3: Oversubscribed Release**
- Measure interest (quantity and quality of signals).
- Ensure sufficiently oversubscribed capacity: Hot signals (5x capacity), Warm signals (10x capacity), Cold signals (100x capacity).
- Create transparency of demand-and-supply tension (show the lineup).
- Execute client selection process (choose who gets to buy).
- Manage energy through: Staged release, Special/limited editions, Price rises and time limits.

**Phase 4: Sales Follow-Through**
- Proactively follow up with structured sales conversations (not chit-chats).
- Maintain rhythm using LAPS (Leads, Appointments, Presentations, Sales).
- Use scripted sales conversations with six phases: Rapport, Discovery, Confirming, Advising, Discussing, Completing.
- Avoid "ABS braking" (Anything But Sales).

**Phase 5: Remarkable Delivery**
- Ensure delivery is "positively remarkable" (worthy of being talked about).
- Create "up energy" (delight, surprise) not just sideways satisfaction.
- Conduct a "Remarkable Audit" (score every touchpoint 1-10).
- Maintain technology and media infrastructure to support delivery.

**Phase 6: Celebrate and Innovate**
- Tell your stories (capture client successes, behind-scenes narratives).
- Measure results ("the truth is in the results").
- Conduct debrief sessions (six questions: score 1-10, what went well, what could improve, tools needed, magic moments, anything else).
- Party and rest.
- Prepare for the next campaign.

**Four Market Positions/Drivers:**
1. Innovation (something new/shiny).
2. Relationships (owning the relationship so buyers ignore others).
3. Convenience (frictionless, right place/time).
4. Price (efficiency allowing lower profitable pricing).

### 8. Tools, Exercises, and Practices

### 9. Objection Handling and Resistance
- **Price too high:** Counter by naming terms, creating exclusivity, and demonstrating that high-achievers want dedicated service (Rich Litvin example). "People who earn millions per year don't want to pay $32.50 to a coach... High-achievers want a dedicated level of service."
- **Need to think about it:** Counter by providing the "7-11-4" content journey (7 hours of material) so they can think with your resources. "Expect your interested people to 'think about it' for up to seven hours while they explore their options."
- **Market saturated:** Counter by creating your own market. "You don't need everyone... you only need more people than you can handle."
- **Can't afford it:** Counter by finding the market with capacity to pay and linking to their dominant values. "If something is in line with our values, there's no stopping us."
- **Desperation/availability:** Counter by showing "with-or-without-you energy." "An oversubscribed business tunes in to that little voice [saying no] and acts upon it."
- **Philosophy pushback:** Handle by focusing on those who agree rather than detractors (Pete Evans example). "There's nothing to be gained by being boring, plain, beige and trying to please everyone."
- **Contrarian resistance:** Embrace it. "It's OK to play a different game to everyone else... It's OK to tell the truth when everyone else is exaggerating."

### 12. The Repeatable Execution System
The **Campaign-Driven Enterprise Method** provides the repeatable system:

**Weekly Execution:** Run micro-campaigns (e.g., weekly lunch-and-learns) generating baseline revenue through consistent LAPS (Leads, Appointments, Presentations, Sales) rhythm.

**Quarterly Execution:** Run spotlight campaigns (e.g., quarterly workshops with guest experts) creating buzz and larger sales spikes.

**Six-Phase Execution Cycle:**
1. **Planning:** Calculate exact capacity, identify target market, create theme and timeline.
2. **Build-Up:** Generate and collect signals (Hot 5x, Warm 10x, Cold 100x capacity) using mobile-first media assets; educate and entertain following 7-11-4 rule.
3. **Release:** Maintain transparency of demand-supply tension; select clients from oversubscribed pool using staged releases and time limits.
4. **Sales Follow-Through:** Execute scripted sales conversations (not chit-chat) following Rapport-Discovery-Confirming-Advising-Discussing-Completing structure; maintain LAPS dashboard.
5. **Remarkable Delivery:** Execute delivery exceeding expectations (70% promised, 30% surprise); monitor NPS; conduct Remarkable Audit.
6. **Celebrate/Innovate:** Debrief using six questions; capture stories and metrics; rest; plan next campaign.

**Four Market Positions:** Choose one driver (Innovation, Relationship, Convenience, or Price) and build ecosystem around it without messing with the winning formula.

### 13. The Quality Bar
Excellence is defined as being **"positively remarkable"**—"worthy of being talked about" in a positive way. The specific standards include:
- **Net Promoter Score of 7+ out of 10** (below 7 indicates no chance of growth).
- Creating **"up energy"** (delight, love, joy, surprise, magic) rather than sideways satisfaction or down energy.
- Delivering the **"guitar with strings"**—complete solutions that include everything needed to achieve the outcome, not partial components.
- **Remarkable Audit scores of 10/10** on key touchpoints (website, brochures, premises, packaging, etc.).
- **LAPS metrics:** Consistent rhythm of Leads → Appointments → Presentations → Sales with predictable conversion rates.
- **Experience relative to price:** Exceeding expectations relative to what was paid (whether low price like Walmart or high price like Ferrari).
- **7-11-4 fulfillment:** Ensuring clients have received 7 hours, 11 interactions, and 4 locations of value.
- **"With-or-without-you energy":** Sales confidence that comes from genuine oversubscription.

### 14. Non-Negotiable Execution Rules
- Must know real capacity (number you can delight) and never exceed it during a campaign.
- Must not release sales capacity until sufficiently oversubscribed (5x hot, 10x warm, 100x cold signals).
- Must maintain demand-supply tension; never expand capacity mid-campaign just because people are lining up.
- Must stick to named prices and terms; never raise prices opportunistically mid-release.
- Must say no to wrong-fit clients; interview before accepting.
- Must maintain "with-or-without-you energy"—never appear desperate for any single sale.
- Sales conversations must be scripted and rehearsed; never "wing it" or engage in unstructured chit-chat.
- Must spend 5-9% of revenue on advertising/marketing to maintain oversubscription (never take foot off accelerator).
- Must invest 5-10% of income annually in high-quality training and development.
- Must give away ideas/information freely; charge only for implementation.
- Must build personal brand (Key Person of Influence); cannot remain faceless.
- Must create your own market; never compete solely in the open market on price.
- Must follow the six campaign phases sequentially; never skip build-up to rush to sale.

### 15. Failure Patterns
- **ABS Braking (Anything But Sales):** Avoiding proactive sales follow-up despite having leads.
- **Linear Chasing:** Trying to win clients one at a time rather than using campaign clustering.
- **Market Pricing:** Focusing on what the "market" will bear rather than creating your own market.
- **Desperation Marketing:** Being available 24/7, discounting, and signaling "I will do anything for a sale."
- **Capacity Overreach:** Accepting more clients than capacity allows, diluting quality and destroying reputation.
- **Skipping Build-Up:** Asking for the sale immediately without warming up the market (binary choice).
- **Information Selling:** Trying to charge for ebooks, courses, or information rather than implementation.
- **Feature Focus:** Marketing product features rather than philosophy or transformation.
- **Chit-Chat Sales:** Unstructured sales conversations without scripts or clear completion criteria.
- **Satisfaction Delivery:** Delivering only what was promised (sideways energy) rather than remarkable surprises (up energy).
- **Solo Operation:** Trying to execute campaigns alone rather than building a CDE team.
- **Ignoring Data:** Not measuring LAPS, NPS, or campaign metrics ("truth is in the results").
- **Foot Off Accelerator:** Stopping marketing spend when busy, causing future dry spells.
- **Pleasing Everyone:** Failing to say no to wrong-fit clients, becoming a commodity.

### 16. Decision Logic
- **Demand/Supply Math:** If demand < supply = losses; if balanced = wages; if demand > supply = profits.
- **Signal Thresholds:** Release capacity only when Hot signals = 5x capacity, OR Warm = 10x, OR Cold = 100x.
- **Two-Bidder Rule:** If two people want something and only one can get it, price rises until one gives in.
- **Market Position Selection:** Choose ONE of four (Innovation, Relationship, Convenience, Price); cannot effectively combine conflicting positions (e.g., high innovation + high convenience).
- **Client Selection:** Choose clients based on capacity to pay + high valuation of your work + your desire to serve them; reject if any element missing.
- **Pricing Decisions:** If winning formula is low price (Walmart model), don't raise prices; if premium, package in unexpected extras. Never change price mid-campaign.
- **Investment Allocation:** Spend 5-9% of revenue on advertising; 5-10% of income on training; remainder on remarkable delivery.
- **Capacity Expansion:** Only increase capacity when systems can support it without diluting current client experience (delight must be maintained).

### 17. Self-Review Protocol
- **Net Promoter Score (NPS):** Regularly measure "How likely are you to recommend this business?" Target 7+.
- **Remarkable Audit:** Quarterly scoring of every touchpoint (website, packaging, staff, premises) 1-10; identify one or two monthly to improve to 10/10.
- **Debrief Sessions:** Post-campaign circle answering six questions: (1) Score team performance 1-10, (2) What did we do well?, (3) What could we improve?, (4) What tools did we wish we had?, (5) What were the magic moments?, (6) Anything else?
- **LAPS Dashboard:** Weekly review of Leads, Appointments, Presentations, Sales metrics to identify weak links.
- **Campaign Scorecard:** Using www.dent.global/campaignscorecard to assess readiness.
- **Key Person of Influence Scorecard:** Personal brand assessment at www.keypersonofinfluence.com/scorecard.
- **Truth in Results:** Examine cost per lead, cost per sale, conversion rates, unsubscribe rates—"the truth is in the results."

### 18. Definition of Done
- **Campaign Complete:** When stories captured, numbers reported, team debriefed, celebrated, rested, and next campaign planned.
- **Build-Up Complete:** When signal thresholds met (5x hot, 10x warm, or 100x cold).
- **Sales Complete:** When order form signed, terms agreed, payment received, CRM updated.
- **Delivery Complete:** When client expresses delight (NPS 7+), feels "up energy," and refers others.
- **Planning Complete:** When timeline printed on office wall, assets identified, capacity locked, theme chosen.
- **Year Complete:** When all quarterly and weekly campaigns executed, targets met, team trained, innovations implemented.

- Maintain marketing spend (5-9% revenue) consistently.

- Build Campaign-Driven Enterprise teams (Key Person, Sales/Marketing, Operations, Finance).
