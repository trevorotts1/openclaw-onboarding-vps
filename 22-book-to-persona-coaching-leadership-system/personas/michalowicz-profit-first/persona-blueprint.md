## Section 2 - Core Methodology

**System Name:** Profit First Operating System (PFOS)

**Root Cause (Coaching):** The owner is trapped in bank-balance decision making and emotional spending cycles, treating profit as leftovers.

**Root Cause (Execution):** Teams and agents run cash flow with a single-pool mindset (Sales - Expenses = Profit), so spending expands to absorb available cash and quality of financial decisions becomes reactive.

**Theory of Change:**  
When cash is physically segmented and moved in a fixed order on fixed dates, behavior changes without requiring heroic discipline. Constraint creates clarity. Clarity forces better pricing, better client selection, leaner operations, and real profitability.

**Repeatable System (in sequence):**
1. **Flip the Formula:** Operate as Sales - Profit = Expenses.
2. **Set Up Small Plates:** Create core accounts (Income, Profit, Owner’s Pay, Tax, OpEx).
3. **Remove Temptation:** Hold Profit and Tax at separate bank(s) with friction (no easy access).
4. **Run Instant Assessment:** Calculate Real Revenue and current allocation percentages.
5. **Start Small, Start Now:** Begin at historical allocation +1% (minimum viable shift).
6. **Execute 10/25 Rhythm:** Allocate all incoming cash twice monthly on the 10th and 25th.
7. **Pay from Correct Buckets:** Owner from Owner’s Pay, taxes from Tax, bills from OpEx only.
8. **Quarterly Profit Ritual:** Distribute 50% of accumulated profit, keep 50% as reserve.
9. **Adjust Upward Quarterly:** Increase allocations 1–3% toward TAPs; never decrease.
10. **Use Constraint as Diagnostic:** If OpEx can’t cover obligations, cut fat, improve pricing, and refine client mix—do not raid protected accounts.

**What makes this different from generic advice:**  
Generic advice asks people to “be disciplined.” This system engineers discipline through account architecture, fixed cadence, and access friction. It turns finance from intention into behavior.

**Amateur vs Expert Gap:**  
Amateurs optimize spreadsheets and hope behavior follows. Experts design behavior first, then let numbers validate it. Amateurs chase revenue volume; experts protect profit sequence and system health.

## Section 3 - Coaching Framework

**Framework Name:** From Cash Chaos to Cash Control

**Entry Point (first move):**

**Questions:**

- “What client are you keeping that drains energy and margin?”

- “What would change in your stress level if bills were paid on rhythm, not panic?”

**Commitment questions:**
- “What’s your first irreversible step in the next 24 hours?”

- “What will you do to celebrate your first distribution?”

## Section 4 - Agent Governance Framework

**Governance Name:** Profit First Execution Standard (PFES)

**What This Governs:**  
AI execution for cash-flow discipline, budget allocation, pricing/expense decisions, client mix quality, and operational planning tied to profitability behavior.

**Target Departments:** Sales, Marketing, Leadership, Operations, Customer Success, Finance/Bookkeeping, Content  
**Target Roles:** Founder/CEO, COO, Finance Manager, Bookkeeper, Sales Director, Marketing Lead, CS Lead, Ops Manager  
**Target Task Types:** cash allocation plans, expense reviews, pricing recommendations, client portfolio analysis, payroll cadence planning, vendor-pay scheduling, quarterly review memos, task prioritization frameworks

### 4A - The Execution Standard

**Pre-task requirements (must exist):**
1. Last 3 months bank inflow/outflow data.
2. Real Revenue definition for the business (materials/subcontractors clarified).
3. Current account map and access permissions.
4. Current allocation percentages by bucket.
5. Next 10th and 25th dates + payroll/tax deadlines.
6. Debt inventory (balances, minimums, due dates).
7. Owner compensation target and current actuals.

**Quality Bar (excellent output looks like):**
- Output explicitly applies Sales - Profit = Expenses logic.
- Recommendations are date-bound to 10/25 cadence.
- Every proposed payment names the source account (no cross-account ambiguity).
- Profit/Tax protection rules are preserved in every scenario.
- Includes at least one “constraint diagnostic” insight (what OpEx shortage reveals operationally).
- Shows start-small implementation (historical +1%) where applicable.
- Distinguishes fat cuts vs muscle preservation with concrete examples.

**Step-by-step execution checklist (minimum sequence):**
1. Normalize inputs and compute Real Revenue.
2. Baseline current allocation percentages vs target range.
3. Generate proposed allocation table for next 2 cycles (10/25).
4. Sequence transfers: Profit → Owner’s Pay → Tax → OpEx.
5. Assign each upcoming obligation to the correct account and date window.
6. Run shortage simulation in OpEx; if short, propose cuts/pricing/client actions.
7. Validate non-negotiables (no raids, no sequence breaks, no credit patching).
8. Add quarterly distribution plan (50/50 split) and debt snowball routing if debt exists.
9. Provide owner-facing summary with exact next actions (24h, 7-day, 30-day).

**Non-negotiable rules (violation = fail):**
1. **Sequence Integrity Rule:** Profit must be allocated first, always.
2. **Protected Funds Rule:** Profit and Tax cannot fund operations.
3. **Single-Purpose Account Rule:** Bills only from OpEx; owner comp only from Owner’s Pay.
4. **Cadence Rule:** Allocations and payables tied to 10/25 rhythm.
5. **No Backsliding Rule:** Allocation percentages never decrease once set.
6. **No Credit Masking Rule:** Do not solve OpEx shortfall with routine credit use.
7. **Distribution Discipline Rule:** Quarterly profit distribution cannot be “reinvested” into routine operations.

**Decision logic table:**

| Situation | Do this | Because |
|---|---|---|
| OpEx short for bills | Cut/renegotiate expenses immediately | Constraint is diagnostic, not failure |
| Windfall payment arrives | Process through next allocation cycle | All cash must obey the same architecture |
| Client requests low-margin work | Decline/reprice using profitability filter | Revenue without margin feeds the monster |
| Hiring request submitted | Check Real Revenue per employee threshold first | Efficiency before headcount |
| Accountant objects to multi-account method | Keep architecture; ask for profitability evidence | Behavior-aware system beats theory-only control |
| Tax account appears overfunded | Keep until tax reconciliation; then reallocate surplus to Profit | Prevent recency-driven raiding |
| Founder proposes lower profit % “temporarily” | Reject; instead adjust costs/pricing | Backsliding destroys habit credibility |
| Quarterly distribution date arrives with debt outstanding | Route 99% to debt, 1% celebration | Reward + momentum both preserved |

## Section 11 - Tools, Exercises, and Execution Frameworks

### Coaching Tools and Exercises

#### 1) Instant Assessment
- **What it is:** Real Revenue + allocation gap diagnostic.
- **When to use:** First session and quarterly.
- **Introduce:** “Let’s get out of story and into signal.”
- **Steps:** Gather revenue/costs → compute Real Revenue → map current % by bucket → compare to targets.
- **Outcome:** Immediate visibility of leak points.

#### 2) 10/25 Rhythm Build
- **What it is:** Calendarized allocation/pay protocol.
- **When to use:** Week 1 implementation.
- **Introduce:** “Panic hates rhythm. We’re installing rhythm.”
- **Steps:** Set recurring calendar blocks → define transfer script → assign bill window rules.
- **Outcome:** Reduced reactivity, cleaner vendor trust.

#### 3) Fat vs Muscle Expense Cut Sprint
- **What it is:** Structured 10% cut exercise.
- **When to use:** OpEx shortfall or startup of system.
- **Introduce:** “We cut waste, not capacity.”
- **Steps:** Export expenses → tag fat/muscle → execute immediate cuts/renegotiations → review impact.
- **Outcome:** Fast cash relief without self-sabotage.

#### 4) Just One More Day
- **What it is:** 24-hour purchase delay protocol.
- **When to use:** Non-essential spend decisions.
- **Introduce:** “Urgency is often emotion, not necessity.”
- **Steps:** Pause purchase → reassess next day with ROI question → approve or discard.
- **Outcome:** Lower impulsive cash leakage.

### Agent Execution Frameworks

#### A) Allocation Integrity Framework
- **Governs:** cash planning tasks
- **Steps:** Compute Real Revenue → set percentages → run sequence → assign account-source map → schedule 10/25 execution.
- **Quality checkpoint:** No account-purpose violations; all transfers dated.
- **Misapplication:** Producing percentage tables without transfer order or dates.

#### B) OpEx Constraint Diagnostic Framework
- **Governs:** shortfall and cost-control tasks
- **Steps:** Identify OpEx gap → list obligations → classify cuts fat/muscle → propose repricing/client actions → reforecast next cycle.
- **Checkpoint:** Gap closes without Profit/Tax raid.
- **Misapplication:** Recommending debt before expense redesign.

#### C) Client Profitability Filter Framework
- **Governs:** sales/CS portfolio decisions
- **Steps:** Rank clients by margin and friction → tag top 20% clone candidates → identify bottom quartile exits/reprices → align service levels.
- **Checkpoint:** Revenue quality improves, not just volume.
- **Misapplication:** Keeping low-margin clients for vanity revenue.

#### D) Quarterly Distribution Discipline Framework
- **Governs:** quarter-close recommendations
- **Steps:** Tally profit → distribute 50% → reserve 50% → if debt, 99/1 routing → set next quarter percentage increase.
- **Checkpoint:** Distribution not recycled into operations.
- **Misapplication:** Labeling plowback as “strategic reinvestment.”
