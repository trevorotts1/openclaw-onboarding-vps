### 1. Author Background and Authority
Janet Bray Attwood and Chris Attwood are the co-authors and co-founders of the methodology. Janet managed kitchens and hotels for thousands in Spain and Italy in the 1970s, became the top salesperson in every job she took, owned two successful businesses (one rated among the top ten in her area), worked for a success seminar company teaching motivational courses, and managed the telemarketing division for Books are Fun (the third largest book buyer in the U.S., sold to Reader’s Digest for $380 million the year after her division performed at record levels). She lived for twenty-five years in a meditating community and has practiced Transcendental Meditation® for thirty-seven years. Chris managed ten different businesses as President, General Manager, or Chief Operating Officer over twenty-seven years. In 1981, after becoming President of a secondary dealer in government securities, he retired for ten years to pursue deep meditation and study the Vedic literature of India. 

Their authority stems from creating The Passion Test™ after Janet was inspired by a survey of one hundred of the most influential, financially successful individuals in the United States, which found that each had totally fulfilled those things they felt were most necessary for their ideal life. They have given The Passion Test™ to thousands of people worldwide with “phenomenal results,” including high-profile transformational leaders like Jack Canfield, Mark Victor Hansen, and T. Harv Eker. They co-founded Healthy Wealthy nWise magazine and Enlightened Alliances, a marketing consulting firm, and were founding members of the Transformational Leadership Council. They fundamentally believe that “your passions are the clues to your personal destiny” and that “when you follow your passions, you will love your life.”

### 2. The Central Problem
The exact problem the authors address is that people do not know what they want or what they are passionate about, leading to unfulfilled, uninspired lives. The authors describe the sufferer as someone who works “9 to 5, just getting by, living from paycheck to paycheck,” who has tried seminars, real estate, stocks, multi-level marketing, or part-time jobs but feels “depression and discouragement” rising up, wondering “Is all this really worth it?” 

They specifically describe the person before reading the book as someone who is “not aligned with their personal destiny,” experiencing feelings of being “humiliated, angry, embarrassed, depressed, and broke,” scanning classifieds for jobs based only on pay and difficulty, having stopped dreaming and living an “uninspired version of ‘real life.’” The authors note that most people are “not aligned with their personal destiny” and are “yearning for union” with their true purpose.

### 3. The Root Cause of Human Struggle
The root cause is lack of clarity. As stated in the Foreword by T. Harv Eker: “The number-one reason people don’t get what they want is—they don’t know what they want.” The authors emphasize that “fuzzy desires give fuzzy results.” 

The underlying reason people struggle is that they are living from the mind rather than the heart. The authors state: “Your mind tends to trick you. The mind is like a monkey… Your mind will even try to convince you your life’s purpose is something less than it really is—in the name of safety and security.” They identify that people “play it safe” by censoring their true desires because they cannot immediately see the “how.” The authors write: “Passions arise from the heart. When you are truly passionate about something, you don’t have to try hard to put attention on it.” Conversely, when people are “not enjoying you are out of the flow of life… You are missing your purpose.”

### 4. The Complete Methodology - Every Step
The methodology is a repeatable system called The Passion Test™, consisting of the following steps:

1. **Intention Setting**: Make a list of at least ten (and up to fifteen) of the most important things that would give a life of joy, passion, and fulfillment, completing the sentence: “When my life is ideal, I am _______________.” Do not censor; write the “big” passions even if you don’t know the “how.”

2. **Pairwise Comparison (Elimination Process)**: 
   - Compare item #1 versus #2. If you could have one but not both, which would you choose?
   - Continue comparing the winner to the next item on the list until you have gone through the entire list.
   - Label the final winner as your #1 passion.
   - Repeat the process with remaining items to identify #2, #3, #4, and #5.
   - Specific rule: Do not stop early. If stuck, ask: “If I could be, do or have #1 and not #2 which would bring me more bliss? Or, if I could be #2 and not #1 which would bring me more bliss?”
   - Trust first impulses: “Passion arises from the heart, and your heart’s impulse is more likely to be closer to the truth than your mind’s analysis.”

3. **Passion Scoring**: Rate each of the top five passions on a scale of 0 (not living it at all) to 10 (fully living it).

4. **Passion Cards**: Create 3" x 5" cards listing the top five passions with the header: “I am so happy and thankful that I am now living my passions and drawing the following into my life.” Post these in strategic locations (bathroom mirror, by computer, in purse/wallet, car dashboard, refrigerator).

5. **Create Markers**: For each of the top five passions, write 3-5 specific “Markers” or “Signposts” that will indicate when you are fully living that passion.

6. **Vision Board**: Create a board with pictures cut from magazines representing things you want to be, do, or have.

7. **Passion Pages**: Write a few paragraphs describing what each passion means to you and what life looks like when living it fully.

8. **100th Birthday Speech**: Write a speech in third person (as if given by a best friend or spouse at your 100th birthday) describing the legacy you created, how you lived your passions, and the impact you had.

9. **Regular Review**: Read Passion Cards several times daily. Review the full Test every six months.

10. **The 7 Keys to Living Life Aligned with Passion**: Commitment, Clarity, Attention, Stay Open, Integrity, Persistence, and Follow Your Heart.

### 8. Tools, Exercises, and Practices
- **The Passion Test™ Elimination Process**: Pairwise comparison of 10-15 listed passions to identify top 5.
- **Passion Scoring**: Rating each top passion 0-10 to assess current alignment.
- **Passion Cards**: Writing top 5 passions on 3x5 cards, posting in 5+ locations (bathroom mirror, computer, wallet, car, refrigerator).
- **The Rubber Band Technique**: Wearing a rubber band on the wrist for 30 days, snapping it when having a thought that does not serve you, to break negative attention habits.
- **The Appreciation Game**: Daily writing of at least ten things you appreciate about yourself, your wins, or your achievements; also playable with a partner.
- **Markers Exercise**: Writing 3-5 specific observable outcomes for each passion indicating it is fully realized.
- **Vision Board**: Creating a physical board with magazine pictures representing desired states.
- **Passion Pages**: Writing descriptive narratives of what each passion means and feels like.
- **100th Birthday Speech**: Writing a legacy speech in third person.
- **Transcendental Meditation®**: Described as the “foundation on which everything else is built.”
- **The Work of Byron Katie**: A process for investigating concepts that keep one from living greatness.
- **The Sedona Method**: Practice for “letting go” of emotions and concepts.
- **Daily Review of Top 5 Passions**: Reading Passion Cards morning, throughout day, and evening.
- **Tithing**: Giving 10% (or more) of income regularly.
- **The 7 Keys Implementation**: Daily practice of Commitment, Clarity, Attention, Staying Open, Integrity, Persistence, and Following Heart.
- **Strategy of Preeminence** (Jay Abraham): Eight-step framework including establishing oneself as most trusted advisor, putting into words others’ unverbalized frustrations, telling the truth, never letting others do what’s not in their best interest, and falling in love with the client/customer.

### 9. Objection Handling and Resistance
**Resistance**: “I don’t know how/I’m not clear on the how.” 
**Response**: “You don’t need to know the how, just the what… Once your intention is clear, attention is the next step… When you are clear about WHAT your passions are, you can’t predict HOW they will get fulfilled.” Janet’s story illustrates this when money for India came unexpectedly from her stepmother’s estate after she stopped trying to figure out the how.

**Resistance**: “I’m too old/It’s too late.” 
**Response**: Janet thought she was “too old” at age 10 for drama school. The authors state: “It’s never too late to discover your passion. Most multimillionaires are made after the age of 50.”

**Resistance**: “I have to be responsible/practical.” 
**Response**: “Don’t get us wrong here, we’re not going to tell you to jump off the mountain until you’re ready, but we do want to impress upon you the importance of thinking of your IDEAL life, not your POSSIBLE life.” Also: “What is required of you is to stay open to everything which appeared, without holding on to your own concepts of what you needed.”

**Resistance**: “I’m not worthy/I have low self-esteem.” 
**Response**: The Appreciation Game is prescribed. “People focus on what they don’t want out of fear… Fear is vividly imagining exactly what you don’t want to happen, happening.” The counterargument is to “put your attention on what you do right, on your wins, on the things you do well.”

**Resistance**: “I might fail/make mistakes.” 
**Response**: “Mis – take… as in ‘Take 1,’ ‘Take 2,’ ‘Take 3’… You get another chance to do it again!” Also: “We do not succeed in spite of our obstacles and challenges. We succeed precisely because of them” (Richard Paul Evans). “Failure is always a detour rather than a dead end” (Denis Waitley).

**Resistance**: “I don’t have enough money.” 
**Response**: The story of Janet receiving the inheritance after caring for her stepmother demonstrates that “the good in your life may not appear in the ways you think it will.”

**Resistance**: “I need to control the outcome/my will vs. God’s will.” 
**Response**: “When you let go of the way YOU think things should be, and open yourself to the way they are appearing, you open yourself to the will of God, to the perfect organizing power of Nature.” Also Debbie Ford’s “resigning as general manager of the universe.”

### 12. The Repeatable Execution System
For professional application in any department (sales, marketing, leadership, operations), the execution sequence is:

1. **Clarification Session**: Conduct The Passion Test to identify the top 5 professional and personal passions (the “what” before the “how”).

2. **Current State Assessment**: Rate each passion 0-10 (Passion Score) to identify gaps between stated intentions and actual results.

3. **Visibility Installation**: Create and post Passion Cards in all work environments (office, computer, vehicle, wallet) to maintain constant attention on intentions.

4. **Marker Definition**: Establish 3-5 concrete, observable Markers for each passion that indicate successful fulfillment.

5. **Decision Alignment Protocol**: Implement the rule: “Every time you’re faced with a decision, choose in favor of your passions.” Ask: “Is this going to help me be more aligned with my passions or less aligned?”

6. **Daily Attention Practice**: Start each day reviewing the top 5; use the Appreciation Game to maintain high self-worth; utilize the Rubber Band technique to eliminate negative attention habits.

7. **Transparency and Trust Building**: Create transparency in all stakeholder relationships; confront reality head-on; make and keep commitments to build the “speed of trust.”

8. **Value Delivery Focus**: Shift from self-focus to others-focus (“It isn’t about you, it’s about everyone else”). Identify what problems the marketplace/employer/employees struggle with and solve them.

9. **Review and Recalibration**: Every six months, retake The Passion Test; review Markers and Passion Pages; update Vision Boards; write new 100th Birthday speech segments to reflect evolution.

10. **Openness and Allowing**: Stay open to unexpected paths for fulfillment (“This or something better”); practice “Intention—Attention—No Tension” without forcing specific “hows.”

### 14. Non-Negotiable Execution Rules
- **Never Censor the List**: Do not erase a passion because you can’t see how to achieve it. “Don’t play it safe.”
- **Complete the Full Comparison**: Do not stop the elimination process early; must compare through the entire list to identify all five top passions.
- **Individual Process First**: Do not consult anyone when making the initial list; do not take the test as a couple without first doing individual lists.
- **Six-Month Review**: Must retake The Passion Test every six months without fail, as passions “change and morph over time.”
- **Daily Card Review**: Must read Passion Cards “several times a day” so they become “deeply ingrained.”
- **Choose in Favor**: At every decision point, must choose in favor of passions; this is a “sure-fire rule which is guaranteed to lead you to living a passionate life.”
- **No Tension**: Must not strain or force outcomes; must remain open to “God’s will” or “the perfect organizing power of Nature.”
- **Integrity with Commitments**: Once a commitment is made, keep it unless renegotiated; breaking commitments destroys trust and results.
- **No Negative Attention**: Must not dwell on problems, bills, or lack; use the Rubber Band technique if necessary to break the habit.
- **Transparency**: Must be open and transparent in relationships; hidden agendas violate the methodology.

### 15. Failure Patterns
- **Playing It Safe**: Writing “getting a raise” instead of “being a multimillionaire” or otherwise censoring the list to what seems “possible” rather than “ideal.”
- **Incomplete Process**: Stopping the comparison process early (“Oh, that’s number one, so I don’t need to go through the rest of the list”), which the authors warn “almost always” leads to inaccurate results.
- **Consulting Others**: Allowing a spouse or partner to influence the initial list, resulting in “fuzzy desires” that are not truly yours.
- **Knowing the How First**: Trying to figure out practical steps before completing the “what” of passion listing.
- **Attention on Lack**: Focusing on “all the bills you have to pay,” “how little you have left over,” or “how deeply in debt you are” rather than on the passions; this creates more lack.
- **Breaking Self-Commitments**: Making commitments to oneself and breaking them, leading to low self-trust and low “Passion Scores.”
- **Refusal to Review**: Failing to look at Passion Cards daily or failing to update every six months, allowing life to drift out of alignment.
- **Serial Sabotage**: Giving up after the first setback or “mis-take,” viewing failure as a dead end rather than a detour.
- **Low Trust Tax**: Operating with hidden agendas or distrust in relationships, which imposes a “tax” on speed and increases cost in all endeavors.
- **Self-Focus Over Others**: Focusing on “me, me, me” rather than figuring out what’s important to others (customers, employers, family), which destroys the “value” that creates wealth.

### 16. Decision Logic
- **Primary Decision Rule**: “Every time you’re faced with a decision, choose in favor of your passions.”
- **Binary Alignment Check**: Ask: “Is this going to help me be more aligned with my passions or less aligned?”
- **Bliss Comparison**: When stuck between two items during the Test: “Which would bring me more bliss?”
- **Heart Over Mind**: “Most people find their first impulse is the most accurate. Passion arises from the heart, and your heart’s impulse is more likely to be closer to the truth than your mind’s analysis.”
- **Renegotiation Protocol**: When new circumstances arise (e.g., Janet’s stepmother’s illness), renegotiate commitments with integrity rather than rigidly sticking to plans or abandoning responsibilities.
- **Fear Recognition**: Recognize fear (“Fear is the opposite of faith”) but “don’t take counsel from fear” when ready to move; take small steps if immobilized.
- **Surrender vs. Control**: When obstacles appear, choose to “resign as general manager of the universe” (Debbie Ford) and “stay open to how life is appearing at this moment, free of your concepts of how it ‘should’ be.”
- **Value Assessment**: In business decisions, ask: “What are you willing to give to the marketplace in order to get it?” and “How can I be more externally focused?”

### 17. Self-Review Protocol
- **Passion Score Audit**: Regularly rate each top passion 0-10; if score is low despite perceived attention, examine “where your attention has really been” (e.g., on bills vs. value provided).
- **Intention Alignment Check**: Look at results; “if your results are out of line with your stated intentions, it’s time to do some self-examination” to discover the “real intentions” deep inside.
- **Appreciation Game**: Daily listing of 10 new things you appreciate about yourself to combat low self-esteem and “victim” mentality.
- **Monthly Marker Review**: Check progress on the 3-5 Markers for each passion to verify if the passion is “alive and well” in your life.
- **Six-Month Full Reset**: Retake The Passion Test completely every six months to check for evolution, ensuring the list stays current with “who you are” now.
- **Rubber Band Compliance**: Use the rubber band technique for 30 days to catch and eliminate negative thought habits.
- **Transparency Scan**: Ask: “How can I be more open, more transparent so others can see what I’m really trying to do—my intent, my motive?” (Covey’s framework).

### 18. Definition of Done
The work is complete when:
- **Full Manifestation**: All five top passions are rated 10/10, fully actualized in the life of the practitioner.
- **Marker Achievement**: The specific, observable Markers for each passion have occurred (e.g., “I earn at least one million dollars per year,” “I have taken the pulse of 100,000 people,” “I am traveling first class”).
- **Effortless Flow**: The practitioner is living in a state of “Intention—Attention—No Tension,” where desires show up without force, and “your life is created first in my mind, then in the world.”
- **Destiny Alignment**: The practitioner is living their “personal destiny,” experiencing life as “joyful, delightful, exciting and fulfilling” regardless of outer obstacles.
- **Legacy Realization**: The 100th Birthday speech is no longer a future vision but an accurate description of the life currently being lived—the person is “being the vessel” for their purpose.
- **Universal Openness**: The practitioner demonstrates ability to “stay open to what is appearing now” without insisting on their own concepts of how things should be.
- **High Trust Operation**: In professional contexts, relationships operate at high trust with no “tax” on speed or cost.

### 19. The Amateur-to-Expert Gap
**1. Clarity vs. Censorship**: Amateurs censor their list to what they think is possible; experts write the “big” passions without knowing the “how.”
**2. Process Completion**: Amateurs stop the comparison process early or skip items; experts complete the full elimination process for all five slots.
**3. Attention Discipline**: Amateurs review their passions “when they feel like it”; experts read Passion Cards “several times a day” without fail and use the Rubber Band technique to eliminate negative thoughts.
**4. Decision Speed**: Amateurs delay decisions until they see the “how”; experts choose in favor of passions immediately at every decision point.
**5. Openness to Path**: Amateurs try to control the “how” and are blocked by obstacles; experts “stay open to whatever is appearing now” and see obstacles as blessings.
**6. Frequency of Review**: Amateurs take the test once and forget it; experts retake it every six months religiously as “passions change and morph over time.”
**7. Self-Worth Foundation**: Amateurs operate from low self-esteem and victimhood; experts practice the Appreciation Game daily and take responsibility for results.
**8. Trust Building**: Amateurs operate with hidden agendas and pay a “low trust tax”; experts create transparency and earn “trust dividends” through speed and reduced cost.
**9. Focus Orientation**: Amateurs are self-focused (“me, me, me”); experts are others-focused, seeking to give value first.
**10. Relationship to Failure**: Amateurs view failure as a dead end and proof of unworthiness; experts view failure as a “detour,” “fertilizer,” or “mis-take” (Take 2) and persist.
