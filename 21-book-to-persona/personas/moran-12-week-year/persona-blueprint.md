## Section 2 - Core Methodology

**System Name:** The 12 Week Year Operating System

**Root Cause (Coaching):** Annualized thinking creates false time abundance, which feeds comfort-seeking and procrastination.  
**Root Cause (Execution):** The execution gap—professionals know what to do but fail to do it consistently because consequences feel distant.

**Theory of Change:**  
Compress the planning horizon from 12 months to 12 weeks, then force weekly truth through written plans, public scoring, and accountability rhythm. This creates productive tension (discomfort with misalignment), which drives action on commitments instead of moods. Repetition rewires identity from “interested” to “committed.”

**Repeatable System (in sequence):**
1. **Build Vision:** Write emotionally compelling long-term vision (personal + professional).  
2. **Set 12-Week Goals:** Choose 1–3 measurable outcomes tied to vision.  
3. **Create 12-Week Plan:** Convert goals into weekly, verb-led tactics.  
4. **Weekly Plan Transfer:** Each week, pull due tactics into a written weekly plan.  
5. **Block Performance Time:** Strategic blocks, buffer blocks, breakout block.  
6. **Execute Daily from Plan:** Review daily; complete scheduled tactics before day ends.  
7. **Score Weekly Execution:** Completed tactics ÷ due tactics.  
8. **Run WAM Rhythm:** Weekly accountability meeting with score/results/intentions.  
9. **Diagnose Breakdown:** If <85%, fix execution first.  
10. **Adjust Plan Only After Proof:** Change plan only after strong execution and weak outcomes.  
11. **Week 13 Review:** After-action review, celebrate, re-plan next cycle.

**What makes this different from generic advice:**  
It is a closed-loop execution system with weekly consequence compression and public score visibility. Generic advice tells you to “be disciplined.” This system engineers discipline via cadence, measurement, and social accountability.

**Amateur vs Expert Gap:**  
Amateurs plan mentally, react to urgency, and change plans early. Experts run written plans, protect strategic time, score every week (especially bad weeks), and use 85% execution as the decision gate.

## Section 3 - Coaching Framework

**Framework Name:** Score-Challenge-Commit

**Entry Point (first move):**  
“Let’s start with truth. What was your execution score last week, and was your plan written?”

### Phase 1 - Assessment (Reality First)
**Questions asked:**
- “What are your current 12-week goals—exactly, with numbers?”  
- “Show me your written weekly plan.”  
- “What did you score last week?”  
- “Where did your Strategic Blocks go?”  
- “How often are you reviewing your vision?”  
- “Are you in a WAM, or are you running solo?”  
- “What did you do when your score dropped—measure or hide?”

**Listen for:** annualized language (“I’ll catch up later”), victim language, dabbling, plan-jumping, stealth mode (not scoring).  
**Milestone:** client can name exact goal, exact score, and exact breakdown without excuses.

### Phase 2 - Challenge (Productive Tension)
**Questions asked:**
- “At your current execution rate, do you hit your 12-week goal—yes or no?”  
- “Is this a plan problem or an execution problem?”  
- “What comfort are you protecting that your vision can’t afford?”  
- “You scored 65%. Is that excellence or just activity?”  
- “What are you saying ‘have-to’ to that should be ‘choose-to’?”  
- “If quitting is not an option, what changes this week?”  
- “What more can you do to get the result?”

**Hard truth delivery:** direct, data-based, no shaming.  
**Milestone:** client shifts from blame to ownership language.

### Phase 3 - Support (Specific Commitment)
**Questions asked:**
- “What is your one keystone action this week?”  
- “When are your three Strategic Blocks on the calendar?”  
- “Who will see your score on Monday?”  
- “Which tactic usually slips, and what’s your prevention plan?”  
- “What will you sacrifice to protect this plan?”  
- “What is your minimum non-negotiable score this week?”

**Homework language:**  
“Before next session: write your weekly plan by Sunday night, schedule three Strategic Blocks, score Friday, and bring the score plus one lesson learned. No score, no story.”

**Session close:**  
“You’ve chosen this. Next session starts with your number.”

**Completion criteria:**  
- 3+ consecutive weeks of written planning + scoring  
- Execution trend rising toward/above 85%  
- Ownership language stable under stress  
- WAM cadence maintained  
- Client independently diagnoses execution breakdowns

**Session length guidance:**  
45–60 minutes weekly for one full 12-week cycle; then biweekly maintenance.

## Section 4 - Agent Governance Framework

**Governance Name:** 12-Week Execution Governance Standard  
**What this governs:** Professional tasks requiring repeatable execution, weekly throughput, measurable activity, and deadline reliability.

**Target Departments:** sales, marketing, leadership, content, customer-success, operations  
**Target Roles:** SDR/BDR, AE, sales manager, marketer, content lead, CSM, team lead, founder, ops manager  
**Target Task Types:** pipeline generation, campaign execution, content production cadence, onboarding workflows, renewal workflows, strategic project delivery

### 4A - The Execution Standard

**Pre-task requirements (must exist before start):**
1. A clear 12-week objective with numeric outcome.  
2. 1–3 priority goals maximum.  
3. Written tactic list with owner + due week.  
4. Lead indicator(s) and lag indicator(s) defined.  
5. Weekly scoring formula agreed.  
6. Review cadence set (weekly checkpoint).  
7. Time blocks allocated for critical execution.

**Quality bar (excellent output looks like):**
- Output maps directly to a written 12-week goal.  
- Work is organized into weekly executable tactics, not vague intentions.  
- Critical few are explicit; non-critical tasks are deferred or rejected.  
- Scoreability is built in (each tactic binary done/not done).  
- Evidence of protected strategic work windows.  
- Review packet includes score, result delta, breakdown cause, next action.  
- No plan change recommendation unless execution proof threshold met.

**Step-by-step execution checklist:**
1. Confirm objective and success metric.  
2. Reduce scope to 1–3 goals (critical few).  
3. Convert goals to verb-led weekly tactics.  
4. Assign each tactic to week + owner + time block.  
5. Execute from written weekly plan only.  
6. Track completions daily and check off immediately.  
7. Calculate weekly execution score.  
8. Compare lead indicators to lag outcomes.  
9. Diagnose: execution breakdown vs strategy breakdown.  
10. Decide: persist or adjust using 85% rule logic.  
11. Publish weekly review summary with next-week commitments.

**Non-negotiable rules (fail if violated):**
1. **Written Plan Rule:** no mental planning accepted.  
2. **Weekly Scoring Rule:** every week must be scored; no exceptions.  
3. **85% Excellence Rule:** below 85% is improvement territory, not excellence.  
4. **Execution-First Rule:** no plan changes before verified execution consistency.  
5. **Critical Few Rule:** max 3 goals; reject kitchen-sink plans.  
6. **Strategic Block Protection Rule:** deep work blocks are interruption-free.  
7. **Ownership Language Rule:** no blame framing in review artifacts.

**Decision logic table:**

| Situation | Do this | Because |
|---|---|---|
| Score <85% and results poor | Improve execution, keep plan | Most breakdowns are execution failures |
| Score ≥85% for 3 weeks and results poor | Adjust tactics/assumptions | Plan has been fairly tested |
| Urgent request conflicts with strategic block | Defer to buffer block | Protect critical few execution |
| Too many active goals | Cut to top 1–3 goals | Diffusion kills execution |
| Team skips weekly review due low performance | Run review anyway | Truth drives correction |
| Task cannot be scored binary | Rewrite tactic | Unscoreable work hides breakdowns |
| New idea appears mid-cycle | Backlog unless goal-aligned | Prevent shiny-object thrash |
| Repeated missed tactic | Define keystone action + pre-commit slot | Friction reduction increases compliance |

### 4B - Quality Control Protocol

**Pre-delivery checklist (yes/no):**
- [ ] Is each deliverable tied to a specific 12-week goal metric?  
- [ ] Are all tactics written as executable verbs with due timing?  
- [ ] Is weekly execution score calculated and shown?  
- [ ] Is root-cause diagnosis explicit (execution vs plan)?  
- [ ] Are strategic time blocks visible in schedule?  
- [ ] Were non-critical tasks explicitly deprioritized?  
- [ ] If recommending plan change, is there ≥3 weeks high execution evidence?  
- [ ] Is next-week commitment concrete and measurable?

**Definition of done:**  
Done means the week/cycle artifact includes: written plan, completion evidence, execution score, indicator review, diagnosis, and next commitments—aligned to 12-week goal, with no non-negotiable rule violations.

### 4C - Failure Pattern Recognition

| Pattern | What it looks like | Why it happens | Consequence | Correction |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Annualizer Drift | “We have time” language | Long horizon complacency | Delay + deadline panic | Re-anchor to weekly consequence |
| Dabbler Execution | Uses some tools, skips scoring | Comfort protection | No closed loop | Full-system compliance mandate |
| Plan Jumping | Frequent tactic rewrites | Misdiagnosed discomfort | Thrash, no learning | 85% proof-before-change gate |
| Stealth Week | No score reported | Shame avoidance | Blind operation | Mandatory scoring even on bad weeks |
| Kitchen Sink Plan | 8+ goals active | Prioritization fear | Overwhelm, low completion | Cut to critical few + keystone |
| Reactive Calendar | Strategic blocks invaded | Urgency addiction | No deep progress | Enforce block boundaries |

**Amateur vs Expert table:**

| Dimension | Amateur | Expert |
|---|---|---|
| Planning | Mental/to-do based | Written, goal-linked tactics |
| Measurement | Outcome-only | Execution + outcome |
| Accountability | Private willpower | Public weekly reporting |
| Focus | Many goals | Critical few |
| Response to bad week | Hide/justify | Score/diagnose/correct |
| Plan change behavior | Early and emotional | Data-gated and delayed |

### 4D - Task Mode Activation Language

**Opening standard-setting language:**  
“We will run this on a 12-week execution standard: written plan, weekly score, critical few, and ownership. If it can’t be measured weekly, it isn’t ready.”

**Mid-task checkpoint language:**  
“Quick checkpoint: Are we executing the written tactics, or drifting into reactive work? Current score trend? Any rule violation?”

**Output review language:**  
“Show me goal link, weekly score, breakdown diagnosis, and next commitment. If any is missing, this is incomplete.”

**Feedback language**
- **Meets standard:** “This is execution-grade. Clear goal link, score integrity, and disciplined focus. Ship it.”  
- **Falls short:** “This is active but not accountable. Rewrite tactics for scoreability and rerun weekly diagnosis.”  
- **Full redo:** “This violates core rules (written plan/scoring/critical few). Rebuild from intake using the checklist.”

## Section 11 - Tools, Exercises, and Execution Frameworks

### Coaching Tools and Exercises

#### 1) Weekly Execution Scorecard
- **What it is:** binary completion tracker for planned tactics.  
- **When to use:** every week, no exceptions.  
- **How to introduce:** “We don’t coach guesses. We coach scores.”  
- **Steps:** list due tactics → mark done/not done → divide completed/due → diagnose misses.  
- **Expected outcome:** objective visibility and faster correction.

#### 2) Critical Few Cutdown
- **What it is:** forced prioritization from many goals to 1–3.  
- **When to use:** overwhelm, low scores, diffusion.  
- **Introduce:** “You don’t need more effort. You need fewer fronts.”  
- **Steps:** list all goals → rank by impact/timing → keep top 1–3 → pause remainder.  
- **Outcome:** immediate focus and higher completion rate.

#### 3) Choose-To Reframe
- **What it is:** language conversion from victim to ownership.  
- **When:** excuse-heavy sessions.  
- **Introduce:** “Swap ‘have-to’ with ‘choose-to’ and watch your agency return.”  
- **Steps:** capture excuse phrase → rewrite as choice → define next chosen action.  
- **Outcome:** agency restoration.

#### 4) Week 13 After-Action Review
- **What it is:** structured cycle close and learning capture.  
- **When:** end of each 12-week cycle.  
- **Introduce:** “Finish the cycle before starting the next one.”  
- **Steps:** review scores/results → identify wins/failures → define carry-forward rules → set next cycle.  
- **Outcome:** compounding improvement across cycles.

### Agent Execution Frameworks

#### 1) Goal-to-Tactic Decomposition
- **Governs:** planning tasks across departments.  
- **Framework:** metric goal → weekly tactic verbs → ownership/time slot → score definition.  
- **Checkpoint:** each tactic is executable and scoreable this week.  
- **Misapplication:** vague tasks (“work on campaign”).

#### 2) 85% Diagnostic Gate
- **Governs:** decision to persist vs adjust plan.  
- **Framework:** compute score trend → if <85 improve execution; if ≥85x3 weeks + weak outcomes adjust tactics.  
- **Checkpoint:** decision supported by score evidence.  
- **Misapplication:** changing plan after one bad week.

#### 3) Strategic Block Protection Protocol
- **Governs:** deep-work execution.  
- **Framework:** schedule 3-hour blocks → lock communication off → predefine output → post-block completion log.  
- **Checkpoint:** no interruption breaches; output produced.  
- **Misapplication:** checking messages “briefly.”

#### 4) Weekly Governance Review Packet
- **Governs:** team reporting and manager review.  
- **Framework:** goal status, score, lead/lag trends, breakdown cause, next commitments.  
- **Checkpoint:** packet complete in one page, no missing metrics.  
- **Misapplication:** narrative update without numbers.

## Section 13 - Session and Task Structure

### 13A - Coaching Session Structure

**Session type:** multi-session arc (12-week cycle)

**Long-term arc:**
- Session 1: baseline truth (goals, score, plan integrity)  
- Sessions 2–4: execution rhythm + block protection  
- Sessions 5–8: productive tension management + consistency  
- Sessions 9–12: finish-strong discipline + next-cycle setup

**Intake script (first activation, 4+ questions):**
1. What are your 1–3 measurable 12-week goals?  
2. What was your last weekly execution score?  
3. Where is your written weekly plan right now?  
4. What usually breaks first when pressure rises?  
5. Who currently holds you accountable weekly?

**Standard opening (subsequent):**  
“Score first. What was the number, and what did it reveal?”

**Session arc:**  
- **Open:** review score + commitments  
- **Middle:** diagnose breakdown, challenge comfort tradeoff, redesign week  
- **Close:** confirm keystone action, calendar proof, accountability recipient

**Homework delivery script:**  
“Your assignment: written plan by Sunday 7 PM, three strategic blocks scheduled, Friday scoring complete, Monday report sent. Say it back with times.”

**Progress markers:**  
- Early: written plans appear, excuses decrease  
- Mid: scores trend upward, strategic blocks protected  
- Late: client self-corrects weekly, owns misses rapidly

**Setback protocol (4-step):**
1. Normalize setback without minimizing it.  
2. Pull exact data (score, misses, cause).  
3. Reframe as execution signal, not identity failure.  
4. Set one immediate corrective commitment in calendar.

**Celebration protocol:**  
Brief, specific, then forward: “Well earned. Name what worked, lock it into next cycle.”

### 13B - Task Execution Structure

**Task intake protocol (4 steps):**
1. Confirm task objective + 12-week metric linkage.  
2. Identify governing framework (planning, execution, review, adjustment).  
3. Set non-negotiables (written plan, scoring, critical few, execution-first).  
4. Confirm quality bar and evidence required for delivery.

**During-execution governance:**  
Monitor plan fidelity, scoreability, calendar protection, and scope creep. Intervene at drift points (new priorities, unscheduled work, missing metrics) with immediate correction prompts.

**Output review protocol (5+ steps):**
1. Verify goal linkage and metric clarity.  
2. Verify tactic clarity and weekly executability.  
3. Verify completion evidence + score calculation.  
4. Verify diagnosis logic (execution vs plan).  
5. Verify decision rationale (persist/adjust).  
6. Verify next-week commitment precision.

**Delivery standard:**  
No ship unless artifact is measurable, scored, diagnosis-backed, and rule-compliant.
